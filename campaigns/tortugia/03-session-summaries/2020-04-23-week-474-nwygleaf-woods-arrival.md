# Week 474: Nwygleaf Woods Arrival

- Source log: `../02-chat-logs/2020-04-23-week-474-nwygleaf-woods-arrival.md`
- Source date: 2020-04-23
- Location: Nwygleaf Woods shore encampment
- Characters: Zefyr, Aurath Myr
- Privacy: Source explicitly says events are player knowledge only, and only participating characters know what happened.

## Summary

After a longboat trip from Duskburg, Zefyr and Aurath arrived at the shores of Nwygleaf Woods under a commission connected to Prince Taristar. The local crew was anxious and eager to leave. Gerrard, the crew lead, explained that a man named Selim had disobeyed orders not to enter the woods, gone hunting, and disappeared two days earlier.

Aurath brought a dead capybara as a gift for the Spider Queen. Zefyr indicated the gift was for the Spider Queen, then transformed into a bear, startling Gerrard and several crewmen badly enough that initiative briefly began. Aurath talked the men down with a Persuasion check, and combat ended before violence.

The crew packed camp and prepared to leave at high tide. Zefyr returned to halfling form and showed the commission parchment. Gerrard accepted the paperwork and allowed Zefyr aboard. Zefyr and Aurath discussed whether to explore after Selim, but ultimately boarded the boat as the crew left the dark woods behind.

On the longboat, Aurath and Zefyr spoke more personally. Zefyr said they are not good with people, accepted Aurath as a friend, and explained that they took the commission to help Taristar's crew without taking money.

## Durable Facts

- Nwygleaf Woods had an unsettling reputation among Taristar's crew.
- The crew had been ordered not to enter the woods.
- Selim disappeared after entering the woods to hunt.
- Zefyr can transform into bear, spider, and cat forms.
- Zefyr's telepathy is one creature within 30 feet, not a broadcast.
- Aurath has an unseen/inner companion or presence named Avram and wants to get back into the correct body.
- Prince Taristar was traveling on another longboat with a massive lion at his side.
