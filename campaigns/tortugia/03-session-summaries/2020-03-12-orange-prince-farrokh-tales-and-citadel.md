# Orange Prince Farrokh, Tales, And The Orange Citadel

- Source log: `../02-chat-logs/2020-03-12-orange-prince-farrokh-tales-and-citadel.md`
- Approximate dates in source: 2020-03-12 to 2020-03-25
- Material type: prince lore, realm description, event announcement, Avrae coin transfer

## Summary

The Orange Prince, Farrokh, is described as a shifting, charismatic Kalashtar bard-prince whose appearance changes to suit the person he speaks with. He governs Espera and the docks of Port-Leroy, values knowledge and oral storytelling, and comes from a plane without writing where spells are memorized as stories.

Farrokh's realm is fertile and welcoming, blending nature and civilization. Refugees and people of all races are accepted. His lieutenants are Razafaenut, a powerful archdruid with deep Tortugian history, and Abraz, a young brass dragon who joined Farrokh after Thulwar's defeat.

Farrokh's Orange Citadel is a floating cluster of lush islands maintained by unseen servants. The music played across the islands may double as a coded alarm system that only Farrokh and his trusted lieutenants understand.

Farrokh also hosted a tale-sharing event for newly arrived Rovers. Characters could tell a story from their background, home plane, or imagination, roll Performance or Persuasion, and receive gold based on the result. Atash told a song and story about his king, war, and survival; Farrokh responded gently and paid him, then offered patience when the story became painful.

## Durable Facts

- Farrokh values stories whether or not they are literally true, as long as they are interesting.
- Farrokh's leadership style in this source is generous, patient, and culturally centered on oral memory.
- The Orange Prince encouraged newcomers to remember who they were before Tortugia.
- The tale event used `!misc Orange Prince tale` to track downtime.

## Avrae / Mechanics

- Atash initially rolled Performance 15, then Persuasion 10, with modifiers later clarified to total 24.
- Farrokh paid Atash 34 gp from his Coin Pouch.
- The event instructions allowed +5 for writing a story, optional bard accompaniment for +1d6, and +1d12 help from the prince on natural 1 or 2.
