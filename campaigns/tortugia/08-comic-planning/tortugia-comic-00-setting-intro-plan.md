## Adaptation Approach

Treat Comic 00 as a setting prologue, not a mission adaptation. The dramatic question is: what does it mean to be called by Tortugia before you understand what Tortugia is?

The issue follows an unnamed Rover from first dream to arrival at Port-Leroy, using the dream as a visual spine. The narration introduces the major setting promises: dreams, monoliths, shadowmists, cutters, slivers, Wardens, the Covenant, the Silent Fortress, and the uncertain Council of Seven era. It avoids naming too many historical figures on the page; names can appear in captions only when needed.

The emotional movement is:

1. **The Call** - the Rover is haunted by a dark monolith in repeated dreams.
2. **The Crossing** - the mists become real, and the dream becomes a voyage.
3. **The Kingdom** - Tortugia appears: volcanic, maritime, dangerous, alive.
4. **The Burden** - the Rover learns this freedom has rules, history, and enemies.
5. **The Hook** - the kingdom has no king, the Silent Fortress is closed, and the next story belongs to those who answer the call.

Invented connective tissue: the unnamed Rover and the harbor guide are framing devices. They are not campaign facts unless later adopted. The setting facts come from the imported Tortugia background and source notes.

## Issue Shape

- Format: 6-page introductory comic.
- Point of view: a newly called Rover, with omniscient mythic captions.
- Tone: maritime dark fantasy, wonder under pressure, pirate-west-march energy.
- Visual motif: rainbow color inside otherwise cold shadowmist and black basalt.
- Shareability: suitable for curated public comic planning. Avoid private notes and raw Discord identifiers.

## Page Beats

### Page 1: The Dream Finds You

1. A person sleeps in an ordinary room from an unspecified home world. Moonlight, travel gear, restless hands.
2. Dream-panel: a black triangular monolith with a round opening at its top, white light throbbing through fog.
3. Repeated dream montage: the monolith is closer each time; the sleeper ages by days or weeks through small changes in hair, stubble, candle length, or travel dust.
4. The dreamer reaches out and touches the stone. Rainbow-colored shadowmists whirl around their hand.
5. Final panel: the dreamer wakes, but the mist is still curling around the bedframe.

### Page 2: The Boat in the Mist

1. The room dissolves into a small boat on black water, the Rover seated alone.
2. A pale rainbow appears ahead, cutting through thick fog.
3. The boat passes shadowy shapes beyond the mist: tentacles, teeth, or impossible silhouettes implied but not confirmed.
4. The monolith rises from rocks battered by waves, colossal and silent.
5. The monolith's light pulses. Far away, the rainbow arcs toward a brooding fortress above an island.

### Page 3: First Sight of Tortugia

1. Wide establishing splash: Tortugia beyond the monolith, red volcanic glow along Los Diablos, a sprawling city on the slope, Port-Leroy lights near the harbor, the Silent Fortress above.
2. Cutters emerge from mist, high-sided and enchanted, crewed by Rovers watching the monolith pulse.
3. Below the waterline, sea-devil shapes circle the rocks and ropes.
4. The Rover is helped aboard or guided toward harbor; they are soaked, wide-eyed, and not yet sure whether they have been rescued or claimed.

### Page 4: A Kingdom Made of Oaths

1. Mythic flashback: fire and water clash as black basalt land rises from the sea.
2. Four Warden silhouettes around Tortugia; Ogeron is suggested by calm dream-light, Shelobb by nightmare eyes, Belfegor and Murhgren by opposing Shadowfell silhouettes.
3. Grenel and darkwood roots bind the fire titan Swelish, while the water titan withdraws into the depths.
4. War across the young demi-plane: ships burning, citadels distant, sliver-light dividing factions.
5. Ogeron splits his sliver into seven shards, the panels fragmenting into seven colored reflections.

### Page 5: The Throne Opens, Then Closes

1. The Rainbow Omen crowns the Silent Fortress after 900 years.
2. Princes and Princesses are shown as symbolic colored silhouettes, not detailed portraits, converging on the fortress.
3. Salish and Tian clash with Xarkus and Ashardon in a distant mythic duel, shown as silhouettes and dragon fire rather than literal battle detail.
4. Ogeron's throne room opens in morning light; a Kalashtar figure made of shimmer speaks into every mind.
5. Fast historical montage: Salish's reign ends, The Stone pulls the Staff of Power from The Henge, Artus receives the crown, and then Artus vanishes near Edilos.
6. The Silent Fortress closes again. One small caption: `The King's Sliver vanished with him.`

### Page 6: Welcome, Rover

1. Present day Port-Leroy: crowded docks, off-worlders, Rovers, cutters, longboats, trade, tension, and rain.
2. A harbor guide or veteran Rover tells the newcomer the plain truth: Rovers can cross the mists, but Tortugia always calls them back.
3. A map-like montage of current threats: Grenel's ghouls beyond the eastern wall, Shelobb stirring in Nalfgard and Espera, the Raven Queen's shadow over the mists.
4. The Council of Seven sits in silhouette beneath an empty crown.
5. Final panel: the new Rover looks from the harbor up toward the Silent Fortress. The monolith's white beam glows behind them through mist.
6. Last caption: `Tortugia is waiting for the next name written into its history.`

## Dialogue And Captions

- Keep spoken dialogue sparse. The setting should feel bigger than the speaker.
- The unnamed Rover does not need dialogue until Page 6, and even then one short question is enough.
- Captions should sound like a campaign chronicle, not boxed exposition from a rulebook.

Suggested recurring caption voice:

> `Somewhere between the planes, a kingdom dreams of sailors.`

> `Those who answer are called Rovers.`

> `They are free to cross the shadowmists. They are never free of the call.`

Potential Page 6 dialogue:

- **New Rover:** `Can I go home?`
- **Harbor Guide:** `Aye. If you learn the mists. If you survive the sea. If Tortugia lets you sleep long enough to forget it.`

## Visual Continuity Notes

- Tortugia is a demi-plane with maritime, volcanic, Shadowfell-touched imagery. Do not make it generic tropical pirate fantasy.
- Monoliths are dark stone, oddly triangular, with a round opening at the top and a powerful white beam.
- Shadowmists can carry rainbow color, especially during Rover arrival and monolith visions.
- Cutters are high-sided vessels able to cross the shadowmists; longboats are lower, open, shield-lined coastal vessels.
- Port-Leroy should feel busy and lived-in: trade, wet planks, ropes, lanterns, mixed peoples, sailors, and recent arrivals.
- The Silent Fortress is black basalt, brooding above Cayona, tied to kingship and Ogeron's sliver.
- The historical princes' mechanics are marked as historical in the source. Show them mythically, not as current political UI.

## Character Visual References

- **Unnamed Rover:** Leave species, gender, and class flexible. Design as a visually readable newcomer with travel-worn clothing from another world. Their lack of local context is the audience's entry point.
- **Harbor Guide:** A practical veteran Rover or cutter crew member. Weathered coat, mist-wet hair, lantern or boat hook. Avoid making them an existing named NPC unless the user later chooses one.
- **Ogeron:** Middle-aged Kalashtar, calm and sorrowful, rendered as an illusion or dream-presence rather than a fully physical flashback actor.
- **Wardens:** Use symbolic silhouettes to avoid overdefining canon visuals not fully established in the repo.
