# Tortugia Return Invitation: Single Page Script

## Page Goal

Invite former players back by making Tortugia feel like an old dream turning real again. The page should work for readers who remember the campaign and for readers who only know the broad myth: monoliths call Rovers, Princes scheme, Wardens threaten the demi-plane, and the call has returned.

## Reader Orientation

- **Visible character:** An unnamed former Rover, shown only enough to stand in for any returning player character.
- **Scene context:** Years or months have passed outside Tortugia. The Rover thought the campaign was over, or only a memory.
- **Place:** Starts in the Rover's ordinary room, then becomes a dream montage of Tortugia.
- **Stakes:** The old powers are not gone. The monoliths are calling again.
- **Continuity guardrail:** Use symbolic silhouettes for Princes and Wardens. Do not imply specific current political facts beyond the campaign orientation.

## Layout

- Six panels.
- Panel 2 can be the largest panel, or Panels 2-4 can form a vertical dream column.
- Panel 6 should echo Panel 1, but with the impossible made real.
- Color rhythm: muted real-world blues and grays in Panels 1 and 5, cold storm-black and white monolith light in Panel 2, sharp jewel colors for the Princes in Panel 3, sickly shadow colors for the Wardens in Panel 4, rainbow shadowmist returning in Panel 6.

---

## Panel 1

A quiet night in an ordinary room. The unnamed Rover sleeps badly, half-lit by moonlight. On a nearby table: old travel gear, a folded map, a dry ink bottle, maybe a weapon or instrument not touched in a long time. A faint curl of mist gathers near the floor, barely visible.

**Caption:** `I thought it was only a dream.`

**Art Notes:** Keep the character nonspecific. The invitation works better if many former players can project their own character into the page.

**Prompt:** Restless fantasy adventurer asleep in a quiet moonlit room, old travel gear and folded map on table, faint rainbow-tinted mist near the floor, melancholic dark fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Wide storm panel. A black triangular monolith rises from wave-battered rocks in a violent sea. Its round opening emits a hard white beam through rain and fog. The sea is enormous; the monolith is older than the storm.

**Caption:** `The monolith, standing in the storming sea.`

**Caption:** `Calling me.`

**Art Notes:** This is the first hook. The monolith should be the page's strongest shape.

**Prompt:** Colossal black triangular monolith rising from wave-battered rocks in a storming sea, round opening at top emitting hard white light, rain, fog, rainbow shadowmist, maritime dark fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

A symbolic court of seven jewel-colored silhouettes. Princes and Princesses lean across a fractured map of Tortugia. Hands move tokens, cards, sliver-like shards, contracts, or knives. No face is fully clear. The mood is elegant, playful, dangerous.

**Caption:** `The Princes, wearing masks of every color...`
**Caption:** `...and we are the pieces they move.`

**Art Notes:** Make this mythic and theatrical, not a definitive portrait lineup. The word "Princes" can include historical Princes and Princesses as the campaign did.

**Prompt:** Seven jewel-colored fantasy prince and princess silhouettes around a fractured island map, hands moving tokens, cards, magical shards and hidden knives, elegant political betrayal, symbolic dark fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 4

The page shifts into public Warden imagery rather than fully revealed forms. Shelobb is suggested through a spider-queen silhouette with the poise of a beautiful princess and a crown of spider legs. Murrghen appears through ordered sea-temple symbols: pearls, clean stone, wind-and-wave motifs, Koalinth patrol shapes, and an eight-pointed water vortex. Belfegor appears as a lamia-like chaotic patron surrounded by cafe-table revelry, masks, pipe smoke, music, and a living message-board cactus shape. Below them, tiny ships, walls, forests, and settlements bend under the pressure.

**Caption:** `The Wardens, paused but not gone...`
**Caption:** `...preparing to bind the world.`

**Art Notes:** Keep Shelobb's true quori/nightmare nature private; for public art, use spider queen and princess imagery. Murrghen should feel orderly and oceanic, not demonic. Belfegor should feel alluring, chaotic, and socially dangerous rather than simply monstrous.

**Prompt:** Public Warden symbols looming over a volcanic island demi-plane: spider queen silhouette with princess poise and spider-leg crown, ordered pearl sea-temple icon with eight-pointed water vortex and Koalinth patrol shapes, chaotic lamia patron with cafe revelry, pipe smoke, masks, pan-flute music and living cactus message board, tiny ships and settlements below, ominous but readable fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 5

Back to the ordinary room. The Rover wakes, sitting upright in bed, breathing hard. The room is quiet again. For one beat, nothing supernatural is visible. The old gear on the table is still gathering dust.

**Caption:** `I thought it was only a bad dream.`

**Art Notes:** Let this panel breathe. It is the pause before the invitation.

**Prompt:** Fantasy adventurer waking suddenly in a quiet moonlit room, old travel gear untouched on table, tense silence after a nightmare, restrained comic panel.

---

## Panel 6

Final reveal. The mist is real now. It curls under the door, around the bedframe, over the old gear. In the window or mirror, the monolith's white beam glows beyond storm and sea. The Rover's hand reaches toward the gear, or toward the mist, not fully committed yet.

**Caption:** `But tonight, the monoliths call me again.`

**Optional small footer caption:** `Tortugia is waiting.`

**Art Notes:** This is the invitation beat. Do not show the Rover already leaving; leave the choice open for the players.

**Prompt:** Rainbow shadowmist filling a moonlit bedroom, old adventuring gear touched by mist, storm-sea monolith reflected in window or mirror with white beam glowing, adventurer reaching toward gear, invitation to adventure, dark fantasy comic final panel.

## Lettering Notes

- Keep all text as captions, not speech balloons.
- Use cream-gray caption boxes with thin near-black outlines, matching the existing Tortugia comic lettering style.
- Let Panel 2 and Panel 6 have the most visual space; avoid covering the monolith beam.
- The six caption beats can be used exactly as the tentative script, with only punctuation and casing cleaned.

## Single No-Text Art Prompt

Create a single vertical comic page, 2:3 aspect ratio, six-panel layout, no text, no captions, no speech bubbles, no SFX, no placeholder text boxes. Stylized limited-palette dark fantasy comic art with strong ink shapes, readable silhouettes, flat textured color blocks, black and off-white plus storm blue, cold gray, jewel rainbow accents, and sickly shadow green-purple only for supernatural threats.

Panel 1: a restless fantasy adventurer asleep in a quiet moonlit room with old travel gear and a folded map on a table, faint rainbow-tinted mist near the floor. Panel 2: a colossal black triangular monolith rises from wave-battered rocks in a storming sea, round opening at the top emitting a hard white beam through rain and fog. Panel 3: seven jewel-colored prince and princess silhouettes around a fractured island map, hands moving tokens, cards, magical shards, contracts, and hidden knives, elegant betrayal. Panel 4: four enormous symbolic Warden silhouettes loom over a volcanic island demi-plane, nightmare eyes, calm dream-light, Shadowfell darkness, necromantic shadow, tiny ships and settlements below. Panel 5: the adventurer wakes suddenly in the same quiet room, old gear untouched, tense silence. Panel 6: rainbow shadowmist fills the bedroom and curls around the old gear; in the window or mirror, the storm-sea monolith glows; the adventurer reaches toward the gear, leaving the choice open.

Make Panel 2 and Panel 6 visually dominant. Leave natural negative space for later caption boxes, especially in skies, walls, floor shadows, and empty sea. The page should feel like an old campaign calling its players home.
