# Tortugia Comic 00: Page 4 Script

## Page Goal

Compress Tortugia's origin myth into visual symbols: elemental creation, Forebears, Wardens, war, Ogeron's sacrifice, and the seven shards.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- The page can be more abstract than Pages 1-3.

---

## Panel 1

Fire and water collide in cosmic scale. Black basalt land rises from the sea. Volcanic peaks punch upward through storm and steam.

**Caption:** `The kingdom was made where planes met.`

**Caption:** `Prime. Shadow. Fire. Water. Negative dark.`

**Art Notes:** This is mythic, not geological realism.

**Prompt:** Mythic creation of a demi-plane, black basalt land rising between elemental fire and water, volcanoes erupting through storm and steam, epic fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Three Forebear impressions: Swelish as a vast fire-titan shape; Ixilthetocal as a colossal presence under the sea; Grenel as a beautiful earth-spirit mask with necromantic darkness behind it.

**Caption:** `Forebears rose first from the fabric of Tortugia.`

**Art Notes:** Grenel's deceptive presentation matters: earth-spirit front, sinister truth suggested.

**Prompt:** Three mythic Forebear figures in fantasy comic art, fire titan, water titan beneath dark sea, masked earth-spirit necromancer with hidden darkness, symbolic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Four Warden silhouettes stand around the young land. Ogeron is lit by calm dream-light. Shelobb is nightmare eyes and angular shadow. Belfegor and Murhgren are contrasting Shadowfell silhouettes, one severe and one chaotic.

**Caption:** `Then came the Wardens, each an answer to a different hunger.`

**Art Notes:** Do not overdefine canon visuals. Silhouettes are safer and stronger.

**Prompt:** Four symbolic Warden silhouettes around a young volcanic island, calm dream-lit Kalashtar figure, nightmare eyes, two contrasting Shadowfell shadow figures, mythic fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 4

War-torn Tortugia. Ships burn. Small armies clash on black shores. Distant citadels and sliver-light divide the horizon. Grenel's shadow gathers behind the casualties.

**Caption:** `Their factions built, sailed, raided, ruled, and bled.`

**Art Notes:** Keep violence broad and historical, not gore-focused.

**Prompt:** War-torn volcanic island kingdom, burning ships, small armies clashing on black shore, distant magical citadels, colored sliver lights, necromantic shadow behind battlefield, fantasy chronicle comic panel.

---

## Panel 5

Ogeron holds a dark sliver at his chest as his form dissolves into dream-light. The sliver breaks into seven colored shards that arc outward like a shattered rainbow.

**Caption:** `Ogeron chose sacrifice.`

**Caption:** `One sliver became seven shards. One age of war became a Covenant.`

**Art Notes:** This is the emotional center of the origin page.

**Prompt:** Kalashtar Warden dissolving into dream-light while a dark magical sliver breaks into seven colored shards like a shattered rainbow, solemn mythic fantasy comic panel.

## Lettering Notes

- Captions can be denser here, but each panel should still read visually first.
- Avoid explaining every named entity in balloon text. Let the plan's context carry the detail.

## Continuity Notes

- Ogeron sacrificed himself to create the Covenant and split his sliver into seven shards.
- Grenel's true nature as a negative-energy Scion should be suggested, not over-explained.
