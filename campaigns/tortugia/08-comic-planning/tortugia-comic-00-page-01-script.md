# Tortugia Comic 00: Page 1 Script

## Page Goal

Make the Rover's call feel intimate before it becomes epic. The reader should understand that Tortugia does not begin with a quest board. It begins with a dream that keeps coming closer.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 2 is the first strong reveal.
- Panel 5 should be quiet and unsettling.

---

## Panel 1

A modest room from an unspecified home world. A traveler sleeps badly under thin moonlight. Their boots, pack, and a half-read book or map sit nearby. One hand grips the blanket as if they are already holding onto something else.

**Caption:** `Before the sea, before the crown, before the wars that would learn your name...`

**Art Notes:** Keep the room culturally nonspecific. The Rover can be any new player character. This is a threshold, not a backstory.

**Prompt:** Moonlit fantasy bedroom, travel-worn adventurer sleeping restlessly, boots and pack beside bed, faint mist beginning to curl near the floor, cinematic dark fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Dream image. A dark stone monolith shaped like an odd triangle stands alone in fog. At its top, a round opening emits a throbbing white light. The rest of the page color drops colder; only the white beam and a faint rainbow sheen in the mist break the darkness.

**Caption:** `...there is a stone that dreams of you.`

**Art Notes:** The monolith must read as ancient and alien, not a normal obelisk.

**Prompt:** Black triangular stone monolith in dense fog, round opening at top emitting throbbing white light, faint rainbow shadowmist, ancient alien maritime fantasy, comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Montage strip divided into three narrow beats inside one panel: the dreamer sees the monolith far away, then closer, then closer again. The dreamer changes subtly between beats: candle burned lower, travel cloak moved, stubble or hair changed, new scratch on the hand. Time is passing.

**Caption:** `For some, the dream takes months.`

**Caption:** `For others, days.`

**Art Notes:** Show repetition without confusing the reader. The monolith is always the same shape; distance is what changes.

**Prompt:** Three-beat comic montage of a dream monolith coming closer through fog over repeated nights, sleeping traveler changing subtly between dreams, rainbow mist, dark fantasy.

---

## Panel 4

The dreamer stands before the monolith in the dream. Their hand touches the cold black stone. Rainbow-colored shadowmists burst outward from the contact point, wrapping their wrist and spilling toward the panel border.

**Caption:** `Eventually, every Rover touches it.`

**Art Notes:** This is the conversion moment. The panel should be beautiful and frightening at the same time.

**Prompt:** Fantasy traveler touching a colossal black monolith, rainbow-colored shadowmist bursting around their hand, white beam above, awe and dread, cinematic comic panel.

---

## Panel 5

Back in the room. The traveler wakes, eyes open. They are still in bed, but rainbow mist curls around the bedframe and spills under the door like a tide. The ordinary room is no longer fully ordinary.

**Caption:** `Then the mist remembers where you sleep.`

**No dialogue.**

**Art Notes:** Do not show a monster. The threat is the impossible presence of the mist.

**Prompt:** Adventurer waking in moonlit room as rainbow shadowmist curls around bedframe and under door, quiet supernatural dread, fantasy comic panel.

## Lettering Notes

- Captions should be restrained, parchment or dark translucent boxes.
- Do not overuse exclamation or horror lettering. The page is eerie, not loud.

## Continuity Notes

- The first line establishes a mythic narrator. It does not assert this specific Rover becomes historically important.
- The dream sequence follows the Rover source: repeated dreams, monolith, touch, rainbow mists.
