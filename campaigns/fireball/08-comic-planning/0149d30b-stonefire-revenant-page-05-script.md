# Stonefire and the Revenant — Page 5 Script

## Page Goal

The second exchange ends worse. Stonefire adapts his approach, lands hits, but this time the Revenant doesn't go all the way down — and then it crits. Stonefire ends the page on the floor. This is the lowest point.

## Layout

- Four panels.
- Panels 1 and 2 are medium action panels.
- Panel 3 is the splash: the crit.
- Panel 4 is a narrow strip — aftermath on the floor.

---

## Panel 1

Medium action panel. Stonefire attacking again — but differently. This strike is more angular, targeting a joint or a weaker point in the Revenant's form rather than pure center-mass. He has adapted. His expression is concentrated.

The Revenant is reacting — but this time it doesn't simply take the hit. Its arm is raised in something that might be a guard, or might be a reach.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** The contrast from page 4 panel 1 is important: that hit was massive and direct, this one is precise and calculated. Show the difference in Stonefire's body language — page 4 was power, page 5 panel 1 is technique.

**Prompt:** A goliath monk in a dungeon corridor making a precise angular strike on a revenant, targeting a specific point rather than full force, the revenant's arm partially raised, calculated combat, fantasy horror comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Medium panel. The Revenant has taken the strike but not gone down. It is staggered — but it catches Stonefire's second blow mid-swing. Its hand closes around Stonefire's wrist.

This is the first time in the fight the Revenant has physically stopped him.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** The wrist-catch is the moment the fight's logic shifts. Stonefire's first hit this page worked; the second didn't. The Revenant adjusting, catching him — this is it beginning to respond rather than just absorb. Stonefire's expression is focused, not panicked, but his body language tightens.

**Prompt:** A revenant catching a goliath monk's wrist mid-strike in a dungeon corridor, stopping the blow, the monk's other hand still free, physical grapple moment, dread atmosphere, fantasy horror comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Splash panel. The crit.

The Revenant uses Stonefire's caught wrist as a pivot — and delivers a single massive strike with its free arm. The impact is enormous: Stonefire is driven backward, boots leaving parallel skid-marks on the stone floor, body involuntarily angled from the force. Dust and stone chips from the floor.

The Revenant is in the foreground, arm extended after the blow. Stonefire is in the middle of a stumble at the center of the panel, fighting to stay upright. In the background, the corridor stretches away — and where Stonefire was standing, there are skid-marks.

**SFX:** `KRAKH`

Heavier than the previous `KRAK` — the extra letter signals the difference in scale.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This should feel worse than the hit that sent the Revenant into the wall on page 4. That hit used physics; this hit uses the Revenant's strength, which should be read as supernatural. The visual comparison (Revenant into wall vs Stonefire into a stumble-slide) shows the power gap. Stonefire does not go unconscious — he is upright, just barely. But he has been hit in a way that very few things have ever hit him.

**Prompt:** A revenant delivering a massive strike on a goliath monk in a dungeon corridor, the monk driven sliding backward across stone floor leaving boot skid-marks, force and scale overwhelming, heavy impact SFX "KRAKH", fantasy horror comic splash panel, power imbalance visible.

---

## Panel 4

Narrow horizontal strip at the bottom of the page. Stonefire has gone down to hands and knees. Not unconscious — his eyes are open, his head up — but he cannot rise yet. One hand presses the stone floor. Blood on the back of his knuckles from where the force redistributed. The corridor stretches ahead of him, the Revenant's feet visible at the far edge of the panel.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** Do not let Stonefire look unconscious. He is present, aware, and trying. The reader needs to see him trying. The Revenant's feet at the panel edge signal it is still there, still coming, and now there is no urgency in its approach. It has time.

**Prompt:** A goliath monk on hands and knees on dungeon stone floor, head up and aware but unable to rise, blood on knuckles, the shadowed feet of a revenant visible at the panel edge, narrow strip panel, quiet and heavy, fantasy horror comic.

---

## Lettering Notes

- No dialogue or captions this page.
- The `KRAKH` on Panel 3 uses the same letterform as the `KRAK` on page 4 Panel 1, but slightly larger and with a heavier weight. The one added letter signals the escalation without requiring a completely different style.
- The silence across this page is intentional — the fight has moved past words.

## Continuity Notes

- Stonefire is not unconscious and should not be drawn as unconscious. He is functionally unable to continue fighting for the moment, but he is aware.
- The Revenant does not gloat or speak after the crit on Panel 3. It waits. Its speech will come on page 6 when it stands over him.
- The skid-marks on Panel 3 should remain visible on Panel 4 as background detail — the physical evidence of what just happened.
