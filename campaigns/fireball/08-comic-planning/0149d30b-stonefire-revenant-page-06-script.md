# Stonefire and the Revenant — Page 6 Script

## Page Goal

The Revenant claims its victory before it has finished. It stands over Stonefire and begins the speech we saw interrupted on page 1. We hold this moment longer than is comfortable. Then the speech is cut.

This page should be slow. Every panel should feel like it takes too long to turn to the next.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panels 1 and 2 are close-up panels — Stonefire's perspective looking up.
- Panel 3 is a close-up — Stonefire's hand, still trying.
- Panel 4 is the large statement panel — the Revenant's speech begins.
- Panel 5 is a narrow final strip — the speech is cut.

---

## Panel 1

Low-angle close-up. Stonefire's point of view from the floor: looking up at the dungeon ceiling. Stone and shadow. A torch sconce at the edge of the frame. He is flat, or nearly flat — this is the angle of someone who has been brought to the ground.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This is disorientation. The ceiling is what you see when you're on the floor. This angle has never appeared in the issue before; it is new because Stonefire has never been in this position before. It should feel wrong — the reverse of the low-angle shots of the Revenant looming that come later.

**Prompt:** Low-angle view looking up from dungeon floor at a stone and shadow ceiling, torch sconce at the edge of the frame, slightly disorienting, the angle of someone who has been brought down, fantasy horror comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Wide panel, still low angle but now including the Revenant as it comes into frame — it is walking toward Stonefire, unhurried. It fills the upper portion of the panel, looming. The torch sconces on either side cast its shadow in two directions simultaneously, which should read as wrong.

Stonefire is at the bottom of the panel — we are still near his perspective, but elevated just enough to see both him and the approaching figure.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** The two shadows are the visual detail that elevates this beyond a standard looming shot. Two light sources, two shadows — it should feel slightly supernatural, slightly more wrong than the previous panels. The Revenant is moving at the same deliberate pace it has used throughout. No hurry.

**Prompt:** Wide dungeon corridor panel from low perspective, a humanoid revenant walking slowly toward a downed goliath monk, the revenant lit by two torches casting two simultaneous shadows in different directions, atmospheric wrongness, fantasy horror comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Close-up. Stonefire's hand pressing the stone floor. His fingers are splayed, pushing, trying to rise. The knuckles are white with effort. He is getting no traction — the hand trembles with the attempt.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This panel is entirely about trying. Not giving up — trying. The hand should be the emotional center of the page. It connects to the wrapped hands that were established as his whole equipment on page 2; now the same hands are fighting just to push him upright. The trembling detail is important: he is using everything he has and it is not enough yet.

**Prompt:** Close-up of a goliath monk's wrapped hand pressing a dungeon stone floor, splayed fingers, white knuckles, trembling with effort trying to rise, no magic, no weapons, just the hand and the stone, fantasy horror comic close-up.

---

## Panel 4

Large panel. The Revenant stands directly over Stonefire, claw raised. This is the image from page 1, but now we see it from a different angle — not the near-horizontal splash of page 1, but a steeper angle that gives us both Stonefire's face turned upward and the Revenant's full towering stance.

Stonefire is looking up at the Revenant. His expression is not fear — it is the controlled face of someone in extreme pain and extreme danger who refuses to show either.

The Revenant's speech balloon begins. It is larger than any previous balloon. It takes up significant vertical space in the panel, the black balloon and pale Zalgo text clearly readable:

**Revenant:** `YoUr SoUl WiLl Be DeLiCiOuS, aNd YoUr BoDy ShAlL bE UsEd ApPr-`

**Art Notes:** The speech balloon should be as large as it has ever been — this is the loudest the Revenant has been in the issue. The balloon body can break the panel border at the top. The text is identical to page 1 panel 2: verbatim. The reader who has been paying attention realizes this is the same moment. We are back where we started.

The Revenant's claw is raised. It is about to bring it down.

**Prompt:** A humanoid revenant standing over a downed goliath monk in a dungeon corridor, claw raised, the monk looking up with controlled expression, massive corrupted Zalgo-text speech balloon in black with pale lettering starting at the top of the panel: "YoUr SoUl WiLl Be DeLiCiOuS, aNd YoUr BoDy ShAlL bE UsEd ApPr-", fantasy horror comic, dread atmosphere.

---

## Panel 5

Narrow horizontal strip at the bottom of the page. The speech balloon from Panel 4 is cut off by this panel's left edge — just the last visible character is `A` from `ApPr-`, sticking out from the panel border between panels 4 and 5.

In Panel 5 itself: the corridor from the far end. We are now behind the Revenant, looking down the corridor toward where Stonefire is — the reverse angle from all previous corridor panels. At the far end of the corridor, barely visible: light. Warm orange-white. It is not from a torch.

The light is coming from the other direction. From the corridor Stonefire came from.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This strip is the setup for the fireball. The reversed angle (now looking toward Stonefire from behind the Revenant) is a POV we haven't had before. The reader sees the same corridor from the opposite end. The warm light appearing at the far end is the last panel before the detonation on page 7. It should take the reader a beat to understand what they're seeing.

**Prompt:** Narrow dungeon corridor strip panel, reverse angle from behind a revenant looking down toward a downed goliath monk in the distance, at the far opposite end of the corridor a warm orange-white glow beginning to fill the frame, not from a torch, the source off-panel, fantasy horror comic, the beat before impact.

---

## Lettering Notes

- The Revenant's speech on Panel 4 is verbatim from both the session log and page 1 panel 2. The text must be identical: `YoUr SoUl WiLl Be DeLiCiOuS, aNd YoUr BoDy ShAlL bE UsEd ApPr-`. The balloon cuts off with the dash mid-word.
- The balloon in Panel 4 should be the largest in the issue so far. It physically crowds the panel.
- The cut-off `A` leaking between panels 4 and 5 is a lettering decision: the balloon from panel 4 is technically still present in the gutter, just barely. This requires close coordination between artist and letterer.
- Panel 5 has no text of any kind.

## Continuity Notes

- Panel 4 is the same moment as page 1 panel 2 — the reader should recognize it. The different angle is intentional: page 1 showed it horizontally, page 6 shows it from above.
- The warm light in panel 5 is Lloyd's fireball approaching through the corridor. He is not visible — only the light precedes him.
- Stonefire does not see the light in panel 5; his view from the floor faces upward at the Revenant. Only the reader (and the Revenant's back) is positioned to see the approaching corridor.
