# Noe and Ivy — Page 4 Script

## Page Goal

Recovery and reestablishment. Both characters get up, the fight resumes — but it's different now. Ivy performs her signature move: the snake-sway charm. Noe is not charmed, but he watches. He admits he watches. The page's emotional center is his *"yeah.. I do, keep going if you will"* — a line that reveals more than either of them intends.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is a wide strip across the top: recovery.
- Panel 2 is medium, left of center: Ivy back in motion.
- Panel 3 is wide, carrying the main banter exchange.
- Panel 4 is medium: the looking.
- Panel 5 is narrow closing strip.

---

## Panel 1

Wide strip across the top. Both characters getting to their feet. They are in a process of standing — not dramatic, not heroic, just the mechanical work of getting up after being on the floor. The sand around them shows evidence of the fight so far: scorch marks from fire bolts, the dissolved area where the cloud of daggers was, his dropped sword still lying where it fell. They are about equidistant from each other, roughly ten feet apart. Neither is rushing.

**No dialogue.**

**Art Notes:** This is a breathing-room panel. The pace slows for a moment. The scorch marks and the dropped sword are continuity details; the artist should make them present without emphasizing them. Both characters look physically roughed up but functional — not desperate.

**Prompt:** Two characters getting to their feet in a desert at night, grey-skinned bard and snake sorceress both standing from the sand, scorch marks on the desert floor from fire magic, a longsword lying abandoned on the sand, equidistant from each other, recovery moment, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Medium panel, left of center. Ivy is back in her combat posture — but now her combat posture and her performance posture are the same thing. She begins to sway. The movement starts at her hips and travels upward. Her arms come up slowly. She is casting Charm Person again, but the casting IS the dance — there's no mechanical gesture separate from the movement.

**Ivy:** *"You like the way I shake?"*

Second balloon, smaller, lower:

**Ivy:** *"Even though I'm a snake?"*

**Art Notes:** The two lines should feel like a couplet — she is delivering a call-and-response structure, and the second line answers the first. Her scales should be visible here, catching whatever light is available. She is absolutely aware of exactly what she is doing and doing it with full commitment.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress in a swaying dance-cast pose, arms rising from her sides, hips turned, scales catching moonlight, casting a charm spell through movement, desert night, confident and performative, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Wide panel. Two-shot. Noe is watching her. He is not charmed — the save succeeded — but he is watching. Not looking away. His expression is specific: he is genuinely watching someone do something he finds compelling, and he is aware that he is watching, and he hasn't decided what to do about that yet.

**Noe:** *"yeah.. I do"*

A beat — a small gap between balloons:

**Noe:** *"keep going if you will"*

**Art Notes:** His expression is the most complex moment in the issue so far. Not charmed. Not unmoved. Something between appreciation and something more careful — the expression of someone who is choosing to look rather than being compelled to. His sword is still on the sand behind him; his hands are empty. He is not fighting right now.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard watching a snake sorceress dance mid-combat, his expression specific — not charmed, but genuinely watching and aware of it, hands empty, his longsword still on the sand behind him, desert night, two-shot panel, quiet tension, fantasy comic.

---

## Panel 4

Medium panel. Ivy mid-movement — she has registered his response. Her charm attempt failed (the mechanical save), but his response was not a refusal. Her expression shifts: the performance is still there, but there's a curiosity behind it now. She looks at him sideways, mid-sway, processing.

**No dialogue.**

**Art Notes:** This is the second crack in Ivy's performance register (the first was Panel 5 of Page 3). She was expecting either charmed or resistant. She got something else. Her expression should show that the something else is more interesting to her than the expected outcomes.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress mid-dance movement looking sideways at a grey-skinned bard, her expression mid-performance but with curiosity visible behind the confidence, recalculating, desert night, medium comic panel.

---

## Panel 5

Narrow closing strip. They resume combat — she throws a scorching ray, he deflects or steps away, both in motion. But the register has shifted from the opening pages. The fight is still happening; the conversation is what's underneath it now.

A missed ray leaves a scorch mark in the sand between them.

**No dialogue.**

**Art Notes:** The movement should feel like a resumption rather than an escalation. They are picking up where they left off, but the audience knows something has shifted. The gap in the sand between them — the miss — is the visual expression of the moment.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress releasing a scorching ray that misses a grey-skinned bard who moves aside, the fire bolt hitting the sand between them and leaving a scorch mark, both in motion, desert night, the fight resuming, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Lettering Notes

- Ivy's *"You like the way I shake? / Even though I'm a snake?"* is a two-balloon couplet. The second balloon should be slightly smaller, slightly lower, positioned as a continuation not a new thought.
- Noe's *"yeah.. I do / keep going if you will"* is also a two-balloon delivery. The pause between them matters — the ellipsis in *"yeah.. I do"* is a beat.
- No captions on this page.

## Continuity Notes

- The dropped sword stays on the sand. Do not retrieve it in this page.
- The charm attempt fails mechanically — visually, Ivy's magic doesn't reach him, but the reader should understand that his watching her is his own choice, not a result of the spell. These are two different things happening in the same moment.
- Noe's line *"keep going if you will"* is the most revealing thing he says in the issue. He is giving her permission he wasn't asked to give. This should land as a character beat, not a throwaway.
