# Stonefire and the Revenant — Page 2 Script

## Page Goal

Establish Stonefire as a lone descender, competent and unhurried. Introduce the labyrinth geography. Plant the cloaked figure at the edge of a panel so the reader can find it on a reread but won't catch it the first time. End with the corridor that contains the Revenant — but don't show the Revenant yet.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is a wide establishing exterior shot.
- Panels 2 and 3 are medium interior panels stacked vertically.
- Panel 4 is a narrow slice — the almost-invisible follower.
- Panel 5 is a wide bottom panel of the target corridor.

---

## Panel 1

Wide exterior panel. The entrance to a dungeon or ancient ruin — stone archway, heavy ivy, torch burning in a bracket just inside the mouth. It is overcast outside, the sky a cold pale gray. There is no one visible in the frame, but a single set of large footprints in damp earth leads toward the entrance.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** The footprints imply Stonefire has already gone in; we are just catching up to him. The entrance should feel old and purposeful, not randomly discovered — like something he came here specifically to find.

**Prompt:** Ancient stone dungeon entrance with ivy-covered arch and torch just inside, overcast gray sky, large footprints in damp earth leading to the entrance, no characters visible, fantasy dungeon exterior, comic establishing panel.

---

## Panel 2

Interior, medium panel. Stonefire descends a long stone staircase. He fills about a third of the panel — his goliath frame enormous in the narrow stairwell but completely at ease with it. One hand trails the wall for the geometry of it, not for balance. He has done this before.

The light below is faint and orange. Above, from the archway, daylight is already receding.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** Stonefire's posture should read as totally unhurried. He is not sneaking. He is not afraid. The staircase is narrower than is comfortable for someone his size, but it doesn't slow him. This is the confidence that makes the later horror land.

**Prompt:** A large gray-skinned goliath monk in simple cloth and wrapped hands descending a narrow torch-lit stone staircase in a dungeon, one hand trailing the wall, completely at ease, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Interior, medium panel. A labyrinth junction — three identical stone corridors branching from a central point. Each has the same torch sconces, the same worn flagstone floor, the same darkness at the end. They are indistinguishable.

Stonefire stands at the center. He has paused. He is not consulting a map. He is listening — head very slightly tilted. After a beat, he steps toward the left corridor.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** The identical corridors are the maze establishing shot — the reader should feel the disorientation. Stonefire choosing by instinct (or something else) rather than logic adds to the atmosphere. He should look certain, not guessing.

**Prompt:** A goliath monk at a dungeon junction with three identical torch-lit stone corridors, standing still and listening, choosing one, underground labyrinth, fantasy comic panel, top-down-ish perspective showing all three branches.

---

## Panel 4

Narrow vertical slice — almost a margin panel. The same junction, but now from a different angle: one of the corridors Stonefire did not choose. At the very far end of it, barely visible in the shadow, a cloaked figure stands. Hood up. Perfectly still. It is watching the junction Stonefire just left.

No light falls on the figure. It is there only as a shape.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This panel should be easy to miss. It is not labeled. The cloaked figure occupies perhaps 10% of the panel's area, at the far end of the corridor, in deep shadow. The reader will likely not notice this on first read. On second read, after page 8, they will remember it. Keep the figure's cloak featureless — no color, no defining details, just a shape.

**Prompt:** A barely-visible cloaked figure in deep dungeon shadow at the end of a stone corridor, standing still, watching, no light on the figure, tiny and distant, easy to miss, atmospheric horror comic panel.

---

## Panel 5

Wide bottom panel. Stonefire is at the entrance of a long corridor — the one that will contain the Revenant. He is a silhouette from behind, looking into it. The corridor is long, torchlit along the sides, but the far end is dark in a way the other darknesses have not been.

The darkness at the end is slightly wrong. A reader paying close attention might notice it is darker than the torchlight mathematics should allow. But no figure is visible.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This is the last panel before the encounter begins. The wrong darkness at the far end is the only hint. Do not show any part of the Revenant here — not a silhouette, not a shape. The absence is what matters. Stonefire's silhouette looking into it should be the image the reader carries onto page 3.

**Prompt:** A goliath monk silhouette from behind, standing at the entrance of a long torch-lit dungeon corridor, the far end impossibly dark, stone walls and sconces, atmospheric fantasy horror comic, wide panel.

---

## Lettering Notes

- No dialogue or captions on this page.
- Silence is intentional. Page 2 is entirely visual narration.
- If a chapter title or issue title is used, it appears over Panel 1 as a small corner text — minimal.

## Continuity Notes

- The cloaked figure on Panel 4 is Lloyd. He is following Stonefire but not with him. This is never explicitly confirmed in this issue.
- The wrong darkness at the end of Panel 5 is the Revenant's presence, but it is not yet visible.
- Stonefire carries no weapons. His wrapped hands are his equipment. This should be readable in Panels 2 and 5.
- The labyrinth junction on Panel 3 does not need to be precisely mappable — it is atmosphere, not architecture.
