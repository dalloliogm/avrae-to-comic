# Stonefire and the Revenant — Page 1 Script

## Page Goal

Drop the reader into the worst moment of the fight without context. They need to understand: someone is down, something terrible is speaking over them, and then fire arrives from nowhere. The `Earlier.` cut starts the rewind. Do not explain who anyone is.

## Layout

- Four panels.
- Panel 1 is a narrow establishing strip.
- Panel 2 is a large horizontal splash taking roughly half the page.
- Panel 3 is a stark narrow panel.
- Panel 4 is a small quiet caption panel.

---

## Panel 1

Narrow horizontal establishing strip. A stone dungeon corridor, ancient, lit by wall-mounted torch sconces. The architecture is maze-like — arched ceiling, worn flagstone floor, identical doorways in shadow on either side. No characters visible yet. Just the corridor.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** Establish the space without people. The corridor should feel like it has been here longer than memory. Warm torch-orange against deep shadow.

**Prompt:** Narrow establishing panel, ancient stone dungeon corridor, arched ceiling, torch sconces casting orange light, deep shadow at both ends, no characters, fantasy horror comic art.

---

## Panel 2

Large horizontal splash, half-page. The same corridor, now in the middle of the scene we are rewinding to.

Stonefire is on one knee on the stone floor, one hand barely keeping him upright. He is a goliath — enormous, gray-skin, monk's cloth, wrapped hands — but right now he looks small. Blood on the stone. His other arm is raised, trembling, in a futile guard.

The Revenant stands over him. It is tall and wrong — once-humanoid, skin pulled too tight, movement too deliberate for a body that should not be standing. Its claw is raised. Its speech balloon is black with pale lettering and it bleeds outside the panel border into the page gutter:

**Revenant:** `YoUr SoUl WiLl Be DeLiCiOuS, aNd YoUr BoDy ShAlL bE UsEd ApPr-`

The balloon is cut off by the right edge of the panel. It does not finish.

**Art Notes:** The truncated speech balloon is the entire point of this panel. The reader doesn't know what interrupted it yet. Stonefire must look genuinely unable to continue — not heroically defiant, actually spent. The Revenant should look like it has all the time in the world.

**Prompt:** A revenant looms over a downed goliath monk in a torch-lit dungeon corridor, claw raised, corrupted Zalgo-text speech balloon in black with pale lettering bleeding past the panel border, cut off at the edge, fantasy horror comic splash panel, dread atmosphere.

---

## Panel 3

Narrow vertical panel. Complete white. No texture, no scene. At the horizontal center of the panel, a single sound effect word:

**SFX:** `FWOOM`

In orange-red lettering, the letters themselves shaped slightly like fire — not excessive, just suggestive.

**No other dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** The whiteness is the fireball before the eye adjusts. This panel should feel like a flash.

**Prompt:** Abstract single-color panel, pure white, single large orange-red sound effect word "FWOOM" centered, minimalist, flash of light effect, comic art.

---

## Panel 4

Small quiet panel at the bottom of the page. Nearly abstract — warm ember-orange light drifting upward in a dark corridor, residual heat and ash. No characters visible. Just the aftermath of fire.

Two caption boxes, spaced apart:

**Caption:** `Stone. Fire. Ash.`

**Caption:** `Earlier.`

**Art Notes:** The `Earlier.` should feel like a chapter break. The warmth of the embers is the last thing the reader sees before the story rewinds to cold dungeon stone. Let the captions breathe.

**Prompt:** Abstract dungeon corridor aftermath panel, ember-orange light drifting upward in darkness, residual fire glow on stone walls, no characters, quiet and still, fantasy comic art.

---

## Lettering Notes

- The Revenant's balloon on Panel 2 uses black balloon body, pale off-white lettering, slightly jagged balloon edge. The text is verbatim from the session log in Zalgo alt-caps. Do not normalize the capitalization.
- The balloon is deliberately cut off by the panel border — the letterer should leave the word `ApPr-` with a dash and let the border eat the rest.
- The `FWOOM` sound effect in Panel 3 uses warm red-orange lettering only. No other decoration.
- Captions in Panel 4 are restrained rectangular boxes, dark stone-gray or near-black.

## Continuity Notes

- Stonefire's exact injuries are not specified in this issue; what matters is that he cannot rise.
- The Revenant's form is left somewhat indeterminate — the corrupted speech is its signature, not its visual design.
- Lloyd is not visible on this page. His fireball is the cause of the white panel, but the reader doesn't know that yet.
- The `Earlier.` cut connects directly to Page 2's dungeon descent.
