# Noe and Ivy — Page 6 Script

## Page Goal

The poison kiss and the fall. This is the page where Ivy wins and the fight ends. The kiss is both a weapon and something else. Noe's line about his first kiss is the most important line in the issue. His final *"I am at your mercy now"* has to land as both funny and true.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is medium: Ivy's reaction to Page 5's closing line.
- Panel 2 is wide: the approach.
- Panel 3 is the largest panel, roughly one-third of the page: the kiss.
- Panel 4 is narrow: the aftermath on Noe.
- Panel 5 is a closing strip: the final exchange and his fall.

---

## Panel 1

Medium panel. Ivy has heard Noe's closing line from Page 5 (*"doing so actually feels like I'm degrading you in a way. I don't like that thought."*). She is looking at him. Her expression is the most unguarded it has been — not charmed, not performing, just responding. Something landed. She processes it for exactly one beat.

Then her expression shifts back to performance mode — but just slightly off from the earlier version. She has registered the line. She is going to do something with it.

**No dialogue on this panel.**

**Art Notes:** The expression transition is the work of this panel. We go from: genuine, unguarded reception (the line landed) → return to performance mode (but different). The difference should be visible in the quality of the performance — less pure artifice than before. Her performance now has something real behind it.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress in a medium close-up, her expression shifting from a brief unguarded reception of something said to a return to her performance mode — but visibly changed, something behind the performance now, desert night, character moment, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Wide panel. Ivy advancing toward Noe — not running, walking. The walk is slow and deliberate. The combat distance between them is closing. She has stopped throwing spells. Both hands are at her sides. She is looking at him the whole time. Her movement still has the sway quality, but slower — it reads as approach rather than dance.

**Ivy:** *"Hey sugar."*

Second balloon:

**Ivy:** *"How about we stop with the flame wars, and I just give you a little kiss"*

**Art Notes:** The approach should feel distinctly different from her earlier combat movement — this is not an attack stance, it is a walk. The intentional slowness, the lowered hands, the sustained eye contact — this is what genuine attention looks like from her. The combat register has shifted.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress walking slowly and deliberately toward a grey-skinned bard, both hands at her sides, holding sustained eye contact, her movement swaying but the sway slower and more deliberate than earlier, desert night, the approach, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Large panel — roughly one-third of the page. The kiss. She is close to him now, and she kisses him. His expression in the instant before is caught between off-guard and not-moving-away — he could dodge. He doesn't. She kisses him, and from the contact point, purple-green gas begins to expand: poison spray, filling the air between them and beginning to enter his lungs.

**No dialogue during the kiss itself.**

**Art Notes:** The poison should not overwhelm the kiss visually — the kiss is primary, the gas is secondary. The gas is present and growing but the main image is two people close together in a desert night, her leaning in, him accepting it. The purple-green of the poison should be distinct from the orange fire of earlier spells — colder, subtler, pervasive. His expression during: genuinely surprised, not fighting it.

**Prompt:** A snake sorceress kissing a grey-skinned bard, close-up on both faces, purple-green poison gas beginning to emanate from the kiss and spread, her eyes open and watching, his expression surprised but not pulling away, desert night, intimate scale, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 4

Narrow strip. Noe coughing. The gas has filled his lungs. He has stepped back — or staggered — and is bent slightly, one hand over his mouth, coughing in double-asterisk coughs that communicate both the physical reality and the slightly undignified quality of it.

**Noe:** *"I.. don't think I \*cough* like my first kiss that much.."*

The coughs are indicated by asterisks within the dialogue — the line is interrupted by them.

**Art Notes:** The line needs to be funny and not funny at the same time. He is genuinely saying this was his first kiss. He is coughing through the statement. He is dying a little. His expression — what we can see of it through the coughing — should have the quality of someone updating their priors on a new experience.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard bent over coughing, hand over mouth, purple-green poison gas still dissipating around him, his expression simultaneously dismayed and updating a new experience, desert night, fantasy comedy-tragedy panel.

---

## Panel 5

Closing strip. Noe on his knees now, unable to rise. The coughing has not stopped. He looks up at Ivy, who is standing over him — not looming, not threatening, just there. His hands are on the sand. His sword is long gone. He is looking up at her with clear eyes and a calm expression — not defeated, just accurately assessing.

**Noe:** *"I am at your mercy now"*

**Art Notes:** This line should not be played as dramatic defeat. He is stating a fact, calmly, with the same equanimity he has had throughout the issue. He is on his knees in the desert, coughing through poison, looking up at a snake sorceress, and his affect is measured. This is who he is.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard on his knees in desert sand, hands planted, looking up at a snake sorceress standing over him (not menacingly — simply present), his expression calm and clear, poison gas still fading, desert night, quiet conclusion of the fight, fantasy comic strip panel.

---

## Lettering Notes

- Ivy's *"Hey sugar."* is a separate balloon from *"How about we stop with the flame wars, and I just give you a little kiss"* — the pause between them is intentional.
- Noe's coughing line includes asterisk-coughs mid-sentence: *"I.. don't think I \*cough* like my first kiss that much.."* — the asterisks format is from the session log and should be preserved in the lettering.
- Noe's closing *"I am at your mercy now"* is quiet. Small balloon. Not an exclamation.
- No captions on this page.

## Continuity Notes

- This is Noe's first kiss. His line about it is verbatim from the session log and should be taken at face value.
- The page ends with him on his knees but conscious — he is not knocked out until the scorching rays on Page 7.
- Ivy's Panel 1 expression shift is a continuity beat for Page 7: something has registered in her that will show in how she treats him after he falls.
- The poison gas is purple-green throughout — distinct from all fire effects.
