# Noe and Ivy — Page 3 Script

## Page Goal

The pivot page. Noe breaks the register of the fight entirely by hugging Ivy. This is the hug. The cloud of daggers makes it complicated. He smells like he has been dead. He is aware of this. The page ends with both of them on the ground and him out of options.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is narrow across the top — a moment before.
- Panel 2 is the largest panel, roughly one-third of the page: the hug setup.
- Panel 3 is medium: the hug and the cloud.
- Panel 4 is narrow: aftermath.
- Panel 5 is narrow closing strip: on the ground.

---

## Panel 1

Narrow strip across the top. Noe in mid-combat motion — he's moving toward Ivy, sword raised for a strike. His expression is calculating, ready. This is him fighting normally.

Then he stops. The sword lowers.

The stopping is the panel: he has stopped mid-attack. Something has changed.

**No dialogue.**

**Art Notes:** This panel is the moment of decision before we see it executed. The sword should be raised at the start of its swing and then — stopped. His body language shifts from aggressive to something else. We don't know what yet.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard mid-sword-swing stopping in place, the sword lowering, his posture shifting from combat-ready to something more deliberate, desert night, fantasy comic panel, quiet beat before action.

---

## Panel 2

Large panel. Noe has dropped the sword. It is on the sand beside him, just lying there. He is standing with both arms open — genuinely outstretched, the way you hold your arms when you are about to hug someone. He is walking toward Ivy. His expression is the most unguarded it has been: serious, decided. A man doing something he has chosen.

**Noe:** *"since I still have my free will, I will do what I want before it's gone."*

**Art Notes:** The dropped sword on the sand is important. He didn't sheath it, he dropped it — this is not a tactical decision, it's a statement. His arms should be genuinely open, not threatening. His grey skin in the moonlight, the long hair, the missing ear barely visible now as his hair has shifted — this is the closest thing to a vulnerable image of Noe in the issue.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard with both arms open walking toward a snake sorceress mid-combat, his longsword dropped and lying on desert sand behind him, his expression open and decided, her expression uncertain and off-guard, desert night, fantasy romance-comedy comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Medium panel. The hug. He has his arms around her — fully, both arms, her arms caught at her sides. She is inside the embrace, expression moving from surprise toward something she hasn't named. This is an actual hug, not a grab. It is committed.

The cloud of daggers hangs in the air just over and to the right — visible in the background, blue-white spinning blades. He is walking them both slowly but deliberately toward it.

Narrative caption (Noe's voice — can be styled as his speech but without a balloon, as a narration box):

**Caption:** *"she could smell that his body is.. not quite fresh"*

**Art Notes:** This is the page's centerpiece image. The comedy and the sincerity have to coexist: the hug is real and warm, the cloud of daggers is present in the frame and the implication is clear, and his body smells like a reborn corpse. All three facts are true simultaneously. Do not play any of them for pure comedy or pure romance — let them exist together. His expression over her shoulder: eyes closed, content.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned reborn bard hugging a snake sorceress with both arms in a full embrace in a desert night, she is surprised with her arms partially at her sides, behind them a hovering cube of blue-white spinning daggers, his expression peaceful with eyes closed, fantasy romance-comedy comic panel.

---

## Panel 4

Narrow strip. The cloud of daggers envelops both of them — the daggers spin through the hug, both characters flinch simultaneously, visible damage. He's still holding her but barely — he is clearly thinking. He is actively choosing to stop before she is hurt worse.

**No dialogue.**

**Art Notes:** The flinch should be simultaneous and symmetrical. They both get hurt. This is the comedy of the bit: the romantic gesture is also causing physical harm to both parties, and he is the one who did it. His expression is still holding the embrace but now there's a *calculating* quality — he's timing when to let go.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard and snake sorceress both flinching within a cloud of spinning blue-white daggers, still in an embrace, both taking visible damage, simultaneous pain expressions, desert night, fantasy comic action panel.

---

## Panel 5

Narrow closing strip. Both of them on the sand. The cloud of daggers has dissolved — he dropped concentration. He is face-down, arms out, breathing hard. She is beside him, slightly more upright, looking at him. Neither is badly hurt but both are clearly spent. The dropped sword is visible a few feet away.

**No dialogue.**

**Art Notes:** The visual joke of the page's conclusion: all of that, and they're both on the floor. Neither won. His expression, if we can see it, is tired but not regretful. She is looking at him with an expression that is genuinely uncertain for the first time in the issue.

**Prompt:** Two characters lying on desert sand at night, grey-skinned bard face-down arms out, snake sorceress partially sitting up beside him looking at him, a dissolved cloud of daggers now absent from the frame, a longsword lying abandoned nearby, exhaustion, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Lettering Notes

- Noe's big line (*"since I still have my free will, I will do what I want before it's gone"*) should be his most carefully lettered line in the issue — it should feel like a declaration, not a quip.
- The caption (*"she could smell that his body is.. not quite fresh"*) is Noe's narration — dry, slightly amused about himself. Narration box styling: simple, clean.
- No other dialogue on this page after Panel 2. The rest of the page is physical and silent.

## Continuity Notes

- The dropped sword stays on the sand until the end of the fight sequence. He picks it up off-panel; by Page 7 it is gone. Do not have him retrieve it on-panel.
- This is the first time Noe's missing ear may be fully visible — as his hair shifts during the hug or fall, the left ear (or right — pick one and stay consistent) is missing. Not foregrounded, just present.
- The cloud of daggers appears here and does not return. It was a single-cast concentration spell that he dropped.
- Ivy's first moment of genuine uncertainty is the closing panel. This is the first crack in her performance register.
