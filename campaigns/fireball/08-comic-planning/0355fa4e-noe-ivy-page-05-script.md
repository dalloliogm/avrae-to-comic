# Noe and Ivy — Page 5 Script

## Page Goal

Escalation to its peak — burning hands hits. Noe on fire, startled, genuinely caught off guard because he was watching instead of fighting. Then a moment of unexpected sincerity: he heals himself by reciting a poem, and admits he stopped watching because watching felt wrong in a way he doesn't want to carry.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is wide: the setup.
- Panel 2 is medium: impact — Noe on fire.
- Panel 3 is narrow: the exchange.
- Panel 4 is wide: the healing poem.
- Panel 5 is medium-narrow closing: the admission.

---

## Panel 1

Wide panel. Ivy's expression has shifted from calculating to delighted — she has noticed something. She spreads her hands wide, palms forward, and releases burning hands: a full cone of orange fire erupting from her open palms and washing toward Noe, who is still standing approximately where he was during the watching moment. He was not ready for this.

**Ivy:** *"Oh, a huge fan I see—"*

Smaller second balloon, triumphant:

**Ivy:** *"—how about I heat things up?"*

**Art Notes:** This is her biggest attack in the issue so far. The burning hands cone should be bright and wide — this is not a precise attack, it's a flood. She is delighted by this. Her expression is the most openly pleased she has been; she caught him watching and she punished it immediately.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress releasing burning hands, a wide cone of brilliant orange-red fire from her open palms toward a grey-skinned bard caught flat-footed, her expression triumphant and delighted, desert night, the fire lighting the scene warmly, fantasy comic action panel.

---

## Panel 2

Medium panel. Noe, on fire. Not catastrophically — a graze, but visible. His clothing is alight on one side. His expression is pure, un-performed shock: *"GAH!"* He has both arms out, ineffectively trying to address the fire situation.

**Noe:** *"GAH!"*

**Art Notes:** This is a slapstick panel. His expression should be maximum startled — eyes wide, mouth open. The fire on his clothing is visible but manageable. He is not in a cool action pose; he is flailing slightly.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard caught in burning hands fire, one side of his clothing alight, his expression pure wide-eyed shock, arms out in a startled flail, not a heroic pose, desert night, fire casting warm orange light on him, fantasy comedy comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Narrow panel. An exchange — two speech balloons close together. He has patted out the fire on his clothing (the motion visible, not the result). She is watching him with a smile. The delivery is casual and quick.

**Noe:** *"Y-YEAH THAT'S.. PRETTY HOT ALRIGHT"*

In all-caps — he is exclaiming this while patting himself.

**Ivy:** *"hot? Why thank you!"*

**Art Notes:** The exchange is the comic payoff of the panel 1-2 setup. Her response treats his observation as a compliment about herself. His capitalized exclamation should be larger text than usual — he is yelling this. Her response is in smaller casual text, delivered lightly.

**Prompt:** Two-shot of grey-skinned bard patting fire off his clothing while exclaiming, and snake sorceress standing nearby in a pleased pose receiving a compliment she interpreted for herself, desert night, comedy exchange body language, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 4

Wide panel. Noe has stopped flailing. He is standing with his hand pressed to his own chest — the healing gesture. His head is slightly bowed. When he heals, the words come out as a spoken verse: light radiates gently from his chest, and small fragments of text or musical notation drift around him — the visible residue of the spoken spell.

**Noe (spell-casting voice — italic):** *"With the mercy within, and the strength to go on, please let this healing word save this fate's pawn.."*

**Art Notes:** This is the third time we see his spell-casting voice in verse — it should be consistently typographically distinct from his ordinary speech. The healing visual is soft and warm, not bright. Small text-fragments drifting rather than musical notation here — he is reciting, not singing. His expression is focused, eyes nearly closed.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard with hand pressed to chest casting Healing Word, a soft warm glow radiating from his chest, small fragments of text drifting in the air around him, eyes nearly closed in concentration, the verbal spell rendered as drifting light, desert night, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 5

Medium-narrow panel. Noe is healed — standing upright, better. But his expression has changed. He's looking at Ivy, but not with the watching quality from Page 4. He looks like someone who has thought something through and arrived at a conclusion.

**Noe:** *"okay, I'll stop watching your moves now.."*

Beat balloon or continued balloon:

**Noe:** *"doing so actually feels like I'm degrading you in a way. I don't like that thought."*

**Art Notes:** This line should land as genuine. He is not performing sincerity — this is the most unperformed moment in his arc. He was watching her like she was a spectacle. He decided that wasn't right. He is telling her. This is a small, clear moment of character — take it at face value.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard looking at a snake sorceress with a changed expression — more serious, more direct than the watching look from the previous page — giving her a straightforward look while speaking, desert night, quiet character beat between them, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Lettering Notes

- Noe's *"GAH!"* on Panel 2 is large all-caps — slapstick scale.
- His *"Y-YEAH THAT'S.. PRETTY HOT ALRIGHT"* on Panel 3 is also all-caps, mid-exclamation size.
- Ivy's *"hot? Why thank you!"* is lowercase casual — small balloon, delivered lightly.
- The healing verse on Panel 4 is in italic (or distinct spell-casting font) — the same treatment as Dissonant Whispers on Page 1.
- Noe's Panel 5 line is his normal voice. It should be readable as sincere and slightly quiet — smaller balloon, not reduced, just measured.

## Continuity Notes

- Noe's clothing sustains visible fire damage from this page forward — a scorched side should be visible on Pages 6 and 7.
- The *"I don't like that thought"* line is the complement to *"keep going if you will"* on Page 4. Taken together: he watched, he liked it, he decided the watching was something he didn't want to carry. This is character movement.
- Ivy's reaction to Panel 5's line is not shown on this page — it's held for the start of Page 6.
