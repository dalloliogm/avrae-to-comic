# Stonefire and the Revenant — Page 4 Script

## Page Goal

The first exchange. Stonefire hits hard, hits true, the Revenant goes down — and then it gets back up. The regeneration is the turn of this page. It should feel wrong and unfair when the wounds close.

## Layout

- Four panels.
- Panel 1 is a high-impact action panel.
- Panel 2 is a short beat panel — aftermath silence.
- Panel 3 is a check-in panel — Stonefire's moment of confidence.
- Panel 4 is a large splash — the regeneration.

---

## Panel 1

Action panel. Stonefire connects. This is the full impact: both wrapped fists, full goliath mass behind the strike, landing on the Revenant. The Revenant is driven backward — feet leaving the floor, body angled from the impact, the corridor wall approaching fast behind it.

The force should be immediately readable as devastating. Stonefire is a monk — the strike is precise and total, not wild. The Revenant's body bends with physics.

**SFX:** `KRAK`

The sound effect should feel bone-and-stone — not a comic-bright POW, something heavier. Placed at the impact point.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** The point of this panel is that the strike is real. Stonefire isn't missing, isn't being deflected — he hits the Revenant as hard as he can and the hit lands. This matters because the horror is the regeneration, not some failure of strength. Show the power.

**Prompt:** A goliath monk delivering a devastating two-fisted strike on a humanoid revenant in a stone dungeon corridor, the revenant driven back and off its feet from the impact, full body momentum, heavy impact SFX "KRAK", fantasy horror comic action panel.

---

## Panel 2

Short beat panel. The Revenant crumpled at the base of the corridor wall. Stonefire a few paces back, in stance, breathing. The corridor is still. Dust settling from where the revenant hit the wall.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** Silence. The reader should feel — just for a moment — that it might be over. Don't play this up; just let it sit. The stillness before the wrongness.

**Prompt:** A revenant slumped at the base of a stone dungeon wall, crumpled from impact, dust settling, a goliath monk standing several feet away in fighting stance, both still, dungeon torch light, quiet beat panel, fantasy horror comic.

---

## Panel 3

Close-up on Stonefire's hands. He is looking at them — or rather, looking at the result of the strike across the corridor. His wrapped hands are open slightly after the closed-fist impact, returning to ready position. His expression is not triumphant; he is checking — assessing whether it is finished.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This panel is Stonefire being professional. He doesn't celebrate. He checks. The checking is what makes the next panel worse — because he thinks he has a reasonable expectation that it's over.

**Prompt:** Close-up of a goliath monk's wrapped hands relaxing from a strike, in the background slightly out of focus a crumpled figure at a dungeon wall, assessing expression, fantasy comic close-up detail panel.

---

## Panel 4

Large splash. The Revenant is standing up. Not lurching, not struggling — it is simply returning to vertical, with the same deliberate wrongness it moved with before. Its wounds are visible closing: tears in the skin knitting shut, something underneath the surface pulling itself back into alignment. The process looks wrong because it is wrong.

The Revenant's head turns toward Stonefire. Its speech balloon is larger than the one on page 3 — it takes up more of the panel, it bleeds a little further outside the border than before:

**Revenant:** `YoU cAnNoT kIlL wHaT iS aLrEaDy DeAd.`

**Art Notes:** The regeneration should not look like healing magic — it should look like something mechanical and horrible, the body refusing to stay broken because something is forcing it not to. The speech balloon being larger than before is important: as the fight progresses, the Revenant's text invades more space. It is getting louder or more present, even if the volume of the words is the same. Stonefire's reaction is visible in this panel — he hasn't moved, but his expression has changed slightly. Something just shifted.

**Prompt:** A humanoid revenant standing up in a stone dungeon corridor, wounds visibly closing as it rises, looking toward a goliath monk, corrupted Zalgo-text speech balloon in black with pale lettering bleeding past the panel edge: "YoU cAnNoT kIlL wHaT iS aLrEaDy DeAd", the regeneration process visible and wrong, fantasy horror comic large panel.

---

## Lettering Notes

- The Revenant's balloon on Panel 4 is visibly larger than the one on page 3. Both the balloon body and the text should be slightly bigger. The bleed past the panel border is more pronounced — perhaps the first letter and half the second letter of the final word extending outside the border.
- The `KRAK` sound effect on Panel 1 uses a heavy, angular letterform. Not cartoonish — grounded.
- No captions or Stonefire dialogue this page.

## Continuity Notes

- The regeneration is the first time in the story the rules have changed. Everything before this — the descent, the encounter, the fight — has followed predictable logic. This is the moment predictable logic ends.
- Stonefire's change of expression in Panel 4 is not fear. It is the recognition that this requires a different solution, and he does not yet know what that solution is.
- The Revenant's wounds closing are partially visible: skin-level, not internal organs. The horror is implied, not gruesome.
