# Noe and Ivy — Page 8 Script

## Page Goal

The oasis. The coda. He wakes up. He starts a fire. He's roasting something. She's still there. Neither speaks. End.

This page resolves the two competing endings from the session log: Noe imagined winning and finding Ivy sitting beside him at a campfire. Ivy actually knocked him out and carried him to the oasis and waited for him to wake. The comic gives them both their ending, on Ivy's terms. He wakes up at the oasis she chose. He makes the fire he imagined. She's there for both.

## Layout

- Four panels.
- Panel 1 is wide across the top: the oasis at night.
- Panel 2 is medium: he wakes.
- Panel 3 is wide: the fire.
- Panel 4 is a half-page final panel: both of them by the fire.

**No dialogue anywhere on this page.**

---

## Panel 1

Wide establishing panel across the top. An oasis at night. Dark water, still, reflecting the stars. One or two palm trees at the water's edge. The sand is pale in the moonlight. Ivy is sitting at the edge of the water — not in a combat stance, not dancing, just sitting. Her feet may be near the water. Noe is lying nearby, unconscious, on his back on the sand. She has arranged him — he is not face-down, not in the position he fell. She placed him there. The scene is quiet. Stars in the water and in the sky.

**Art Notes:** This is the first interior-feeling environment of the issue, even though it's exterior. The fight was in open desert — vast and exposed. The oasis is enclosed by the water and the trees. It should feel like a different kind of space. The palette shifts here: the orange-red of fire spells is entirely absent. This is cool blue-dark water, silver moonlight, pale sand.

**Prompt:** Wide establishing panel, a small desert oasis at night, still water reflecting stars, one or two palm trees at the water's edge, a snake sorceress sitting quietly at the water's edge, an unconscious grey-skinned figure lying on the sand nearby, cool moonlight palette, quiet and still, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Medium panel. Noe, coming back. His eyes opening — not all the way, just a crack. He is lying on his back. The stars are overhead. He is looking upward. One hand moves slightly, testing the ground under it — he feels sand, not stone, not the fight-floor. His expression is the slow coming-in of awareness.

**Art Notes:** This should be a quiet panel. He is not startled awake; he surfaces slowly. His expression is not fear or confusion — it is someone who has been unconscious and is re-registering the world. The scorch marks on his clothing from Page 5 are still present; continuity.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard lying on his back on pale desert sand, eyes opening slowly, looking upward at stars, one hand moving slightly to feel the ground, returning to consciousness, quiet and unhurried, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Wide panel. Noe has moved — he is now sitting up, slightly away from where he was laid. He has found fuel for a fire (desert scrub, driftwood from the oasis edge — something plausible in this environment) and started a small campfire. He is tending it: crouched slightly forward, adding to it, the fire beginning to build. He is roasting something small over it — a piece of something on a stick. He is focused on the fire. He has not looked toward Ivy yet.

The small campfire is the only warm light source on the page. It catches his grey skin, his scorched clothing, the side of his face. His long hair has fallen slightly back — the missing ear is visible in this light, if the artist chooses; it is not emphasized.

**Art Notes:** This is the fulfillment of his imagined ending from the session log, which he narrated during the fight before he lost. He imagined: *"beside her is the adventuring bard, sitting by the campfire and roasting something."* That is exactly what he is doing. He imagined winning and this outcome — he didn't win, but here he is.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard tending a small campfire by an oasis at night, crouched forward adding fuel, roasting something small on a stick, the firelight catching his grey skin and scorched clothing, focused on the fire, not yet looking up, fantasy comic panel, warm firelight against dark desert night.

---

## Panel 4

Half-page final panel. Both of them. This is the last image of the issue.

Noe is sitting by the fire, tending it, the roasting stick in his hand. Ivy is sitting nearby — not at the fire's edge, a small distance, but close. She is watching the fire. Her posture has the stillness of Page 7, Panel 2 — she is not performing. She is not moving. She is simply there, watching the fire he started at the oasis she chose.

Neither is looking at the other.

Neither is speaking.

The stars are reflected in the oasis water behind them.

**Art Notes:** The final image is two people sitting beside a fire by an oasis at night, not talking, not looking at each other, both present. The distance between them is not cold — it is the natural distance of two people who have been through something and are processing it. She is watching the fire. He is tending it. The water reflects the stars.

This should feel complete. Not happily-ever-after — complete. Both of them are here. That's the whole ending.

**Prompt:** Final panel, snake sorceress and grey-skinned bard sitting near a small campfire by a desert oasis at night, neither looking at each other, both watching the fire or the water, the oasis behind them reflecting stars in still water, quiet and complete, warm firelight, no dialogue, fantasy comic final panel.

## Continuity Notes

- The campfire Noe starts is the exact outcome he narrated during the fight — *"beside her is the adventuring bard, sitting by the campfire and roasting something."* He imagined this scenario before he lost. The fact that it happened anyway, on Ivy's terms and at Ivy's oasis, is the resolution of the two competing ending narratives in the session log.
- Ivy is still present. She waited. The issue does not explain why. It does not need to.
- Noe's scorched clothing from Page 5 is visible — continuity.
- The missing ear, if shown, is incidental — visible when his hair falls back in the firelight, not foregrounded.
- His sword is not present. It was left in the desert. This is fine.
