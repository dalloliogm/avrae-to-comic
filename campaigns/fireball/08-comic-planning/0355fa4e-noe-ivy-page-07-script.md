# Noe and Ivy — Page 7 Script

## Page Goal

He falls. She picks him up. The entire page has no dialogue. The page's work is done by Ivy stopping — the first time she is genuinely still since Page 1. That stillness, relative to everything before, is the emotional pivot of the issue.

## Layout

- Four panels.
- Panel 1 is wide: the fall.
- Panel 2 is the largest panel — roughly half the page: the beat. Ivy standing still.
- Panel 3 is medium: she picks him up.
- Panel 4 is a narrow closing strip: she walks. He is in her arms.

**No dialogue anywhere on this page.**

---

## Panel 1

Wide panel. From medium distance: Noe, on his knees, takes two final scorching ray hits — the bolts arrive from off-panel, implied to be from Ivy, and they connect. He goes down. Not dramatically — he tips forward and catches himself on both hands, then slowly goes flat. Face down on the sand. His arms go out to his sides. He is not dead, just done. The desert is quiet around him.

**Art Notes:** The fall should be quiet, not explosive. No action lines, no impact burst — just a person becoming horizontal on the ground. The scorching ray bolts can be seen leaving their scorch mark on the sand beside him. He chose not to dodge.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard falling forward slowly from kneeling to face-down on desert sand, two scorching ray scorch marks in the sand beside him, the fall quiet rather than explosive, desert night, stillness descending, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Half-page. The emotional center of the issue.

Ivy standing. She is not dancing. She is not in a combat stance. She is not swaying. She is simply standing in the open desert at night, looking at Noe where he lies face-down on the sand. Her arms are at her sides. Her posture is completely still.

The desert is around her. Stars overhead. The scorch marks from the fight are visible in the sand. Noe is in the frame, small, face-down.

She is looking at him.

**Art Notes:** The art instruction here is about what is absent. This is the first panel in the issue where Ivy is not moving. Every other panel featuring her has had some quality of motion — the sway, the dance, the cast, the walk. Here: nothing. She is standing and looking. The stillness is the content. Her expression should not be triumphant. It should be thinking.

The visual contrast between this panel and all her earlier appearances is the entire point. The artist should know that this stillness is intentional, that it is the pivot, and that it should be held. Give it space.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress standing completely still in an open desert at night, no dance, no sway, no combat stance — simply standing, arms at sides, looking at a grey-skinned figure lying face-down on the sand in the near distance, stars overhead, the quiet desert around her, thinking expression, emotional pivot, fantasy comic panel, stillness as the subject.

---

## Panel 3

Medium panel. Ivy crouches beside Noe, gets her arms under him, and lifts. She is not struggling — she has the strength for this — but she is being careful. It is a careful lift, not a drag. His arms hang. His face is slack. She adjusts her hold.

**Art Notes:** The care in the lift is the detail that matters. She is not throwing him over her shoulder. She is picking him up the way you pick up someone you don't want to damage further. This should be legible in the angle of her arms, the adjustment of her hold. Her expression is still not named — focused on the physical task.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress carefully lifting an unconscious grey-skinned bard from the desert sand, crouching beside him, her arms under him, a careful and deliberate lift not a drag, desert night, the action of carrying someone with consideration, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 4

Narrow closing strip. Ivy walking. She is carrying Noe — he is in her arms, face angled away, hair fallen. She is moving through the desert at night. The oasis is not visible yet — she is walking toward it, out of frame. Her movement has returned, but it is different from the earlier dance-walk: this is purposeful, quiet, carrying a weight she chose to carry.

**Art Notes:** This panel is forward-motion after the stillness of Panel 2 — the pivot is complete, she is moving again, but for a different reason than before. No fire. No performance. Just a figure walking through the desert night with someone in her arms.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress walking through open desert at night carrying an unconscious grey-skinned bard in her arms, her movement purposeful and quiet rather than performative, the desert night vast around them, moving toward somewhere not yet visible, fantasy comic closing strip panel.

---

## Lettering Notes

- No dialogue anywhere on this page.
- No captions.
- No sound effects.
- The page is silent.

## Continuity Notes

- This is the first time Ivy is still in the issue. The artist should know this is structural — every earlier appearance has included motion. This stillness in Panel 2 is intentional and should be held.
- The careful quality of the lift distinguishes this from a victor disposing of an opponent. This distinction is visual only — the art must carry it.
- The oasis destination is established on Page 8. On this page we only see desert.
- Noe's sword is not present in any panel on this page — it was abandoned on Page 3 and is not retrieved.
