## Adaptation Approach

Treat this as a tight horror-rescue short. The dramatic question is: what does it feel like to fight something that cannot be killed? Stonefire is alone, physically dominant, and it is not enough. Every time the Revenant goes down, it gets back up.

The story has three emotional movements:
1. **Confidence** — Stonefire is a goliath monk. This corridor should not be a problem.
2. **Dread** — the Revenant regenerates. The fight becomes existential.
3. **Release** — salvation arrives from outside the frame. Lloyd has been watching.

Invented connective tissue is minimal. The dungeon corridor, the labyrinth, and the cloaked figure are all present in the session log. The Zalgo text is used verbatim — no rewriting needed, it functions as panel typography directly.

The relationship between Stonefire and Lloyd is deliberately left unresolved. Lloyd's silver eyes are the final image. We don't explain who he is. The story ends before the audience can ask.

## Issue Shape

- Format: 8-page short comic.
- Point of view: Stonefire, until Page 7 where Lloyd takes over.
- Tone: dungeon horror, physical dread, wordless rescue.
- Visual motif: the Revenant's corrupted speech as invasive lettering that bleeds outside its balloon; Lloyd as negative space until the reveal.

## Cold Open Logic

Page 1 opens at the climax: the Revenant is mid-word, claw raised over a downed Stonefire. Then white. Then fire.

This is the inverse of the normal cold open structure — the interruption IS the hook. The reader spends pages 2-6 dreading the moment they already saw, but still doesn't know if Stonefire survives. The reveal of Lloyd on page 8 is the emotional release.

## Page Beats

### Page 1: The Moment Before

1. Establishing panel: a stone dungeon corridor, ancient, lit only by torch sconces. Doorways in shadow. The labyrinth aesthetic — the sense of maze.
2. Splash panel: the Revenant looms over Stonefire, who is on one knee, barely upright. The Revenant's claw is raised. Its corrupted speech balloon bleeds across the panel border: `"YoUr SoUl WiLl Be DeLiCiOuS, aNd YoUr BoDy ShAlL bE UsEd ApPr-"`
3. Narrow panel: white. Complete white. One word centered: **FIRE.**
4. Caption below the white: `Earlier.`

### Page 2: The Descent

1. Wide panel: exterior of the dungeon entrance — ancient stone, ivy, torchlight from within.
2. Panel: Stonefire descends stone stairs alone. Goliath frame, small in the architecture.
3. Panel: Stonefire at a labyrinth junction, three identical corridors. Pauses. Chooses.
4. Panel: a flicker at the edge of the frame — a cloaked figure, barely visible, following at distance.
5. Bottom panel: Stonefire enters a long corridor. At the far end, darkness that doesn't move the same way darkness should.

### Page 3: Something Steps From Shadow

1. Narrow panel: Stonefire stops. Stance shifts — feet spread, weight forward. He's sensed something.
2. Half-page: the Revenant emerges from the back end of the corridor. Its form is recognizable as once-humanoid but wrong — skin too tight, movement too deliberate. It turns its head toward Stonefire. Its first speech balloon is small, almost polite: `"AnD yOu ShOuLd HaVe Run."`
3. Panel: Stonefire's expression. Not fear. Calculation.
4. Narrow bottom panel: he moves.

### Page 4: It Gets Back Up

1. Action panel: Stonefire lands a devastating strike — both fists, full monk momentum, the Revenant crashes into the corridor wall.
2. Narrow beat panel: silence. The Revenant is crumpled at the wall's base.
3. Panel: Stonefire exhales, checks his hands. That should have done it.
4. Half-page: the Revenant's fingers move. Then its arms. It stands. The wounds are closing. Its speech balloon is larger now: `"YoU cAnNoT kIlL wHaT iS aLrEaDy DeAd."`

### Page 5: The Second Exchange

1. Panel: Stonefire attacks again — different approach, more desperate.
2. Panel: the Revenant takes the hit and does not go down this time. It catches Stonefire's second strike.
3. Splash panel: the Revenant crits — a single massive strike that sends Stonefire sliding along the stone floor, leaving marks. Stonefire ends up on hands and knees, blood on stone. High-damage visual: the hit should feel like hitting a wall at speed.
4. Strip panel: Stonefire doesn't get up. Not unconscious — but not able to rise either. The corridor stretches away behind him.

### Page 6: It Looms

1. Low angle panel: Stonefire looks up.
2. Wide panel: the Revenant approaches slowly. No hurry. It has time.
3. Close panel: Stonefire's hand presses the floor. Trying to rise. Doesn't quite make it.
4. Large panel: the Revenant stands over Stonefire, claw raised. Its speech balloon begins: `"YoUr SoUl WiLl Be DeLiCiOuS, aNd YoUr BoDy ShAlL bE UsEd ApPr-"`
5. Narrow bottom panel: the speech balloon is cut off by the panel border. Left edge of the next panel already glowing orange.

### Page 7: Fireball

1. Full-page splash: the corridor detonates in flame. The Revenant is at the center of the blast — the fire eats the Zalgo-text balloon mid-sentence, the corrupted letters fragmenting into the explosion. The corridor walls are scorched. Stonefire is in the foreground, face buried in his arm, shielded by the geometry of the floor.

   The fire does not come from Stonefire.
   
   In the far background, barely visible through the blast — a figure. Standing in the corridor entrance. Hood up.

### Page 8: Silver Eyes

1. Half-page: smoke. Settling ash. The Revenant's charred remains scattered on the stone floor. Stonefire still face-down for a moment.
2. Panel: Stonefire lifts his head.
3. Panel: footsteps approaching on stone. A shadow on the floor.
4. Panel: the hood falls back. Lloyd's face is obscured except for two things: silver eyes, glowing faintly in the smoke-dark corridor. He crouches to Stonefire's level.
5. Final panel: Stonefire, battered, looks up. Lloyd's hand extended to help him up. The silver eyes looking at him, expression unreadable. One line, verbatim from the session:

> **Lloyd:** *"Are you alright?"*

   No answer from Stonefire. No caption. End.

## Dialogue and Captions

- The Revenant speaks only in Zalgo alt-caps. Use the verbatim session text: `AnD yOu ShOuLd HaVe Run`, `YoU cAnNoT kIlL wHaT iS aLrEaDy DeAd`, `YoUr SoUl WiLl Be DeLiCiOuS, aNd YoUr BoDy ShAlL bE UsEd ApPr-`. Do not clean up the text.
- The Revenant's speech balloons are black with pale (off-white or pale gray) lettering. They slightly break their panel borders.
- Stonefire has no dialogue. He is the silent protagonist throughout.
- Lloyd has one line, on the final page. Verbatim.
- Captions are used only on pages 1 and 2: the `Earlier.` cut and the dungeon descent. No captions from page 3 onward — the horror relies on silence.

## Visual Design Notes

### Stonefire
- Goliath — large, gray-stone-toned skin. 7+ feet tall. Bald or close-cropped.
- Monk: no armor, wrapped hands, simple cloth. His body IS the weapon — the visual language should read as someone who has never needed anything else.
- Crucial: for most of the story he looks like the one in control. The horror is that it's not enough.
- Expression discipline: he doesn't emote much. The moment his expression breaks (page 5, on the floor) is the emotional turn.

### The Revenant
- Formerly humanoid — vaguely recognizable as having been a person. Decayed, skin tight, movement slightly wrong.
- Not shambling. Not slow. It moves with purpose.
- Key visual signature: the Zalgo speech balloon. The typography IS the character design. Every time it speaks, the lettering invades the panel.
- The regeneration should be shown, not told: wounds visibly closing between panels.

### Lloyd
- Cloaked throughout pages 2-7 — hood up, full silhouette. He should appear once as a background flicker on page 2 (barely visible), once in the fireball splash (silhouette only), and then fully revealed on page 8.
- The silver eyes are the reveal. They should glow slightly in the smoke-dark.
- His expression at the end is deliberately ambiguous — neither warm nor cold. He is present. That's enough.
- No other character details are shown. He keeps secrets.

### The Corridor
- Narrow, ancient, stone. Torch sconces providing the only warm light.
- The labyrinth quality: every panel should feel like there is no easy way out.
- The corridor should get darker as the issue progresses. By page 6, the sconces are at the edges; by page 7, the fireball is the only light.
