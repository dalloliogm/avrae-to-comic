# Noe and Ivy — Page 2 Script

## Page Goal

Establish the banter register fully. Both characters show who they are at their most performative — Noe bows, Ivy flirts-while-fighting, neither breaks frame. The page ends with Noe declining to be enslaved in a tone that implies he might consider it.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is wide across the top.
- Panels 2 and 3 share the middle row: left and right, roughly equal.
- Panel 4 is wide across the lower-middle.
- Panel 5 is a narrow closing strip at the bottom.

---

## Panel 1

Wide panel. Ivy in motion — she has launched scorching rays, three distinct bolts of orange-red fire from her outstretched arms, thrown mid-dance: arms back, body turning, the fire erupting from her palms. She moves through the spell like it's a choreographed sequence. The bolts are tracking toward where Noe was standing.

**Ivy:** *"Oh, you are so good—"*

**Art Notes:** She should be moving, not posed. The fire bolts arc through the panel with her at one end, Noe (partially visible) reacting at the other. Her combat style is fluid and performative — the attacks are incidental to the movement, not the other way around.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress mid-spin releasing three scorching ray bolts from her hands in a wide arc, orange-red fire bolts against deep blue desert night sky, dynamic motion, fantasy action comic panel, fire magic visual.

---

## Panel 2

Medium panel, left of middle row. Noe, mid-run, catches one of the bolts across his side — his expression goes tight, grimacing. He doesn't stop moving. The hit is real but he absorbs it while maintaining his posture.

**Noe:** *"H-Heavy hitting there, aren't you.."*

**Art Notes:** The hit should register on his face without making him look helpless. He's inconvenienced, not diminished. His grip on the sword stays firm even though the sword is largely decorative so far.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard mid-run taking a fire bolt hit across his ribs, grimacing, but continuing to move forward rather than recoiling, his longsword still in hand, desert night, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 3

Medium panel, right of middle row. Noe is now close to Ivy — inside the range where scorching rays are uncomfortable. He stops. He is looking at her directly. And then — he bows. A genuine, formal, slightly exaggerated bow. He is holding his sword at his side. His hair falls forward with the bow.

**Noe:** *"impressive feat you're showing, 'my' lady"*

The quotation marks around "my" are part of the line — he is quoting a title he's not sure applies.

**Art Notes:** The bow needs to read as genuine and slightly absurd simultaneously. It is absolutely a real bow. It is also happening during a fight. His expression, visible just before the bow completes, should be — appreciative. Not mocking.

**Prompt:** Grey-skinned bard performing a formal bow to a snake sorceress mid-combat, one hand at his chest, longsword at his side, hair falling forward, expression of genuine appreciation just before the bow completes, desert night, fantasy comedy comic panel.

---

## Panel 4

Wide panel. Ivy has her arms raised mid-gesture, the beginning of another charm attempt — her movement is now slower, more deliberate, swaying. She is looking at Noe with an expression that has shifted: he was supposed to run, and instead he bowed, and she is recalculating. The charm attempt begins — she is dancing, drawing his attention.

**Ivy:** *"You like me? Like what ya see?"*

**Art Notes:** This is her using her physical presence consciously as a weapon. The sway should be present in the art — she is genuinely, intentionally moving in a way designed to hold attention. The question is not rhetorical; she expects an answer.

**Prompt:** Snake sorceress swaying in a deliberate slow dance, arms raised mid-gesture, casting a charm spell, looking directly at a grey-skinned bard (partially visible), expression calculating and confident, desert night, fantasy comic panel, warm lighting.

---

## Panel 5

Narrow closing strip. Two-shot: Noe and Ivy, both visible, facing each other. Noe's expression: considering, unbothered, not charmed but not cold. The charm failed — he shook it — but he didn't look away.

**Ivy:** *"I may be a sorceress, but I am sure I could make some arrangements... once you are my servant!"*

**Noe:** *"heh.. maybe some day"*

**Art Notes:** His *"heh.. maybe some day"* should land as genuinely ambiguous. He is neither accepting nor refusing. His tone is a verbal shrug with something warmer behind it. This is the page's closing line and it should feel like an invitation to Page 3.

**Prompt:** Two-shot panel, grey-skinned bard and snake sorceress facing each other in desert night, bard's expression amused and noncommittal, sorceress's expression triumphant but slightly puzzled he isn't more affected, casual close-quarters tension, fantasy comedy comic.

---

## Lettering Notes

- Noe's bow line (*"impressive feat you're showing, 'my' lady"*) uses quotation marks around *my* exactly as written — the punctuation is part of the line.
- Ivy's *"You like me? Like what ya see?"* is a conversational question — upbeat balloon, not aggressive.
- Noe's *"heh.. maybe some day"* is quiet, informal — the balloon is smaller than his other lines. The ellipsis matters.
- No captions on this page.

## Continuity Notes

- The cloud of daggers is not yet present; it appears on Page 3.
- Noe's sword has not connected with anything yet. This should be visually readable — it's a prop so far.
- Ivy is constantly in motion. She should not be still in any panel on this page.
- The charm failed (Noe's wisdom save succeeded). The art should show him not being affected, but also not pretending he didn't notice.
