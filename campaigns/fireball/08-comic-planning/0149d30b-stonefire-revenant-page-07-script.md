# Stonefire and the Revenant — Page 7 Script

## Page Goal

One full page. The fireball. Everything that was building for six pages resolves in one image.

## Layout

- Single full-page splash.

---

## Panel 1 (Full Page)

Full-page splash. The corridor detonates.

The fireball originates from the right side of the panel — from the direction of the corridor entrance, the direction Lloyd is coming from. The Revenant is at the center of the explosion, wreathed in fire, its form partially disintegrating into ash and light. The corrupted speech balloon from the previous page is visible — or rather, was visible — the Zalgo text fragmenting, the black balloon body burning at the edges, individual alt-cap letters scattering and dissolving into the explosion as typographic debris. The sentence never finished.

Stonefire is in the lower-left foreground. He is face-down with one arm across his face — he heard or sensed what was coming in the last half-second and covered. The floor around him is scorched at the edges but he is at the blast perimeter, not the center. He is not on fire.

The corridor walls are scorched, the stone blackened and cracked. The torch sconces are gone — the only light now is the fireball's bloom, which fills the panel from right to center in a wash of orange-white.

Far right background, barely visible through the heat bloom: a cloaked figure. Standing in the corridor entrance. Hood up. The figure is partially obscured by the edge of the explosion. Only one detail is readable: the silhouette's proportions, standing still, and the faint outline of an arm in a casting position — lowering now that the spell has been released.

**SFX:** None. The fireball is large enough that it doesn't need a sound effect — or rather, the sound is the image.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:**

The key visual ideas for this page, in priority order:

1. The Zalgo text fragmenting in the explosion. Individual letters — `Y`, `o`, `U` — visibly breaking apart and burning, the corrupted speech literally destroyed by fire mid-sentence. This is the defining image of the issue: the thing that could not be stopped by physical force was silenced by fire, and the fire also silenced its words.

2. Stonefire shielded, not killed. He is clearly alive — the arm across his face is a deliberate response, not a body thrown. The fire is not centered on him.

3. Lloyd as silhouette only. No face, no details. One arm lowering from a cast position. The reader knows who did this but the figure is not confirmed.

4. The scale of the blast relative to the corridor. The corridor is narrow; the fireball fills it wall-to-wall and ceiling-to-floor. This is not a precise spell. Lloyd chose certainty over caution.

**Prompt:** Full-page comic splash, a fireball detonating in a narrow stone dungeon corridor, a humanoid revenant at the center of the explosion disintegrating, corrupted Zalgo-text letters fragmenting and burning in the blast, a goliath monk in the lower foreground with one arm across his face shielded at the blast perimeter, far right background a cloaked silhouette with arm lowering from a casting position barely visible through the fire, scorched corridor walls, no torches, only firelight, fantasy horror comic full page splash, powerful and definitive.

## Lettering Notes

- No dialogue, captions, or sound effects on this page.
- The Zalgo text fragments are lettering elements — individual characters (`Y`, `o`, `U`, `r`, `S`, `o`, `U`, `L`) from the Revenant's last speech visually dissolving into the explosion. They should be placed as if they are caught in the blast, not as a deliberate text element. The letterer places them; they read as debris rather than speech.
- The balloon body itself should be visible at the panel's center-left, its edges burning — the black balloon outline fragmenting into the explosion. It should look like a speech bubble being erased.

## Continuity Notes

- This is the fireball that interrupted the speech on pages 1 and 6. The reader has now seen the same moment from three angles: the cold open (horizontal, from the side), page 6 panel 5 (from behind, approaching), and now the detonation itself.
- The Revenant is destroyed here. This is unambiguous. Its regeneration does not apply to disintegration by fire.
- Lloyd does not speak on this page and is not identified. He is a silhouette only.
- Stonefire's covering-arm position carries forward to page 8 — he begins page 8 face-down, and then lifts his head.
