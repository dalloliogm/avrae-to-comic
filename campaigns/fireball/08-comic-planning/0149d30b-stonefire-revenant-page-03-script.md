# Stonefire and the Revenant — Page 3 Script

## Page Goal

The Revenant emerges. Its first words land. Stonefire decides to fight. The page ends before the first blow — the attack carries over to page 4.

## Layout

- Four panels.
- Panel 1 is a narrow reaction strip.
- Panel 2 is a large vertical half-page splash for the emergence.
- Panel 3 is a close-up of Stonefire's face.
- Panel 4 is a narrow action strip — movement beginning.

---

## Panel 1

Narrow horizontal strip. Stonefire in the corridor — he has stopped walking. His feet are now spread, weight forward, center low. He has shifted into a fighting stance without deciding to. His body knew before he did.

His face is in profile. He is looking at the far end of the corridor. His expression is still: not afraid, not excited. Calculating.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** The stance change is the only event in this panel. Everything else — lighting, corridor, nothing moving — should be identical to panel 5 of page 2. This creates a before/after with a single changed element: Stonefire's posture.

**Prompt:** Profile view of a goliath monk in a dungeon corridor, feet spread in a low wide fighting stance, no weapons drawn, alert expression, torch-lit stone walls, still and waiting, fantasy comic panel.

---

## Panel 2

Large vertical splash, dominant panel. The Revenant emerges from the dark end of the corridor.

It is walking. Slowly. Deliberately. Its form is humanoid — or was: the proportions are slightly elongated, the skin pulled too tight across the frame, the movement subtly wrong in the way that a person who has been made into something against their will moves. It looks like it was taken. It does not look like it chose this.

It turns its head toward Stonefire as it comes into torchlight. The moment its face catches light, its speech balloon appears — black, with pale off-white Zalgo lettering, the edges of the balloon slightly jagged, bleeding just outside the panel border:

**Revenant:** `AnD yOu ShOuLd HaVe Run.`

**Art Notes:** The Revenant should not look like a monster at a distance. It should look like a person in the wrong state. The wrongness is in the details: the skin, the movement, the eyes that catch light at the wrong angle. The corrupted speech balloon is what marks it as fundamentally other. The balloon's slight panel-bleed should be subtle here — it becomes more invasive as the issue progresses.

**Prompt:** A humanoid revenant in a torch-lit dungeon corridor, walking slowly forward with subtly wrong proportions, skin pulled too tight, face turning toward viewer as it enters torchlight, corrupted Zalgo-text speech balloon in black with pale lettering: "AnD yOu ShOuLd HaVe Run", fantasy horror comic vertical panel.

---

## Panel 3

Close-up on Stonefire's face. He is looking at the Revenant. His expression has settled into something that is not fear and not contempt — just the focused attention of someone who has assessed a threat and is deciding how to take it apart.

There is one small thing: the muscle in his jaw is tight. He noticed something about the Revenant that he is processing. He does not look entirely sure.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This panel is the last beat before combat. It should hold for a moment. The tight jaw is the only tell — everything else in his expression is composed. Do not show what he is looking at; just the looking.

**Prompt:** Close-up of a goliath monk's face in dungeon torchlight, jaw tight, eyes focused forward with careful attention, not afraid but not entirely certain, fantasy comic close-up panel.

---

## Panel 4

Narrow horizontal strip at the bottom of the page. Stonefire is in motion — the beginning of a charge. One foot has launched off the stone floor. His wrapped hands are raised. The corridor stretches behind him; the darkness with the Revenant is ahead. He is committed.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This is not the impact — that is page 4. This strip is the breath before. Show movement beginning: foot off stone, body angled forward, the mass of him in motion. The Revenant is not in this panel — only Stonefire, and the corridor he is crossing.

**Prompt:** A goliath monk launching into a charge in a narrow stone dungeon corridor, one foot off the ground, wrapped hands raised, full body in motion, torchlight, the corridor ahead empty, fantasy comic action strip panel.

---

## Lettering Notes

- The Revenant's balloon on Panel 2 is the first appearance of the Zalgo-text style. The text `AnD yOu ShOuLd HaVe Run.` is verbatim. The balloon edge should be slightly irregular/jagged, not perfectly smooth. The balloon should barely cross the right edge of the panel — just the outer tip, not significantly.
- No other dialogue or captions this page.
- Stonefire is silent throughout the issue. He does not respond to the Revenant.

## Continuity Notes

- The Revenant's emergence from darkness echoes the wrong darkness Stonefire noticed on page 2. The reader should recognize they were looking at the same thing.
- The Revenant's speech on page 2 (`...ApPr-`) has been cut off by the panel edge. Its speech on this page is a new, complete line. The reader has now heard two things from it.
- Stonefire's charge on Panel 4 will connect on Page 4, Panel 1. These two panels should read as a clean cut between pages.
