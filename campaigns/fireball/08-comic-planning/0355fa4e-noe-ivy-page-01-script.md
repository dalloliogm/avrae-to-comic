# Noe and Ivy — Page 1 Script

## Page Goal

Establish the characters and the register simultaneously. This is a desert at night. Two people face each other. One of them immediately launches into a spell in verse. The other's reaction — not fear, not flight, just *annoyance tipping into interest* — tells you everything about the tone of what follows.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is a wide establishing strip across the top of the page.
- Panels 2 and 3 share the middle row: left and right.
- Panel 4 is wide across the lower-middle.
- Panel 5 is a narrow closing strip at the bottom.

---

## Panel 1

Wide establishing strip. A desert at night. Open terrain, no walls — just sand extending in all directions, a deep blue-black sky overhead, stars dense and bright. The only light is moonlight and the faint warm glow that precedes magical combat. In the middle distance, two figures face each other. They are roughly thirty feet apart. Their shapes are distinct but not detailed here: one is tall with long hair and holds a sword loosely at his side; the other stands with hip cocked, arms relaxed, the posture of someone who has already decided how this ends.

**No dialogue. No caption.**

**Art Notes:** This panel establishes the scale — two people alone in a very large desert. The comedy is that neither of them looks alarmed. Frame them small in the landscape, not large. Stars are important: the sky should feel genuinely beautiful, which makes the fight about to happen seem slightly absurd.

**Prompt:** Wide establishing panel, two small figures facing each other in an open desert at night, deep blue-black sky dense with stars, moonlight on sand, one figure with a longsword held loosely, one figure in a relaxed pose, fantasy comic art, vast quiet landscape.

---

## Panel 2

Medium panel, left half of middle row. Close on Noe Pavel. He is standing with his left hand raised to his chest, head slightly bowed — a performer's stance before a performance. His grey skin catches the moonlight. His long dark hair falls over one side of his face, hiding his left ear. He is wearing simple, worn clothing — a bard's traveling garb, faded. He exhales, composing himself.

One speech balloon rising from him as he begins to cast — not the spell yet, just a breath:

**Noe:** *"such a day.."*

**Art Notes:** He should look slightly dramatic about this, like someone who has a lot of feelings about everything and intends to share them. The grey skin reads immediately — this is not a living person in the usual sense, but he carries it with complete ease.

**Prompt:** Medium panel, grey-skinned male bard in worn traveling clothes, long dark hair obscuring one ear, hand pressed to chest, eyes slightly downcast, composing himself before casting, desert night setting, fantasy comic art.

---

## Panel 3

Medium panel, right half of middle row. Ivy, watching. She is standing with her weight on one hip, arms loose at her sides — the dancer's rest position. Her scales catch moonlight at her temples and along her collarbone. She is watching Noe's pre-casting pose with the expression of someone who has seen this before and is calculating how long it will take.

**No dialogue.**

**Art Notes:** She should look unimpressed in a way that's already shading toward interested. The scales should be present but not overwhelming — she reads as primarily humanoid. Her stillness here is notable because she will almost never be still again until Page 7.

**Prompt:** Medium panel, snake-scaled sorceress standing in rest position, hip cocked, arms loose at sides, watching someone off-panel with an expression of flat assessment, scales visible at temples and collarbone, desert night, fantasy comic art.

---

## Panel 4

Wide panel. Noe is casting. His left hand is extended toward Ivy, his right still at his chest. Musical-notation lines radiate from his outstretched hand in a directed stream — discordant, slightly off, the visual language of something that sounds wrong. The lines end at Ivy, who flinches — the first time we see her react.

**Noe (spell-casting voice — italic, or differentiated typography):** *"standing in the wind, the dissonant song are what you shall hear me sing!"*

**Art Notes:** The spell-casting voice should feel like verse — it has a rhythm to it even when the meter is slightly awkward. The musical notation lines are not harsh; they are delicate and wrong at the same time. Ivy's flinch is small — she's not hurt badly, but the spell connected.

**Prompt:** A grey-skinned bard casting Dissonant Whispers, extending one hand toward a snake sorceress, discordant musical notation lines flowing from his hand in a directed stream toward her, she recoils slightly, desert night setting, fantasy comic panel, spell effect art.

---

## Panel 5

Narrow closing strip. Ivy straightens. She turns to face Noe fully. Her arms come up slightly from her sides — not aggressive yet, just readying. Her expression has shifted: the flat assessment is gone. Now she looks like someone who has just been made mad and finds that interesting.

**Ivy:** *"Oh, now you made me mad..."*

**Art Notes:** This line is the thesis statement of the entire comic. She is not scared. She is not fleeing. She is *interested*. The half-smile, or the lack of a full frown, should communicate this. She is beginning to move.

**Prompt:** Narrow strip panel, snake sorceress straightening from a neutral stance, arms coming up from her sides, expression shifting from flat to energized, a slight hint of movement beginning, desert night, fantasy comic art.

---

## Lettering Notes

- Noe's spell-casting dialogue is in italic — or uses a distinct font from his normal speech. It has a verse quality.
- Ivy's closing line is her normal voice — confident, direct, not loud. The balloon should be clean and clear.
- No captions on this page. The scene establishes itself.

## Continuity Notes

- Noe's missing ear is hidden throughout Page 1 by his hair. It should become visible at specific moments — the hug on Page 3, when his hair falls.
- Ivy is standing still in Panel 3 — this is worth noting because it is the last time she is still until Page 7. Every subsequent panel featuring her should have her in some degree of motion.
- The cloud of daggers is not present yet. It appears on Page 3.
- This page establishes them as equal. Neither is visually coded as the hero.
