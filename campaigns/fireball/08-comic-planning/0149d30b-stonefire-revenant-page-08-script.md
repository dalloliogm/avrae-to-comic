# Stonefire and the Revenant — Page 8 Script

## Page Goal

Aftermath. Stonefire finds out who saved him. The issue ends before the question can be answered: who is Lloyd, why was he here, what does this mean. The final panel has one line. No answer.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is a wide atmospheric panel — smoke and quiet.
- Panel 2 is a close-up — Stonefire lifting his head.
- Panel 3 is a medium panel — footsteps.
- Panel 4 is the reveal — Lloyd's face, silver eyes.
- Panel 5 is the final panel — the hand, the question, the end.

---

## Panel 1

Wide panel. The corridor after the fireball. Smoke drifts. Ash settles. The stone walls are blackened and cracked. Where the Revenant stood, there is only scorched floor and scattered char. No torches remain — the light is entirely residual from cooling stone and a few embers at the far edge of the panel.

Stonefire is in the foreground, still face-down on the floor. Not moving yet. The corridor is completely still.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This panel should feel like held breath after something tremendous. The contrast with the preceding full-page blast should be immediate and total. Hot → quiet. Big → small. The corridor looks tired. Stonefire looks small in it.

**Prompt:** Wide dungeon corridor aftermath panel, smoke drifting, ash settling on scorched black stone walls and floor, no torches, cooling embers at edges, a goliath monk lying face-down in the foreground, absolute stillness, fantasy horror comic atmospheric panel.

---

## Panel 2

Close-up. Stonefire lifts his head from the floor. His cheek is marked with soot and stone dust. His eyes are open — present, aware. He is not looking at anything specific yet; he is orienting, understanding that he is still alive and the Revenant is not.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** This is the first time in the issue we have seen Stonefire's face in this direction — looking up after being down, from a recovery angle rather than a fighting angle. The expression should be: alive, processing, not yet grateful. He does not know who saved him.

**Prompt:** Close-up of a goliath monk lifting his head from a dungeon floor, cheek marked with soot and stone dust, eyes open and present, looking forward without focus, the awareness of being alive, fantasy comic close-up panel.

---

## Panel 3

Medium panel. The sound of footsteps on stone — represented visually by a shoe or boot landing on the scorched corridor floor. The foot belongs to a figure approaching from the direction the fireball came from. A shadow precedes the figure along the corridor floor.

Stonefire's hand — the same wrapped-hand close-up from page 6 — is visible in the lower portion of the panel, pressed against the floor, beginning to push upright.

**No dialogue or caption.**

**Art Notes:** The boot and shadow give the reader just enough: someone is coming. The same wrapped hand from page 6's desperate press is now pushing Stonefire back toward vertical. Small difference, significant: the hand was trembling before; now it is steady.

**Prompt:** A booted foot landing on scorched dungeon stone floor, a shadow preceding its figure down the corridor, in the lower foreground a monk's wrapped hand beginning to push upright, steady now, fantasy horror comic medium panel, approach sequence.

---

## Panel 4

The reveal. Lloyd crouches at Stonefire's level. His hood has fallen back — or been drawn back; the motion is ambiguous. His face is mostly in shadow from the angle, but two things are immediately readable: the lines of his face, and his eyes. His eyes are silver. Not glowing like magic or divine power — simply silver, lit from within, as if the metal were the iris color rather than brown or blue. They are not casting light; they are holding it.

He is looking directly at Stonefire.

His expression is not warm. Not cold. Not relieved. It is simply: attention. He is looking at Stonefire the way someone looks at a person they have been following, whose condition now matters.

**No dialogue or caption on this panel — the line is in panel 5.**

**Art Notes:** This is the visual payoff of the whole issue. The reader has been given: a cloaked figure at the junction on page 2 (easy to miss), a silhouette casting the fireball on page 7, and now this face. The silver eyes are the defining feature — everything else about Lloyd is left unspecified. He could be any age, any race. The eyes are the answer to the question and simultaneously the start of a larger question.

**Prompt:** A cloaked figure with hood fallen back crouched at a goliath monk's level in a smoke-filled dungeon corridor, face mostly in shadow, silver eyes looking directly forward with focused attention, not warm not cold, mysterious, fantasy comic close-up reveal panel.

---

## Panel 5

Final panel. Wide enough to show both characters. Stonefire on the scorched stone, partially upright now — one knee still down, but head raised, looking at Lloyd. Lloyd still crouched, one hand extended. Not a command, not a performance — a hand extended to help someone stand.

The corridor stretches behind them both. The ash is still settling. It is very quiet.

Lloyd's speech balloon is the only one in the entire issue that is not the Revenant's corrupted text — it is a normal balloon, small, understated. The line is verbatim:

**Lloyd:** *"Are you alright?"*

That is the last line of the issue. Stonefire does not answer. The page ends.

**Art Notes:** The extended hand should be the visual center of this panel — not Lloyd's face, not Stonefire's expression, but the hand between them. It is the issue's final image before the page ends. The question is not rhetorical and it is not small-talk — Lloyd has been following Stonefire and chose when to intervene. The timing of that choice is left without explanation.

Do not show Stonefire reaching for the hand. That beat doesn't happen on the page.

**Prompt:** Wide dungeon corridor final panel, a cloaked figure with silver eyes crouching and extending one hand to a goliath monk partially upright on the scorched stone floor, settling ash around both, quiet aftermath atmosphere, one small understated speech balloon: "Are you alright?", the hand extended between them as the visual center, fantasy comic quiet closing panel.
