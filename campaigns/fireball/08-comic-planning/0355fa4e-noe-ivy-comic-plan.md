## Adaptation Approach

This is a love story that begins with a sword and ends at an oasis.

Unlike the Revenant adaptation, this one goes straight through — no cold open, no rewind. The story earns its ending by moving forward the whole way. The dramatic question is not *will anyone survive* but *will either of them stop performing long enough to admit they like each other* — which they never quite do, and that's the point.

The fight is a dance. The dialogue is flirtation. The physical comedy (hug-into-cloud-of-daggers, poison first kiss, being carried unconscious to an oasis) accumulates into something neither character planned.

**Structural note:** Two competing ending narratives exist in the session log. Noe's player narrated a post-fight scene mid-combat: *"as time passes, Ivy would find herself waking back up to the calm night in the desert, beside her is the adventuring bard, sitting by the campfire and roasting something."* He imagined winning. Instead, Ivy knocked him out and carried him to the oasis. The comic resolves both: he wakes up at the oasis and starts a fire — which is Noe's ending, achieved on Ivy's terms. Neither won. Both ended up at the fire.

The story has three emotional movements:
1. **Performance** — two people being very impressive at each other. Neither wants to lose.
2. **Slip** — Noe breaks the performance with the hug. It doesn't win the fight, but it changes the register.
3. **Admission** — the oasis. Nobody says anything. They don't have to.

## Issue Shape

- Format: 8-page short comic.
- Point of view: shared — Noe and Ivy alternate panel focus throughout.
- Tone: romantic comedy, kinetic, warm. The inverse of the Revenant issue in every way.
- Visual motif: Ivy's dance/sway as a throughline — she is always in motion; the moment she stops moving is the emotional pivot on Page 7.

## Page Beats

### Page 1: The Dissonant Song

Desert at night. Two figures face each other. The encounter has already started. Noe casts Dissonant Whispers in verse. Ivy responds: *"Oh, now you made me mad..."* — and dances.

### Page 2: Impressive Feat

The banter exchange establishes the register. Noe bows mid-fight. Ivy tries to charm him. He's not charmed but he's not disinterested either. *"heh.. maybe some day."*

### Page 3: Free Will

The hug. Noe drops his sword, walks toward her, says: *"since I still have my free will, I will do what I want before it's gone."* He hugs her. It is a real hug. He smells like he has been dead. He drags her into the cloud of daggers while hugging her. Both take damage. He drops concentration before she's shredded and collapses.

### Page 4: Keep Going

Aftermath of the hug. Both on the floor for a moment. Then more combat. Ivy: *"You like the way I shake? Even though I'm a snake?"* Noe is not charmed but he actually watches her. *"yeah.. I do, keep going if you will."*

### Page 5: Heat

*"Oh, a huge fan I see, how about I heat things up?"* — burning hands. Noe on fire. *"GAH!"* and *"hot? Why thank you!"* Noe heals himself by reciting a poem mid-combat. He admits he stopped watching her because he didn't like what it implied.

### Page 6: The Kiss

*"Hey sugar. How about we stop with the flame wars, and I just give you a little kiss."* — she approaches. He lets her. Purple gas fills his lungs. *"I don't think I like my first kiss that much."* He goes down.

### Page 7: Carried

He falls. Beat. She looks at him. She picks him up. No dialogue. No narration.

### Page 8: The Oasis

Stars. Sand. Water. She's set him beside the oasis. He wakes up. He starts a small fire. He's roasting something over it. She's still there. Neither speaks. End.

## Visual Design Notes

### Noe Pavel
- Race: Reborn (previously died; looks human but wrong — abnormally grey skin)
- Description from log: *"appearance of a human but with abnormally grey skin Hiding behind his long hair, one of his ear is missing."*
- Long dark hair that falls forward; the missing ear is on the side revealed later, not immediately obvious
- A longsword he keeps not hitting anyone with — carried with genuine dignity, used badly
- When casting, musical notation or trailing text flows around him. His spells are recited, not gesticulated.
- His grey skin, combined with his formal bearing and tendency to bow mid-combat, reads as someone who is slightly theatrical and slightly dead — he's aware of both
- He smells. He knows. He doesn't apologize.

### Ivy
- Race: Swain (snake-type sorceress)
- Scales at her temples or along her neck — present but not overwhelming; she reads as humanoid first
- She is always in motion. Every panel she is in, she should be mid-movement: swaying, throwing her arms back, turning, stepping
- Fire spells (scorching ray, burning hands) — warm orange-red effects
- The poison spray/kiss: purple-green mist
- Her combat persona is fully performative: she frames everything as flirtation and never breaks the frame
- **Key pivot on Page 7:** the first time she stops dancing is when she walks over to Noe's unconscious body and picks him up. That stillness, relative to everything before, is the emotional beat.

### The Setting
- Desert at night: deep blue sky, cool-shadow sand, no horizon features except open desert and stars
- The fight happens in open terrain — no walls, no dungeon, just open night and light from their spells
- Noe's cloud of daggers: a shimmering blue-white geometric cube of spinning blades (Noe narrates it into existence with verse; it hangs in the air visually distinct)
- The oasis on Page 8: dark water reflecting stars, one or two palm trees, small fire in the foreground, sand in all directions

### Spell Visual Vocabulary
- Dissonant Whispers: discordant musical notation lines radiating from Noe's hand, aimed at Ivy
- Scorching Ray: three distinct bolts of orange-red fire from Ivy's outstretched arms
- Cloud of Daggers: a hovering 5-foot cube of tumbling blue-white blades; it hangs in the panel like a character
- Burning Hands: a cone of fire from Ivy's open palms; maximum orange heat
- Poison Spray (the kiss): purple-green gas from the kiss itself, filling Noe's silhouette

### The Hug
The most important single image in the issue. Reference: a full embrace — not a grab, not a wrestle — he genuinely hugs her with both arms and genuine warmth. She is inside his arms with her own at her sides, surprised. The absurdity is that this is happening mid-combat and that the cloud of daggers is visible in the same panel, waiting. The tenderness of the hug and the violence of what comes next exist in the same frame.
