## Highlighted Scene 1: Cuco — Gem of Brightness (10th Appearance)

**Setup:** Cuco is protecting Kib (kobold) from a flock of griffons. He uses his Gem of Brightness to fire a cone-blind at three griffons.

**Beats:**
- Two griffons save, one fails
- Cuco: *"Guess they are pretty used to the heavens.."*
- Beat. *"it was worth saving some resources. But maybe he was too frugal."*

This is the first time Cuco second-guesses a resource decision *after* it worked. He successfully blinded one griffon, two resisted, and he immediately wonders if he should have used something stronger. His frugality has become a form of self-reproach. ★★★★☆ character beat.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Emyr + Elysium Drow Dungeon

**Setup:** Changeling ranger Emyr and Shadar-kai paladin/warlock Elysium (riding their horse Primrose) infiltrate a drow-guarded area. They need to pick a locked door.

**Beats:**
- Emyr: *"I know how to use these but I'm not the most dexterous"* — pulls out thieves' tools
- Elysium: *"You know me, darling. You really think I would be so well-known without having magic fingers?"* — winks, works on the lock
- Elysium sets off the alarm trap
- Elysium: *"Oops?"*
- Web fills the room
- Drow spot them; 70ft away; ready to raise alarm
- Elysium, commenting on their height difference: *"Pfht, I'm glad to see you're both stooping down to my level then, hm? Or should I say standing up?"* — Emyr fires the Wand of Fireballs directly into the drow priestess's face

**Why it works for comics:** The height-difference joke (Elysium is a Shadar-kai on a horse; Emyr is a changeling of variable height) lands right before the fireball. The *"Oops?"* after setting off the trap is the first punchline; the Fireball is the second.
