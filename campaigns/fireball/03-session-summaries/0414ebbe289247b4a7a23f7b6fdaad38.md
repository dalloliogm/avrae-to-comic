## Highlighted Scene 1: Karst + Sylvia — Mountain Climb

**Setup:** Paladin/warlock Karst (Reborn) and wizard Sylvia ascend a steep mountain together to reach an impossible house at the summit. Air elementals block the path. Sylvia is on her first adventure. One player narrates both characters throughout.

**Beats:**
- First elemental: Sylvia casts Suggestion: *"I suggest you.. leave us alone!"* — it works
- Sylvia *"blinks, repeatedly, almost surprised that it actually worked? She chuckled softly to herself."*
- Karst *"looked equally as surprised, and.. proud?"*
- Second elemental won't be talked down; Karst: *"..Great. Another one."* — kills it
- Dormant third elemental blocks path; Karst sighs, holds back, tries Command, then finishes it
- Near summit: Sylvia casts Fly on Karst; Karst grapples Sylvia; they fly past the dangers together
- Summit description: *"The very top of the mountain is in sight, a small house perched impossibly on it, as you notice the fiercest of winds swirling around you. The air is thin and you hear whispers.. Of death."*
- Sylvia casts Greater Invisibility on Karst; Karst fights the final air elemental invisible, blasting it with Eldritch Blast from thin air
- Approaching the house door: *"Well.. I guess this is the home stretch, miss. Hopefully this can help you out."*
- After surviving another elemental: **"Miss, this is fun!"**
- *Sylvia smiled, seemingly enjoying her first adventure, so far.*

**Why it works for comics:** The mountain climb is a complete 8-page structure: base → first obstacle (talked down) → second obstacle (fought) → third obstacle (worked around) → summit approach → greater invisibility stealth run → final push → house door. Sylvia's escalating delight (*"Miss, this is fun!"*) as the danger increases is the emotional spine. Karst's stoic pride is visible through narration. The invisibility-blast sequence is visually striking without dialogue. The ending (door in sight, whispers of death) gives a natural ellipsis.
