## Highlighted Scene 1: Corryn + Bast + Nara — Githyanki Fight + House Coda

**Setup:** Paladins Corryn and Bastiun (Bast) and rogue Nara Mado fight Githyanki. Bast goes down during the fight. Nara Mado sees this.

**Beats:**
- Bast drops; Nara Mado's reaction: *".....Unacceptable."* — then rages
- Bast is stabilized; fight concludes
- Corryn has a surprise ready for after
- Corryn: *"Bast and I are going to build a house for us right by where they are going to live. What type of house would you like?"*
- Nara Mado: *"Dark and light... shiny gold dragons and dark everywhere else... Just like us... I love gothic style."*
- Corryn sweeps Bast up; they plan inn details

**Why it works for comics:** The pivot from *"Unacceptable"* (rage state) to *"what style of house would you like?"* is the entire emotional arc. Two characters in established love asking a third character — who just watched one of them nearly die — to help design their future home. Nara Mado's answer (*"dark and light... just like us"*) is the last panel. The fight itself is an 8-page structure with this as the closer.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Aegis + Lo'ren — Conglomerate Simulation

**Setup:** Fighter Aegis and half-elf cleric Lo'ren fight in a simulation of the Conglomerate — enemies that continuously morph and replace themselves. Lo'ren fights from invisibility, healing Aegis between waves.

**Beats:**
- Lo'ren heals Aegis repeatedly without being seen
- One enemy is dominated and turns on its allies; it then impales itself
- Lo'ren: *"Well...that was a bit sad."*
- The simulation loops; they keep going
- Neither character breaks; neither panics; they settle into a rhythm

**Why it works for comics:** The invisibility mechanic makes Lo'ren's presence felt without being visible — Aegis knows someone is there, the reader sees Lo'ren acting, the enemies don't. The *"that was a bit sad"* line after a dominated enemy kills itself captures Lo'ren's register exactly. The Conglomerate's endless morphing makes it a visually inventive monster sequence.
