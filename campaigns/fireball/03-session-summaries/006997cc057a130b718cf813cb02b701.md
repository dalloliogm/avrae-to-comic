## Best Scene A: Kib, Cuco, and Namaka'e vs Forsworn + Wendigo (turns ~15–42)

**Setup:** The party fights four Forsworn cultists at an altar, with a banished wendigo set to return. Kib is a 2-foot green kobold artillerist riding Namaka'e's shoulder-cannon slot. Cuco is an albino harengon (6'3" bunny) chronurgy wizard. Namaka'e is a Triton rune knight.

**Key beats:**
- Kib runs at the wendigo: Cuco shouts `"KIB!? What are you DOING!?"` and has to pivot everything to save him
- FO2 grabs Kib as a hostage — the cultists realise killing the kobold will break the party
- Cuco, restrained by Entangle, yells: `"Oi! Think fast chuckle nuts!"` and Mind Whips three cultists at once
- Namaka'e hauls Kib back and force-feeds him a healing potion: `"I'm stuffing this in your mouth. Use it."`
- Kib fires a maximised Fireball: `"Hey, taste napalm!"` — the wendigo takes 112 damage and dies instantly
- Kib cackles `"Kaboom!"` while the flames reflect off his goggles. Then immediately: `"I... Yeah.. um.. didn't expect that to happen."`

**OOC level:** Low-moderate. DM provides IC narrative from the cultists' POV. Player banter mostly doubles as IC.

**Comic potential:** Very high. Kobold-does-something-stupid → forced rescue → kobold accidentally obliterates the boss. Three distinct character voices, great escalation, single explosive payoff.

## Strong Scene B: Dryads and the Charmed Bard (turns ~55–78)

**Setup:** Vaz (Stout Halfling bard), Evelynn Windove (Half-Elf death cleric), and Maria Ravendusk (Variant Human samurai) fight eight dryads in a sacred forest. The dryads keep using Fey Charm on Vaz specifically.

**Key beats:**
- Vaz keeps failing Wisdom saves; each time his eyes glow green and he tries to help the enemy
- One charm breaks when a dryad tries to sacrifice Maria — `"Wait... no Maria?!? What is happening"` — Vaz snaps back
- Another dryad immediately re-charms him
- Vaz eventually shakes it for good: `"WHAT IN TARNATION — you slimy harlots I will end you all one by one for this opprobrium!"` — then casts Shatter on five dryads at once
- Maria fires the killing shot: `"That's more like it! This fight was way more challenging and exciting than the previous ones."`

**OOC level:** Moderate. DM controls all eight dryads and narrates IC from their POV; some confusion between players on which enemy was which.

**Comic potential:** High. Charm mechanics make for strong visual gag panels (green-eyed Vaz helping the enemy), and the eventual snap-out delivers a satisfying reversal. The dryad romantic subplot with Vaz is worth leaning into.
