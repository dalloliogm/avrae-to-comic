## Highlighted Scene 1: Animated Statue Guardians

**Setup:** Four-person party (Gyn the multi-class elf, Louhin the Eladrin ranger/fighter, Kuru the kenku wizard/rogue, Tarhin the half-dragon fighter) enters a temple and triggers two colossus statues. The statues' signature attack: a homing spear that travels between all three party members before returning to their hand.

**Beats:**
- Statue kneels, launches spear — it hits Louhin, swerves to Gyn, turns toward Kuru before returning
- Tarhin acid-spews the statue; it falls; rubble collapses onto Tarhin
- Tarhin: *"No problem no killing guy"* — says cheerfully to the rubble
- Second statue falls; Tarhin and Louhin distribute goodberries, triage HP
- Tarhin: *"Should we go down? Stealthily"* — into a dungeon they haven't scouted
- Louhin casts Pass Without Trace; Gyn casts Guidance

**Why it works for comics:** The homing spear is a great visual — a single weapon drawing lines between all three characters before looping back. Tarhin's cheerful reaction to being buried in rubble from the statue he just killed establishes his character in one panel.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Iron Golem — Sword Through Door

**Setup:** After clearing the statues, the party reaches a locked door. Kuru (invisible) picks the lock. The door is trapped.

**Beats:**
- Lock gives. Then: *"a massive sword has pierced the door and struck right in your stomach"*
- Iron Golem is visible through the door — it's been waiting on the other side
- Kuru, stabbed through the torso, now needs to fight the Golem through the door
- The invisible Kuru, continuing to be re-cast invisible each round by Tarhin

**Why it works for comics:** The image of a sword punching through a closed door as the reward for successfully picking the lock is a perfect setup-subversion. The reader sees the door give — then immediately sees a sword come through it.
