## Highlighted Scene 1: Cuco's First Defeat — The Ghost in the Statue

**Setup:** Cuco (albino harengon artificer/chronurgy wizard, 5th appearance in dataset) enters the arena against SS1 — a ghost that hides inside a statue, using the construct as armor. Neither can damage the other efficiently: Cuco is immune to most of its attacks, but the ghost keeps retreating into cover.

**Beats:**
- Cuco opens: *"They look... Like they're about to wither away. I can't even be asked to fight them."* (dismissive of lesser summoned creatures)
- Ghost enters statue and uses the construct as a shell
- Cuco blinds it, forces it to emerge partially, but the ghost re-hides
- Cuco deals cold damage — only to realize the ghost is fully immune to cold
- *"oh that's awesome.. well it shouldn't matter."*
- Ghost lands two hits for 37 and 38 damage (significant for Cuco's HP)
- Cuco casts Evard's Black Tentacles to pin the statue
- Ghost flees cover to a *different* statue; Cuco can't reach it
- Cuco: *"damnit... How do I kill this thing if it's going to keep hiding. I'm not sure this would be impossible. But it would take too long."*
- Final beat: *"Bleh. I yield. I don't have the time to play this hide and seek."*

**Why it works for comics:** The entire scene is played with Cuco's signature composure — and then punctured by a tactical dead-end he can't solve. The yield isn't defeat by force; it's a chess player flipping the board. The *"I yield"* panel (Cuco walking away while the ghost stares from its statue) would be the most character-defining Cuco moment in the dataset.
