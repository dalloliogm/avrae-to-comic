## Highlighted Scene 1: Cuco — "This Is for the Four Misfits"

**Setup:** Cuco (albino harengon chronurgy wizard, 12th dataset appearance) faces an enemy (FR1) alongside Vaz. Before casting, he addresses it:

**Beat:**
- *"Momo, Namaka'e. Kib. We four misfits tried to survive. Now all it would take is a flick of my wrist."*
- Avrae command title: *"This is for the four misfits. Hold!"* — he embedded the dedication into the spell command itself
- FR1 fails the save. Paralyzed.
- Double Chill Touch crits: command title *"Time is up, a touch of death!"*

**Why it's significant:** Cuco has spent 12 sessions being cold, efficient, and dismissive. He apologizes before fireballs, he calls enemies "annoyances," he yawns at challenges. This is the first time he names people who matter to him — Momo (unknown to this log), Namaka'e (recurring), Kib (recurring) — and frames an entire fight as being for them. The word *"misfits"* implies self-inclusion. He considers himself one of them.

The Avrae command title is especially striking: *"This is for the four misfits. Hold!"* — he wrote it into the mechanical action. That's the character bleeding into the game layer.

**After the kill:** *"Not sure if you needed to spend this much resources on this lowly creature but I'll be sure to not waste it, let's finish this quickly."* — immediately retreating to his usual register. The grief showed for one moment. Then it's gone.

**Why it works for comics:** The speech, the spell title, the double crits, the immediate deflection back to efficiency — this is a complete emotional arc in 4 panels. Widest tonal range Cuco has shown in the entire dataset.
