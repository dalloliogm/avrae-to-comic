## Highlighted Scene 1: Mal vs the Giant Sharks

**Setup:** Mal Neverflower is described in unusual detail — *"a leathery bill like a duck, webbed clawed paws like a lizard, fat tail like a beaver"* — a platypus-based locathah rogue. The shark encounter puts this visually distinctive character in underwater danger.

**Beats:**
1. Giant sharks appear; Mal faces them alone
2. Mal addresses the nearest shark: *"Uh… please die mr. Big angry sushi"* — establishing the character voice instantly
3. Sneak attack with a rogue's finesse; shark takes damage but doesn't go down
4. Shark retaliates — Mal dodges with a webbed tumble, bill cutting the water
5. Second shark closes in; Mal has to manage two targets with rogue's limited action economy
6. Mal delivers the killing blow and exhales
7. Coda: the water stills. Then a single quipper — one of the smallest fish in the game — swims up and bites off a finger. *Mal stares at the stump.*

**Why it works for comics:** The quipper coda is perfect comic timing. Mal's design (platypus bill, webbed claws, beaver tail fighting underwater) is inherently visual. The contrast between "monster that almost killed you" and "tiny fish that actually got you" earns the final panel.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Erien vs Stix

**Setup:** Erien Emberheart (unknown class) challenges Stix Wildly, a harengon (rabbit-person) monk, to a formal duel. Stix relies on unarmed strikes; Erien fights with ice magic and a sword.

**Beats:**
1. The duel opens with a respectful bow
2. Stix launches a rapid flurry — unarmed strikes land twice
3. Erien steps back and sweeps ice along the ground; Stix leaps over it
4. Stix commits to an aerial ice divekick — descending through the frost Erien just laid down, using it as a visual flourish
5. Erien parries the divekick with their sword crossguard and throws Stix to the ground
6. Stix goes unconscious from the throw
7. Erien immediately drops the sword and runs to Stix's side, checking their pulse: *"Are you okay? I didn't mean to—"*

**Why it works for comics:** The emotional turn at beat 7 recontextualizes the entire fight. Erien caring this much is the point of the scene, not the combat. Panel: Stix unconscious in soft snow, Erien kneeling in full sword gear looking genuinely distressed.
