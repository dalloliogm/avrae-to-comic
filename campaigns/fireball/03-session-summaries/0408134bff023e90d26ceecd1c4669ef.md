**Setup:** Half-orc ranger Lythra enters an arena and faces a bear wielding a banjo as its weapon. She narrates both sides of the combat herself — playing the bear via italicized text switches.

**Beats:**
- Lythra opens: *"What the hell is that!?"* then draws both shortswords
- She wins initiative: *"Lythra was just able to move faster than the bear. She closed the distance, then slashed at the bear with her swords!"* (hit)
- Bear's turn: *"But now it was the strange bear's chance to attack! The beast clutched its banjo, raised it above its head and smashed it down on the ranger!"* (miss — *"just bear-ly avoiding 'facing the music'"*)
- Round 2: bear hits for 9: *"The musical instrument slammed into her."*
- Lythra responds: *"Lythra spit the blood out of her mouth. She wouldn't lose to this bizarre bear!"*
- Extended back-and-forth: multiple misses on both sides, one more bear hit, multiple near-misses
- Lythra's internal: *"Take the hit and die!" she hissed through her tusks*
- Killing blow: *"Lythra jabbed her shortsword into its gut! The Arena Master called the fight. The Half-Orc had won again!"*
- Exit: *"She was quite proud of herself. With a grin, she left the arena."*

**Why it works for comics:** One player, one villain, six rounds of alternating narration — this is already a script. The bear's weapon is a banjo. The pun (*"bear-ly avoiding 'facing the music'"*) is in the source. The fight is a pure comedy-action loop with a satisfying ending. Zero setup required; the absurdity is self-contained.
