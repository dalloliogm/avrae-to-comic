## Highlighted Scene 1: Emery vs Bakari (Underground Phase)

**Setup:** The rematch of Anna's earlier spar. This time Emery Idris — a 5'11" dhampir sorcerer/warlock, white hair, black eyes, thin frame — goes underground.

**Beats:**
1. Combat begins normally; Bakari claws Emery
2. Emery pulls out a wand — *"He does that fucking trick all the time"* (Bakari, resigned)
3. Emery activates Umbral Form and sinks into the ground: *"You can't escape me..."*
4. From below the floor, Emery's voice: *"Bakari... you can't escape me..."*
5. Raulothim's Psychic Lance fires upward through stone — Bakari rolls intelligence, fails: 32 damage, incapacitated
6. Emery resurfaces, drinks a speed potion, casts haste on himself
7. Bakari from the ground: *"Emery! How about we end this here. Alright?"*
8. Emery, still floating in umbral smoke: *"Sure."*
9. Final exchange: *"It's annoying... because we both refuse to die."* He chuckles.

**Why it works for comics:** The underground attack is visually distinctive — a shadow sinking through stone, a voice from below. The dialogue at the end is genuinely good character writing. Both fighters acknowledge the futility with black humor rather than anger.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Vaz Dancing vs the Red Dragon

**Setup:** A red dragon appears while the party is already in a flooded bog. Vaz — a short halfling swords bard who moves through enemies and whales with kinetic jaunt — is now facing a dragon.

**Beats:**
1. Dragon descends with fire breath: everyone takes 71 damage, all fail saves (mass fail table — total party hit)
2. Party regroups; dragon uses Wing Attack, knocking Elysium prone
3. Dragon screeches: *"YOU KILL MY SPAWN I KILL YOUR SPAWN TOO, SAY GOOD BYE TO YOUR CHILD HUMAN!"*
4. Dragon swings two claws at Vaz — both miss
5. Narration: ***Vaz dances*** — *"He dances like there's no tomorrow dodging the dragon's attacks... like the both of them are in an intricate ballet... his heart is pounding and adrenaline is at max, once more Vaz finds himself tangoing with death!"*
6. Elysium (frightened, disadvantage) smites from the ground — prone + frightened but lands Eldritch Smite: 45 damage, dragon goes prone
7. The dragon's frightful presence hits Elysium and Fallira — Vaz saves
8. Vaz attacks twice; one hit, one miss — he misses but the narration says he doesn't care, he was dancing anyway

**Why it works for comics:** The dragon-ballet framing is explicitly written as choreography by the player. This is a rare FIREBALL moment where the player's IC voice creates a cinematic image. One spread panel: tiny halfling surrounded by dragon wings and claw arcs, feet in a dance step.
