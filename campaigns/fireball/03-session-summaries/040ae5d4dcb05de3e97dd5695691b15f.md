## Highlighted Scene 1: Hadae vs. Bibu Goggs

**Setup:** Diamondback moth samurai (Hadae) fights goblin druid (Bibu Goggs). Bibu is a friendly curious learner exiled by her tribe for causing an incident through magic. Hadae is pious and disciplined.

**Beats:**
- Bibu takes hits but stays delighted: *"Oooooh y'all got magic blades too?!"* (while being hit by a spiritual weapon)
- Hadae: *"Running will do you no good, I'm afraid."* (unfurls wings, flies forward) *"My god will carry me forth!"*
- Hadae lands Inflict Wounds for 23 damage; Bibu falls unconscious
- Hadae kneels and casts Cure Wounds immediately: *"I have proven my faithfulness once more. Now, you must stand. The ground is no place for a worthy opponent."*
- Follow-up: *"You fought exceptionally well. Had I not harnessed the power of my god, I would have fallen to your blows. Thank you for this spar, miss Bibu."*
- Closing: *"I'm glad to hear it. It was an honor fighting against you, I hope we will get to spar again sometime."*
- Hadae guides Bibu to a tavern to recover

**Why it works for comics:** The moth-samurai is a visually extraordinary design. The arc is complete: excitement → clash → defeat → immediate healing → mutual respect. Bibu's joy (getting hit while asking about magic swords) is an irresistible character note. The healing panel (*"The ground is no place for a worthy opponent"*) is a single strong image.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Casper vs. Orpheus

**Setup:** Sharkfolk samurai Casper (7'8") spars with Abyssal Elf rogue Orpheus. Casper opens aggressively; Orpheus lands katana hits for 12 damage. After Orpheus falls unconscious, Casper catches him and heals him.

**Beats:**
- Casper takes a hit: *"Is that it? Let me show you how to use a sword the right way."*
- Orpheus goes down; Casper catches him: *"Arenas aren't for napping."*
- Casper casts Cure Wounds
- Orpheus, revived: *"You need the practice. I will spar you one more time."*
- Casper: *"Perhaps you should fall into my arms more often, then."*
- Orpheus: *"Maybe I don't even need to fall."* (laughs, mostly to himself)
- Round 2: Casper misses; Orpheus: *"Focus, Orpheus. Your eyes fail you. I will show you how it's done."* (said by Casper, directed at Orpheus — a dig dressed as coaching)

**Why it works for comics:** The catch-and-heal is a strong visual pivot. The banter is genuinely flirtatious without being explicit — both characters understand what they're doing. The *"Maybe I don't even need to fall"* reply is confident and playful. Sharkfolk anatomy (7'8", shark features) makes Casper visually unmistakable.
