## Highlighted Scene 1: Kiriya's Ninja Reveal

**Setup:** Kiriya has been traveling with the party in disguise — a *"dirty brown cloak"* and an *"illusion"* concealing his nature. When werewolves attack, he drops the disguise.

**Beats:**
1. Werewolves emerge from the trees; party draws weapons
2. Kiriya sighs: *"Welp, so much for disguises."*
3. The brown illusion dissolves — cloak shifts to black; Kiriya is revealed as a ninja
4. He makes hand signs and shouts the jutsu name: *"Ninpo, Katon: Gōkakyū no Jutsu!"* — casts Fireball
5. The four werewolves take 17-35 fire damage (save split)
6. Seer (tabaxi warlock genie) watches. Pause. Then: *"Impressive. But let me show you my fire power!"*
7. Seer casts another Fireball at the same spot — all three remaining werewolves fail their save (26 each)
8. The wagon owner, Boi, emerges from hiding and begins clapping: *"Well done! Well done! I have chosen a fine group indeed."*

**Why it works for comics:** The double reveal (disguise drop → ninja → second character copying the move) is a classic escalation structure. Boi's applause is the comedic button. Visual sequence: disguise dissolving, hand signs, fireball, fireball again, golf clap from the merchant.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Ciara vs Grundle

**Setup:** Ciara (5'8" female satyr, wearing a bear-pelt hood as her Giant's Might totem) is challenged by Grundle, a frost giant, while his kin watch. The giants want a show.

**Beats:**
1. The giants form a semicircle, bears on leashes — *"they want a show"*
2. Ciara activates Giant's Might — she grows, bear pelt shadowing her face
3. She charges and immediately tackles Grundle to the ground (grapple succeeds)
4. She pins him, then starts headbutting with her ram horns: *crack crack*
5. Grundle swings his greataxe three times — misses all three, disadvantaged while prone
6. Giant crowd turns on him: *"Grundle! You weak fool! At least have your pride!"*
7. Grundle begs his kin to help; they refuse
8. Ciara continues methodically: ram, ram, ram — breaking his nose, splitting skin
9. Grundle collapses, beaten. The bear-headed satyr stands over him, blood on her horns.

**Why it works for comics:** The giant crowd watching their kinsman crumble is the frame for the whole scene. The satyr wearing a bear on her head is immediately visual. This scene has no magic — just physicality, pride, and a collapsing giant.
