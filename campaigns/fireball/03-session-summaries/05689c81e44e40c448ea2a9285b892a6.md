## Highlighted Scene 1: The Beast of Destruction

**Setup:** A colossal skeletal dragon bound by holy chains tears free from underground. Its body is covered in black tar that extinguishes fire and corrupts the earth on contact. Four adventurers face it in a barren wasteland. One player describes the environment and the beast; others play the party.

**Beats:**
- Beast emerges: *"wherever this substance touches the ground is stained black and the few plants that existed wilted instantly"*
- Robin Ilis (bladesong wizard): *"PUT HER DOWN, YOU GIANT SHIT!!"* — dashes 100ft hasted to reach the beast's grapple target
- Corrin's acid drake breath: discovers tar is vulnerable to acid despite fire immunity
- Anya "just kind of... explodes" (after Plane Shift attempt fails; she channels damage instead)
- Dharxes gives Robin Haste + Aura of Protection throughout; acts as support-anchor for the group
- Beast slams repeatedly; knocks party prone; retreats at low HP trailing burning tar
- Corrin pierces it with her holy lance: the beast roars as its insides glow red-hot
- Robin fires Crown of Stars at it from a flying mount

**Why it works for comics:** The visual concept (tar-dripping skeletal dragon vs acid-using adventurers) is strong. *"PUT HER DOWN"* from Robin as she yoinks herself across the battlefield is the personality moment. The acid vulnerability discovery (Corrin trying lightning, fire, then acid: *"Norn sends a wave of acid... to her surprise the beast's own tar doesn't seem resistant"*) gives the fight a tactical spine.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Cuco — Wereboar Dismissal (9th Appearance)

**Setup:** Cuco encounters 8 wereboars blocking his path to the mountains. He does not want to waste resources on them.

**Beats:**
- Cuco sees them transform: *"Bunch of pigs? Haah. I don't think I should bother... ahh-- lycanthropy? If you think numbers are going to beat me. You're in for another lesson."*
- Activates dragon form (Draconic Form ability)
- Wave 1: breath attack + Toll the Dead on most injured
- Flying back 60ft each round; never letting them reach melee
- Wave 2: another breath attack; all 8 fail saves
- Wave 3: finishes survivors; goes straight from this into a chamber entrance
- Cuco: *"What's up fuckers?"* [fireballs death dogs inside]
- After clearing both groups: *"How annoying. Wasting one of my valuable slots only because they were a bit high in numbers."*

**Why it works for comics:** Cuco solo against a mob is a recurring dataset pleasure, but this is the first time he's actually annoyed about having to use resources. He wins easily — the annoyance is purely about efficiency. The *"What's up fuckers?"* door entrance is a single panel. The fight structure (hover-breath-retreat × 3) is visually monotonous in a way that mirrors his attitude: this is not a fight, it is a task.

## Highlighted Scene 3: Snow + Araka + Phantom Wolves

**Setup:** Snow ❄ (Eladrin monk, katana) and Araka (Dhampir monk) fight a pair of Winter Wolves alongside Leesha and Crowre. The wolves phase into the ground when killed — leaving wounds real but enemies vanishing.

**Beats:**
- Leesha sends Snow a telepathic line during the fight: *"The pain is nothing, keep fighting!"* — Snow grits teeth, slashes
- Wolf bites Snow's leg; Snow shouts involuntarily, grinds it down
- Araka, seeing movement inside the cave: *"There seems to be movement within.... the girl! There's no time to be wasted."*
- Snow kills the wolf; it **phases into the ground** — the blood vanishes, the wounds stay
- Snow: *"They grit their teeth and hold their tears back. They've never been hurt this bad in their life, and it's terrifying."*

**Why it works for comics:** The phasing wolf death is a good visual horror moment — injuries real, enemy gone, no catharsis. Snow's fear response is unusually frank for this dataset.
