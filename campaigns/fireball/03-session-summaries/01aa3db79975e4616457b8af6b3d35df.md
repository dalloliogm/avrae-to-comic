## Highlighted Scene 1: The Alkilith Portal

**Setup:** The party is fighting off summoned hell creatures (HE1-HE3) when Cato realizes the "moss on the wall" is actually an Alkilith — a self-aware demon that tears holes between planes. It has already begun opening a permanent portal to the Abyss.

**Beats:**
1. Cato casts illusion: an army of Bearded Devils in formation marches through the far door, followed by a roaring Pit Fiend speaking Infernal
2. The hell creatures pivot — they now perceive devil enemies, not the party
3. Cato raises a fist to the party: *hold and let them pass*
4. As the confusion holds, Cato realizes the moving moss is a creature. Ally (Silly Stonne) whispers the Alkilith information into his ear — its origin, its danger, the portal
5. Cato shouts: *"Alkilith! That moss empowers the portal! Cut it down before it's permanent!"*
6. The Alkilith detaches from the wall, tentacles extending toward Crucible
7. Cato locks it in Mental Prison: *"Locked in the BRAIN CAGE"*
8. Party converges to kill the Alkilith before the portal stabilizes

**Why it works for comics:** The devil illusion walking through the door is a perfect misdirection panel — readers see the illusion alongside the monsters. Then the reveal of the actual threat on the ceiling. Cato's BRAIN CAGE line is the kind of player-generated caption that works verbatim.

**Setup:** This is less a dramatic scene and more a demonstration of scale. Alta (Barbarian 20) faces a custom hydra with 20 heads.

**Beats:**

2. The hydra bites Alta 17 times in one turn (consecutive Avrae rolls, each showing damage)

6. Nikki also survives. Player: *"Alta simps for you Nikki."*
7. Encounter ends with the title: *"------END QUEST lots and lots of crabs pt3 ----------"*

**Why it works for comics:** The 20-head hydra as a visual is inherently dramatic. The comedy is that it barely matters — the two level-20 characters are fine. The crab quest title suggests this is one chapter in an ongoing absurdist campaign.
