## Highlighted Scene 1: Cuco Kiting the Drakes

**Setup:** Cuco (albino harengon, Chronurgy wizard, described as *"the albino bunny"*) is solo underground against three Blue Drake Gems that he is treating as beneath him. He uses Expeditious Retreat and repeated Ray of Frost to kite them for approximately 15 real-time turns, narrating both his own actions and the drakes' internal monologue in the third person.

**Beats:**
1. Cuco activates Expeditious Retreat: *"Maybe I should use longstrider but freeze!"*
2. Drake 1 takes a ray of frost — slowed, falls behind. Drakes think: *"It will stumble, they will fall! Something will give."*
3. Eight turns of Cuco firing, dashing back 55-80 feet, firing again — the drakes literally cannot close the distance
4. One drake tries to go underground to escape; Cuco notes it doesn't have tremorsense: *"It was effectively blind underground! Why give them burrowing but no way to actually find their prey?"*
5. Cuco lets one drake get close on purpose — *"just to save time"* — baiting them in to group up
6. Drake bites Cuco: 5 damage. Cuco: *"This is going great... Probably off my game more than I had originally thought."*
7. Drake bites again: 7 damage. Cuco: *"To think my greedy nature would cause this."*
8. Cuco casts Haste — *"Having to burn a higher spell slot was just making him upset"*
9. Fireball: *"**Implosion.**"* — two drakes take 30 each
10. Cuco: *"Not even worth the haste... but at least I can quickly gather up your corpses."*

**Why it works for comics:** This is an extended chase sequence told entirely from one character's point of view, with the predator's internal monologue as counterpoint. The final "Implosion" panel — a fireball viewed from above the underground cavern, the albino rabbit standing at the far end — is a natural full-spread. The "greedy nature" moment (taking unnecessary damage because he wanted to kill them all at once) is perfect character writing.

## Highlighted Scene 2: MC Bonesalot and the Thriller Dance

**Setup:** The party has been fighting skeleton hordes that carry trumpets and emit thunder-typed sound attacks. Their squad commanders are called "Party Raisers." Then the boss appears: **Mc Bonesalot**, a 10-foot skeleton with the largest and fanciest trumpet.

**Beats:**
1. Mc Bonesalot activates **Thriller** — everyone except Percy (described as *"a fucking bore"*) is forced to do the Thriller dance in sync with zombies and skeletons
2. Hylga the Crucible dances her way over to the MC: *"gonna thriller my way over to that mc and crack some skulls"*
3. Mc Bonesalot does a jump spin, lands, shockwave: **Wave of Bones** — hits everyone nearby including fellow skeleton allies
4. Sercia Infearia goes unconscious. Her body continues Thriller dancing.
5. *"Sercia wakes up as they are mid dance"*
6. Inquisitus (amethyst dragon wizard, full name unprintable) fires chromatic orbs at the MC between dance steps
7. Hylga attacks the MC twice — hits both — while still in the dance
8. Glace Daveheart kills Mc Bonesalot with a longsword — deal 34 damage — mid-beat

**Why it works for comics:** The forced-Thriller premise is inherently visual: every character on the page is mid-moonwalk while also trying to fight. Sercia dancing while unconscious is a perfect sight gag. The whole fight is a parody that earns its comedy through commitment — the players never break the bit.
