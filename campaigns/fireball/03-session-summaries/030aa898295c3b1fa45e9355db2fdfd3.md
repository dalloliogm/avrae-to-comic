## Highlighted Scene 1: Teddy Gets His Moment

**Setup:** Teddy is a background NPC — a young guard attached to the party. He has been described as earnest and undertrained. During a chaotic fight, Teddy engages an enemy alone and wins.

**Beats:**
1. The main party is occupied; Teddy moves to flank a straggling enemy
2. Teddy's roll is narrated by the player running him — two attacks, one hit, one miss
3. The hit drops the enemy
4. Teddy turns to the party, wide-eyed: *"I GOT 'EM! The Sergeant's trainin' worked!"*
5. No one responds immediately. The fight is still ongoing elsewhere.
6. Teddy turns back to the combat. His body language is described as: *shoulders back, actually standing like a soldier now.*

**Why it works for comics:** The unreceived announcement (*"I GOT 'EM!"* into a room that isn't listening) is a classic comic timing beat — the celebration lands with the reader, not the characters. The posture change in the last panel is the emotional payoff without needing dialogue.

## Highlighted Scene 2: The Jester's Misfire

**Setup:** The Jester is a character using a flintlock pistol — unusual for the implied setting. On a critical fail, the gun misfires.

**Beats:**
1. The Jester announces his action: shoot at the enemy
2. The roll result is revealed — critical failure
3. The player types the narration very quickly: *"The gun kicks back — THE WRONG DIRECTION"*
4. The Jester shoots himself in the face
5. He is not dead — the damage is described as a graze. But he is very upset.
6. In-character: *"AAAAAA! Shit shit shit!"* — he drops the gun and clutches his face
7. An ally moves to help. The Jester waves them off: *"I'M FINE. I'M FINE. Don't — just — I'M FINE."*

**Why it works for comics:** Self-inflicted gun graze is pure slapstick. The denial sequence ("I'M FINE" repeated with escalating punctuation) is a textbook comic page — four panels of him insisting while clearly not fine. Zero tragedy, maximum dignity loss.

## Highlighted Scene 3: Mordred as Projectile

**Setup:** Zinogre (a Monster Hunter-style lightning wolf creature) uses a special attack — and the narration describes it grabbing Mordred and throwing him at another party member, Vanessa.

**Beats:**
1. Zinogre's turn — the creature rears back
2. Narration: it grabs Mordred (mid-combat, already in melee range) by the collar
3. Mordred is yeeted at Vanessa; attack roll treated as a ranged impact
4. Both Mordred and Vanessa take damage from the collision
5. Mordred, prone next to Vanessa: *"...I apologize."*
6. Vanessa: *"Don't."*

**Why it works for comics:** Yeeting a party member as a weapon is a physical comedy image. The formal apology while both are face-down on the ground is the beat that makes it land as character work rather than pure slapstick.
