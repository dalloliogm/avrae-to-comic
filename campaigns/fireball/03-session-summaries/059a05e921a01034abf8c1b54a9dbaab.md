## Highlighted Scene: The Oblex — "Your Name's on a Wall"

**Setup:** Wren (Eladrin sorcerer, chaos-sneezer), Aisling (Reborn cleric/sorcerer, tattoos that light up), and Amity Copperiggs (human artificer) are exploring a vault. They've been talking to a girl named "Sarah" who feels wrong — too familiar with Aisling, who has lost her memories. An Oblex (an ooze that eats memories and creates perfect copies of its victims) has been posing as her.

**Beats:**
- Wren uses Feywild Shard Metamagic — sneezes herself teleporting a few feet behind her position. *"I literally warned you, for this reason. I sneeze a lot okay?"*
- "Sarah" approaches Wren. A tendril of red ooze, nearly invisible, connects from a grate to the girl's foot
- Amity slips on Wren's Grease spell and falls; as she flies through the air, the tendril becomes visible. Everyone now knows what they're looking at
- Wren is charmed by the Oblex casting through its clone: *"Come here, I need help!"* — she walks down the hallway to help "Tizi"
- The door ajar: **cells.** Children's skeletons, 7+ bodies, in 5×5 cages with slits to slide food through. On the wall: a mural of hundreds of names on metal plaques, each with a number, most crossed out
- Wren's eyes drift to number 50: **~~Aisling~~** — etched, and crossed out
- The real Tizi kneels over a vat of "rolling and writhing eyeballs, mouths, limbs and flesh," hand on a lever, smiles as Wren approaches
- This sight snaps Wren out of the charm
- *"holy shit."* she mumbles. Then, through the telepathic link: `Not good! Your name's on a wall…`
- Lightning bolt. The clone explodes into ooze. The lever-Tizi folds into herself. Across the vault, a screech of a thousand voices — then silence

**Why it works for comics:**
This is a 10-page structure: arrive → meet Sarah (wrong feeling) → Amity grease-fall reveal → charm → walk the hallway alone → door ajar → children's cells → name on wall → snap → lightning bolt → silence. Every beat escalates. The horror is visual: the tendril revealed in silhouette as Amity flies, the cell-mural lit by candlelight, "Aisling" crossed out in a list of the dead. The final panel is Wren standing in silence while the ooze drains away.

The emotional weight is doubled for anyone who has read the prior sessions: Wren sneezing her hair out and getting carried by Aisling (batch 4), Wren/Aisling's existing warmth, and now — Aisling's name, written and struck through, on a dead-children list.
