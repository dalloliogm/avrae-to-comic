## Highlighted Scene 1: Archibald — The Oni Kite

**Setup:** Rogue/sorcerer Archibald Lloyd-Jones is alone in a market vs 4 Oni (large ogre-mage demons who regenerate). He has Haste, throwing daggers, and 120ft of movement per turn. They have Cone of Cold. Neither side can outright win: he can't deal enough damage before they heal, and they can't catch him.

**Structure (10 rounds):**
- Archi throws a dagger at the nearest Oni, retreats 60–120ft
- Oni advance 60ft, try to Cone of Cold if he's in range; he's not
- Archi throws another dagger, retreats again
- Haste runs out; one terrifying turn of vulnerability; then recasts
- The last Oni *"charges forward to meet the threat in the middle of the courtyard"* — finally
- Archi closes, draws his Flame Tongue rapier, kills it in melee

Closing line: *"After an absolute slog of a battle, Archi returns to the market. **Telling noone of the near death experience he has just had.**"*

**Why it works for comics:** This is a fight where nothing interesting happens repeatedly, which is the joke. Nine panels of identical composition: dagger thrown, Archi running, Oni advancing, nothing landing. Then one panel of him walking back into the market, face calm, sword sheathed. The closing caption does everything.
