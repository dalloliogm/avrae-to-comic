## Highlighted Scene 1: Noe Pavel vs Ivy

**Setup:** Noe Pavel is a reborn bard — grey skin, one ear missing, described as having the particular emotional flatness of someone who has already died once. Ivy is a snake sorceress: scales at her temples, long hair, openly aware of her own appeal. They enter combat with the energy of a first date that has gone immediately sideways.

**Beats:**
1. Ivy opens with a charm attempt and a line: *"You like the way I shake? Even though I'm a snake?"*
2. Noe is not charmed. His response is not to attack — he compliments her technique and asks where she studied
3. Ivy is briefly thrown. She attacks. He deflects. He is still talking.
4. Three exchanges of escalating banter while they fight — Noe treats each of her attacks as conversational material
5. **Turning point:** Noe steps inside her guard and hugs her — a full, committed hug, mid-combat
6. He has positioned both of them inside his own Cloud of Daggers. Both take damage.
7. Ivy, pulling back: *"That was either very stupid or very brave."* Noe, bleeding: *"One of those, yes."*
8. Ivy poisons him — described as a kiss. *"I don't think I like my first kiss that much."*
9. Noe collapses. Poisoned, not dead.
10. Ivy looks at him for a moment. She picks him up.
11. She carries him to a nearby oasis. She sits. She waits for him to wake up.
12. The sub-encounter ends there. No dialogue. She is just there.

**Why it works for comics:** The Noe/Ivy arc is the single most complete story in the FIREBALL dataset reviewed so far. It has a recognizable romantic comedy structure (antagonism → unexpected tenderness → physical comedy of the hug-into-daggers → reversal → quiet coda) and every beat is legible without external context. The poison kiss panel and the silent oasis final panel are strong enough to be the last two pages of a self-contained short. The Zalgo-text session (`0149d30b`) is technically more visually striking, but this one is emotionally complete.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Celestia vs Grandle

**Setup:** Celestia is a siren bard; her primary combat mechanism is her voice. Grandle is a lizardfolk who appears physically overmatched but proves more thoughtful than expected.

**Beats:**
1. Celestia uses her voice to disorient Grandle — described as a high sustained note that disrupts concentration
2. Grandle covers his ears and fights by feel — surprisingly effective
3. Mid-fight, Grandle: *"You sing to hurt. That seems wasteful."*
4. Celestia pauses — the first moment she has stopped performing
5. The fight continues but the dynamic has shifted; Grandle is less an enemy and more an audience
6. Resolution is unspecified in the log — the sub-encounter transitions away

**Why it works for comics:** "You sing to hurt. That seems wasteful." is an eight-word character reversal. The pause panel — Celestia mid-note, genuinely stopped by a lizardfolk's observation — is strong. This would work well as a B-story in the same issue as Noe/Ivy.
