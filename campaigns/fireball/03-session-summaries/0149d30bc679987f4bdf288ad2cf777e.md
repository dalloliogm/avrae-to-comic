## Highlighted Scene 1: Stonefire vs the Revenant

**Setup:** Stonefire, a goliath monk, faces a Revenant alone in a dungeon corridor. The Revenant regenerates each turn and speaks exclusively in Zalgo corruption text — `AnD yOu ShOuLd HaVe Run` — signaling it as fundamentally wrong, something that should not be here.

**Beats:**
1. Stonefire enters the corridor; the Revenant steps from shadow
2. The Revenant's speech appears corrupted: *"AnD yOuR fLeShY mOrTaL bOnEs WiLl BrEaK"* — player uses alt-caps intentionally
3. Stonefire attacks — solid damage. The Revenant's wounds close at the start of its next turn.
4. Second exchange: the Revenant crits Stonefire, dropping them to low HP
5. The Revenant leans over Stonefire, raising its claw: *"YoUr SoUl WiLl Be DeLiCiOuS, aNd YoUr BoDy ShAlL bE UsEd ApPr-"*
6. Mid-word, a fireball detonates in the corridor from further down the hall
7. The Revenant's body ignites and chars into silence
8. Silence. Then footsteps. Lloyd (cloaked, silver glowing eyes revealed as his hood falls back): *"Are you alright?"*

**Why it works for comics:** This is structured like a horror-rescue beat: monster speech mid-word → interrupted by fire → dramatic reveal. Lloyd's silver eyes glowing in the smoke is a strong closing panel. The Zalgo text communicates monstrousness without any additional description needed — readers have that visual vocabulary.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Vlita the Rooster's Breakdown

**Setup:** Vlita is described as a roosted (bird-type) barbarian. In this encounter his rage breaks past the normal game mechanic into something messier — froth at the beak, literal flames from the eyes.

**Beats:**
1. Vlita enters combat at low HP from a prior fight
2. He rages — but the narration describes it physically: *froth forming at the corner of his beak, eyes beginning to flicker with heat*
3. His attacks land, but he's clearly destabilizing — attack rolls with reckless abandon
4. A player notes: he's going to hurt himself if this continues
5. Vlita crits on an enemy and roars — the narration escalates: *flames leak from his eye sockets now, not magical, something else*
6. He kills the last enemy and immediately drops to one knee, the rage leaving him all at once
7. Party member moves to stabilize him. Vlita is shaking.

**Why it works for comics:** The visual escalation (froth → heat flicker → actual eye flames) maps perfectly to a comic page. Each panel shows the dial moving further. The abrupt collapse after the final kill is the emotional reset.
