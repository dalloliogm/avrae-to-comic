## Highlighted Scene 1: The Train Fight

**Setup:** The party is fighting werewolves on a moving train. The train is breaking apart mid-fight and is "second from going over a cliff." Mechanics include: opening train-car doors costs a full action, combatants are considered in melee for ranged attacks, and characters can be kicked out through the open doors.

**Beats:**
1. Niklaus Zharkov (dhampir deathknight) fights Geezer, a werewolf, in close quarters
2. Geezer tries to drag Klaus toward the open door at the end of the car
3. Kotl' Khan (sorcerer) calls to Tommy the werewolf: *"Tommy, this isn't you, man!"* — then casts Magic Missile on him anyway
4. Tommy bites Kriyra (goblin rogue) — con save fails: Kriyra gains **Werewolf Lycanthropy**
5. Beatrix Salvatore (sorcerer) fires Chromatic Orb at Tommy — the car begins to break apart
6. Klaus is hanging on the edge of a disintegrating car
7. Kotl' narrates: *"The train is second from going over a cliff."*
8. The train goes over. Combat ends mid-fall (no resolution shown — session moves on)

**Why it works for comics:** A moving train fight is a classic action setpiece rarely seen in play-by-post. The mid-combat lycanthropy is an excellent story beat — Kriyra didn't ask for this. The unresolved cliff-drop ending is a natural cliffhanger (literally).

## Highlighted Scene 2: Knight of Night and Knight of Light

**Setup:** Two level-20 half-elf paladins — Knight of Night (darkness theme) and Knight of Light (radiant theme) — fight the party together. They are established as soul-bonded: when one falls, the other regenerates. The duel is not winnable by attrition.

**Beats:**
1. Knight of Light strikes Mallena for 54 then 31 damage (crits then hits)
2. Hakim ibn Al-Udai (tabaxi rogue, level 20) calmly shoots Knight of Night repeatedly: *"please just die... I'd rather be sleeping right now"*
3. Knight of Night falls — Knight of Light smirks: *"You're back, other half of my soul."*
4. Knight of Night regenerates
5. Hakim shoots Knight of Night again, crit for 49 damage
6. Again: *"*sigh* please just die"*
7. Hakim shoots Knight of Light for 43, then Night for 28 in the same turn (two targets)
8. As one falls: *"Night Night"* — Hakim says under his breath

**Why it works for comics:** The soul-bonded regeneration mechanic creates a Sisyphean fight. Hakim's exhausted professionalism (*"I'd rather be sleeping"*) is the comedic counterpoint to the paladins' dramatic declarations. The "Night Night" pun on the final knockout is a perfect closing panel caption.
