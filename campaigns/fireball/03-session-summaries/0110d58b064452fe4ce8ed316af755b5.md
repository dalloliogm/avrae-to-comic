## Highlighted Scene 1: The Kobold Farm

**Setup:** The party arrives at what appears to be an agricultural operation. The kobold laborers are enslaved by an off-screen slaver faction. When combat triggers, the kobolds are forced to attack — but they don't want to fight.

**Beats:**
1. Party enters; kobolds are working, heads down
2. Slavers off-panel order the kobolds to attack the adventurers
3. One kobold, in its attack turn, shouts: *"I'm SORRYYY!!! Please, just lay down your weapons. They have my baby!"*
4. Another kobold moves to comply — but the slaver mechanic forces the attack roll anyway
5. Gnaw (7'3" gnoll cleric, green eyes) kills one of the slavers and stands atop the corpse, blood-soaked, grinning at the party
6. The kobolds stop fighting the moment the slaver dies; one immediately runs to find the baby

**Why it works for comics:** This scene does something rare — it makes the monsters sympathetic within a combat frame. The kobold screaming mid-attack is a perfect single-panel gut-punch. Gnaw's grinning silhouette atop the corpse is a strong visual beat immediately after.

## Highlighted Scene 2: Fallira vs Tsagarthe

**Setup:** Fallira (Shadar-kai drakewarden ranger) and Tsagarthe (leonin) are established rivals. The duel is formally declared.

**Beats:**
1. Both take position — Fallira's spectral fading ability gives her an initial advantage
2. Tsagarthe moves in with lion's grace; Fallira blinks 30 ft sideways mid-swing
3. Tsagarthe gets a solid hit; Fallira's Shadar-kai heritage flares — she becomes ghostly, wreathed in mist, and teleports behind him
4. Tsagarthe recovers; the duel appears even until—
5. Tsagarthe opens his mouth and a bolt of lightning emerges — his heritage had not been disclosed yet. First revelation.
6. Fallira takes the hit, tumbles, stands: *"I didn't know you were hiding that."*
7. Duel ends without a clear winner; both acknowledge the other

**Why it works for comics:** The lightning reveal at beat 5 is a classic manga-style hidden-ability moment. The spectral blinking choreography reads well in sequential panels.
