# Weave Gate Origin Gem Dominion Comic Script: Page 5

## Page Goal

Show the immediate consequence of Dominion's Arcane Cataclysm: active magic is repressed. Dan vanishes from Don's side, summoned creatures wink out or collapse into nothing, and the Defenders' usual tools stop behaving normally while Dominion remains dangerous inside its own dead zone. The reader should understand the new tactical problem before Don chooses a physical solution later.

Use recap captions to explain antimagic visually and simply.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 shows magical creatures and effects being repressed by the blast.
- Panel 2 shows Don reacting as Dan disappears.
- Panel 3 shows Dominion standing untouched inside the dead zone.
- Panel 4 shows Tobias and Riknar testing physical/nonmagical pressure.
- Panel 5 focuses on Don realizing magic is no longer the answer.

## Panel 1

Wide aftermath panel. Ancient ruins are scarred by several dead gray circles where magic has collapsed. The active magic on the battlefield is being repressed all at once: summoned creatures become transparent silhouettes and vanish, spectral weapons break into fragments, protective lights snap off, and spell effects stop abruptly at the gray circles. Defenders are scattered around the battlefield, suddenly deprived of the magical army around them.

**Caption:** The blast did not just hurt. It emptied the air.

**Caption:** Summons vanished. Wards failed. The battlefield lost half its bodies in a breath.

**Art Notes:** This should explain antimagic without rules text. Make the disappearance of magical creatures the clearest visual consequence.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic thumbnail, ancient forest ruins after arcane blast, dead gray antimagic circles, summoned creatures turning transparent and vanishing, magical weapons and wards breaking apart, scattered fantasy defenders, simple grayscale with blue-white crackle accents.

## Panel 2

Medium close panel on Don as Dan disappears beside him. Don reaches toward the space where his icy orc simulacrum stood. Dan's blue-white body is half-gone, dissolving into jagged frost motes and blank air. Don's expression should mix shock, fear, and forced calculation.

**Caption:** Don watched Dan disappear from the field.

**Caption:** Destroyed? Banished? Suppressed? There was no time to know.

**Art Notes:** Dan must still read as an icy orc duplicate of Don in the parts that remain: tusks, broad orc face, frost beard, blue-white body. This is the emotional consequence of the antimagic blast.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic panel, old green orc wizard reaching toward icy blue orc simulacrum duplicate as it vanishes into frost motes in gray antimagic field, shocked expression, simple thumbnail.

## Panel 3

Medium panel. Dominion steps into one of the gray antimagic circles. Everything around it is muted and drained, but its flaming skull and red-black aura still burn. It looks pleased with the unfairness of this.

**Caption:** Dominion, of course, had written itself an exception.

**Dominion:** And that master is me.

**Art Notes:** Dominion's continued flame inside antimagic must look wrong and threatening.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic panel, black-red armored flaming skull tyrant standing inside gray antimagic field while its flames still burn, smug threatening pose, black speech balloon.

## Panel 4

Split action panel or two-beat panel. On one side, Tobias, goblin gunslinger in cowboy hat, fires into the dead zone and bullets visibly disappear into Dominion's void-dark body. On the other side, Riknar, scarred armored warrior, closes into melee with Dominion at the edge of the antimagic field.

**Caption:** Steel, bullets, and muscle were suddenly worth testing.

**Tobias:** Difference between me and you is I don't need magic to function.

**Art Notes:** Keep this physical and grounded. Tobias should look competent. Riknar's pressure foreshadows that moving Dominion by force is possible.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic split action panel, goblin cowboy gunslinger firing revolver at flaming skull void tyrant as bullets vanish, scarred armored warrior closing to melee at edge of gray antimagic field, simple high contrast silhouettes.

## Panel 5

Medium close panel on Don. He stands at the edge of the dead field, old orc face lit by drained gray on one side and remaining spell-light on the other. His hand tightens around his staff, but his expression shifts from grief and alarm to calculation. Empty space beside him marks where Dan used to be.

**Caption:** Don had survived enough Gates to recognize a losing pattern.

**Caption:** This one would not be solved by summoning more help or making a bigger explosion.

**Art Notes:** This is the page's emotional pivot. Don has not dropped the staff yet, but the idea has begun.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic close panel, old green orc wizard with tusks white beard and conical hat at edge of antimagic field, empty space where icy simulacrum vanished, half-lit by gray dead magic and half by spell glow, grief turning into calculation.

## Lettering Notes

- Use concise recap captions; avoid dense exposition.
- Dominion uses black speech balloons with pale lettering.
- Tobias's line can be a normal speech balloon.
- Don has no dialogue on this page.

## Continuity Checks

- The page follows Dominion's Arcane Cataclysm from Page 4.
- Antimagic fields suppress nearby magic but not Dominion's flames or aura.
- Dan disappears from the battlefield after the antimagic blast; his fate remains unresolved.
- Summoned creatures and active magical effects vanish, flicker out, or are suppressed by the antimagic fields.
- Tobias's bullets connect but are absorbed or fail to meaningfully tear through Dominion.
- Riknar's close-quarters pressure matters.
- Don begins to understand that ordinary spell escalation will not solve the problem.
