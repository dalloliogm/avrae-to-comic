# Weave Gate Origin Gem Dominion Comic Script: Page 3

## Page Goal

Deliver the mission threat. Page 2 explains why Don and Dan arrive; Page 3 should show that they are already too late. Dormo's ritual has succeeded, Dominion enters, and Don undercuts the horror with veteran sarcasm.

Use recap captions to keep the reader oriented, but let the Dominion reveal dominate the page.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 establishes the overgrown ruin and ritual.
- Panel 2 shows Dormo's moment of triumph.
- Panel 3 is the Dominion reveal splash.
- Panel 4 shows the Defenders arriving and reacting.
- Panel 5 lands Don and Dan's style joke.

## Panel 1

Wide establishing panel. Dense overgrown forest clearing with ivy-covered stone pillars, cracked ancient masonry, and three ritual figures near the far side. The Defenders emerge from the Gate in the foreground.

**Caption:** The trail ended in an ancient sanctum, already split open by ritual magic.

**Art Notes:** Keep the ritual figures readable as silhouettes. The panel should show distance and the sense that the Defenders have arrived at the last possible second.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic thumbnail, overgrown ancient forest ruin, ivy-covered stone pillars, ritual silhouettes in distance, defenders arriving through portal, simple grayscale with one green magic accent.

## Panel 2

Medium panel on Dormo turning toward the Defenders. He wears or holds a clockwork amulet glowing with green energy. Behind him, the air tears open into a dark void.

**Caption:** Dormo, fallen Brother and seeker of the Negative Energy Plane, had already finished what he came to do.

**Dormo:** You're too late!

**Art Notes:** Dormo does not need a final portrait yet. Design him as a corrupted monk or ritualist, visually connected to Bjorn but more frantic and severe.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic panel of corrupted monk ritualist with glowing green clockwork amulet, turning triumphantly, dark void tear opening behind him.

## Panel 3

Large splash panel. Dominion steps through the torn void: towering figure in black scaled armor, red cape, black gauntlets and boots, pitch-black staff, flaming skull head. The void should break the shape of the panel slightly or appear to eat the background.

Defenders are small at the bottom edge, showing Dominion's scale.

**Caption:** And something answered.

**Dominion:** This world will be consumed.

**Art Notes:** This is the horror image of the page. Dominion's black speech balloon can intrude over the panel border. Keep the design simple but iconic.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic splash panel, towering black-and-red armored void entity with flaming skull stepping through torn reality, small heroes below, simple grayscale with red flame accent, black speech balloon.

## Panel 4

Reaction strip. Defenders take positions: Morana unimpressed, Tobias readying a gun, Riknar bracing to charge, Alistaire and Aeristail as harengon mages arriving with too much enthusiasm. Don and Dan appear at the edge, late but calm. Dan must read as an icy orc duplicate of Don: orc face, tusks, same facial structure, blue-white frost body, not human or elf.

**Caption:** The Gates had sent veterans, prodigies, gunslingers, riders, and at least one person who looked far too cheerful about the whole thing.

**Art Notes:** Do not crowd every character with detail. Use silhouettes and one identifying feature per character.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic reaction strip of fantasy defenders facing void monster: white-haired spellcaster, goblin cowboy gunslinger, armored rider, harengon mage, old orc wizard and icy simulacrum, simple staging.

## Panel 5

Medium comedy beat on Don and Dan looking at Dominion. Don studies the black-and-red cape and staff like a disappointed fashion critic. Dan stands beside him, icy and thoughtful. Dan must keep Don's orc traits: tusks, broad orc face, similar beard/hat silhouette if visible, rendered as blue ice.

**Caption:** Don Explodicus, semi-retired evoker and unwilling fashion critic, identified the first tactical problem.

**Don:** Black cape, black staff, red trim. Unoriginal, but at least he has style.

**Dan:** I don't like the black. Blue and red would suit us better.

**Art Notes:** This should not make Don look unserious about danger. He is a veteran coping with apocalypse through dry commentary.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic panel of old green orc wizard and icy blue simulacrum looking at a distant black-red void lord, dry comedy, simple grayscale thumbnail with blue and red accents.

## Lettering Notes

- Dominion uses black speech balloons with pale lettering.
- Dormo can use normal speech balloons, slightly jagged if desired.
- Don and Dan dialogue should be readable and short.
- Captions should recap enough context that a reader understands who Dormo and Dominion are without prior campaign knowledge.

## Continuity Checks

- The Defenders arrive after Dormo completes the ritual.
- Dormo has the clockwork amulet glowing green.
- Dominion's first visual is black-and-red armor, black staff, red cape, flaming skull.
- Don and Dan are present together before combat begins; Dan is an icy orc simulacrum of Don, not a human-looking ice mage.
- Don's joke is about Dominion's black-and-red style and sets up the later retirement style conversation.
