# Weave Gate Origin Gem Dominion Comic Script: Page 0

## Page Goal

Introduce The Gates as a setting before the cold-open crisis on Page 1. A new reader should understand that the Gates are not ordinary portals: they are a cosmic defensive system, held in a pocket dimension, watched by Gatekeepers, and answered by Defenders when evil presses through.

This page should be atmospheric and explanatory, not character-driven. It should make Page 1's line "Today, Weave Gate nearly failed" feel ominous.

## Reader Orientation

- Characters: no named player characters need to appear. Defenders can be shown as small silhouettes.
- Scene context: The Gates protect the multiverse from evil dimensions.
- Location: a pocket dimension centered around the original mountain and cave.
- Stakes: if a Gate fails, evil breaks through into the wider multiverse.
- Required prior knowledge: none.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is a large establishing image of the Gates complex.
- Panel 2 shows the origin myth of Itrendor and the cave.
- Panel 3 shows the Gates as a dam holding back hostile dimensions.
- Panel 4 shows Gatekeepers and Defenders as the living response system.
- Panel 5 ends on Weave Gate beginning to flare.

## Panel 1

Large establishing panel. A mountain and cave float at the center of a pocket dimension. Around them stand many monumental Gates, each with a different visual identity: water, moonlight, crystal, flame, storm, psychic haze, woven arcane threads. The Hall of Arrivals can be hinted at as a central platform or structure, with small Defender silhouettes moving through it.

**Caption:** The Gates stand where the multiverse is thinnest.

**Caption:** Not doors. Defenses.

**Art Notes:** Keep this iconic and legible. The viewer should immediately understand "many Gates, one central complex, cosmic scale."

**Prompt Note:** Rough no-text comic thumbnail, pocket dimension with ancient mountain and cave at center, many monumental magical Gates around it, central arrival hall, cosmic defensive atmosphere, limited palette, no text or boxes.

## Panel 2

Mythic flashback panel. Itrendor the Everkeeper is shown as a small but luminous arcane figure escaping or standing before the original cave that leads toward a dark hostile dimension. His life force is visually connected to glowing Gate-structures forming around the mountain.

**Caption:** Itrendor the Everkeeper found the way through.

**Caption:** Then he gave his life to hold it shut.

**Art Notes:** This is legend, not literal documentary. Keep Itrendor symbolic unless later lore gives a firm design.

**Prompt Note:** Rough no-text comic panel, legendary arcane figure before dark cave, life force flowing into magical gate structures, mythic fantasy iconography, no text.

## Panel 3

Diagrammatic but dramatic panel. The Gates act like a dam. Behind them, abstract hostile dimensions press forward: claws, shadow armies, void fire, monstrous silhouettes, warped landscapes. On the safe side, the multiverse is represented by small lights or floating worlds.

**Caption:** Evil presses against them like floodwater.

**Caption:** Every mission is a leak before it becomes a breach.

**Art Notes:** This can be symbolic. The goal is to explain the campaign model visually.

**Prompt Note:** Rough no-text comic panel, magical gates as a dam holding back abstract evil dimensions, monstrous silhouettes and void fire pressing from one side, small worlds protected on the other, no text.

## Panel 4

Operational panel. Gatekeepers stand near different Gates, reading their behavior. Defenders answer calls: warriors, spellcasters, riders, gunslingers, and odd silhouettes stepping into portals. This should feel like a living emergency system.

**Caption:** Gatekeepers watch. Defenders answer.

**Caption:** One call. One fight. No time for certainty.

**Art Notes:** Do not over-render individual characters. Use diverse silhouettes to imply many campaigns and missions.

**Prompt Note:** Rough no-text comic panel, gatekeepers studying magical gates while diverse fantasy defenders step into mission portals, emergency response mood, no text.

## Panel 5

Final ominous panel. Weave Gate flares differently from the others: arcane threads twist, snap, and knot into a bright portal. The other Gates recede into shadow. The color should shift toward blue, violet, white, and dangerous black gaps.

**Caption:** Then Weave Gate rang.

**Caption:** And magic itself became the battlefield.

**Art Notes:** This panel should connect directly to the Weave Gate mission without revealing Dominion yet.

**Prompt Note:** Rough no-text comic panel, Weave Gate flaring with blue violet white arcane threads snapping and knotting, other gates receding into shadow, ominous setup, no text.

## First-Version Style

- Limited palette: black, off-white, gate-blue, violet, and one warm gold accent.
- Graphic treatment: rough thumbnail with strong silhouettes and simple symbolic panels.
- No-text art constraints: generate without captions, speech bubbles, SFX, placeholder boxes, or fake text.
- Lettering: add captions afterward with `scripts/letter_comic_page.py`.

## Lettering Notes

- Page 0 should use only captions, no dialogue.
- Captions should read like a short mythic prologue, not a lore dump.
- Keep sentence length short enough for large readable boxes.
- If text feels crowded, remove Panel 2's second caption or Panel 4's second caption first.

## Continuity Checks

- The Gates complex is in a pocket dimension centered around the original mountain and cave.
- The Gates defend against evil dimensions.
- Itrendor's life force and soul are bound into the Gates.
- Gatekeepers interpret Gate behavior.
- Defenders answer mission portals.
- Weave Gate is the specific Gate leading into this issue.
