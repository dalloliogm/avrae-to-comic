## Adaptation Approach

Treat this as a Don-centered single issue, not a full transcript adaptation. Use a cold open from the climactic grapple to hook the reader, then rewind to the polite bell and mission briefing. The dramatic question is: what does Don do when magic, his defining strength, stops being enough?

The emotional turn is Don going from semi-retired confidence and joking with Dan to choosing a physical, painful, very un-wizardly solution. The core visual contrast should be:

- Don and Dan as relaxed veteran spellcasters with style jokes and pre-battle banter.
- Dominion as an over-designed void tyrant whose magic dominates the battlefield.
- The antimagic fields stripping away the Defenders' usual tools.
- Don dropping his staff and choosing brute effort because there is no elegant spell left.

Invented connective tissue should stay minimal. Use the log's own trauma reflection as Don's interior caption sequence during the grapple.

## Issue Shape

- Format: 9-page short comic.
- Point of view: primarily Don, with splash moments for Dominion and the final Meteor Swarm.
- Tone: high-level D&D spectacle with dry comedy at the edges.
- Visual motif: the Weave as bright geometry and threadlike light; Dominion's void as negative space that eats panel borders.

## Cold Open

Start with a half-page or full-page teaser from the climax before the chronological story begins:

- Don has no spell glow around him.
- His staff lies on the cracked stone behind him.
- Dominion towers over him, black staff raised, flaming skull turned in disbelief.
- Don's hand is locked around Dominion's armor as he drags the entity across the edge of an antimagic field.
- The surrounding magic is dead, gray, and crackling, while the world outside the field burns with waiting spells.

Caption option:

> When the Weave went silent, Don Explodicus had one spell left.

Then cut to:

> Earlier.

This should not spoil the ending completely. It should show Don in impossible danger and make the reader ask how a retired orc wizard ended up wrestling a void tyrant by hand.

## Page Beats

### Page 1: No Spell Left

1. Half-page cold open: Don drags Dominion out of the antimagic field, staff abandoned behind him, no spell glow visible.
2. Close panel: Dominion's flaming skull turns toward Don in disbelief and rage.
3. Close panel: Don's hand grips the black-and-red armor; his expression is pain, stubbornness, and old anger.
4. Caption panel: "When the Weave went silent, Don Explodicus had one spell left."
5. Hard cut panel: a tiny polite bell rings behind Don. Caption: "Earlier."

### Page 2: The Polite Bell

1. Small panel: a quiet moment interrupted by a polite bell behind Don.
2. Wide panel: the portal appears with a tiny bell and a note: "Urgent Matter. Weave Gate. Come at once. Don't step off the Skimmer."
3. Tall panel: Don and Dan on the Skimmer, relaxed but wary, crossing a dark void.
4. Wide panel: Alivia's wizard study, Origin Gems glowing on the table.
5. Close panel: Don notices the gems and the worry on Alivia and Bjorn.

### Page 3: Dominion Arrives

1. Establishing panel: overgrown forest clearing, ancient ivy-covered pillars, ritual figures ahead.
2. Medium panel: Dormo turns, clockwork amulet glowing green.
3. Splash panel: Dominion steps through the torn void in black and red armor, flaming skull exposed.
4. Reaction strip: Defenders take positions; Don and Dan enter late and relaxed.
5. Comedy beat: Don critiques Dominion's black-and-red style while Dan proposes blue and red for retirement.

### Page 4: Veteran Wizards

1. Action panel: Dan in dragon form tears away Dominion's protective wards with Dispel Magic.
2. Action panel: Don raises radiant power and detonates Sunburst across the battlefield.
3. Small panels: acolytes blinded; Dominion barely affected; Don's Crown of Stars flickers.
4. Wide panel: Dominion looks insulted, arcane orbs gathering above its head.
5. Full-width bottom panel: Arcane Cataclysm erupts, leaving antimagic dead zones across the ruins.

### Page 5: Magic Fails

1. Silent panel: residual antimagic fields distort the scene; summoned beings and effects falter.
2. Panel: Dominion steps into a dead zone, still burning and untouched by its own antimagic.
3. Panel: Tobias fires, bullets disappearing into Dominion's void body.
4. Panel: Riknar struggles in close quarters while Dominion mocks the Defenders.
5. Panel: Don's expression changes from annoyed to calculating.

### Page 6: The Enemy Vanishes And Returns

1. Panel: an acolyte rescues Dominion with Plane Shift, leaving sudden silence.
2. Panel: Don heals himself and raises Sanctuary, irritated that the fight paused at the best part.
3. Wide panel: a black Gate opens inside an antimagic zone.
4. Splash panel: Dominion returns furious, "THIS. ENDS. NOW!"
5. Bottom panel: Defenders around the battlefield realize the same problem: someone must move Dominion.

### Page 7: Don Drops The Staff

1. Close panel: Don's hand opens; the Staff of Conflux falls to the ground.
2. Tall panel: the old orc wizard runs into the antimagic field, suddenly without his usual light and effects.
3. Flashback inset: a young orc's burned legs and arm, shown symbolically rather than graphically.
4. Caption sequence from Don's memory: the old injury did not seal his destiny; it pushed him toward mental force.
5. Large panel: Don grapples Dominion, teeth clenched, using the weak hand anyway.

### Page 8: Out Of The Dead Zone

1. Panel: Dominion hammers Don with the black staff.
2. Panel: Don's arcane Shield flares just long enough to keep him upright.
3. Wide panel: Don drags Dominion out of the antimagic field by force.
4. Panel: Alistaire's readied magic strikes as soon as Dominion is vulnerable.
5. Panel: Dominion panics and starts Power Word Kill.
6. Panel: Morana cuts the spell with one word: "Cease!"

### Page 9: The First Meteor Swarm

1. Splash panel: thunderous Meteor Swarm drops beside Don and obliterates Dominion.
2. Small silent panel: smoke, dust, and dead quiet forest.
3. Panel: Alivia and Bjorn arrive through a Gate that flickers around the antimagic residue.
4. Panel: Bjorn retrieves Dormo's amulet and thanks the Defenders.
5. Panel: Don cleans soot from his robes with Prestidigitation and picks up his staff.
6. Final panel: Don looks back toward the empty battlefield, hoping Dan will return. Dialogue: "He will be surprised to hear of my feat of strength, today."

## Dialogue And Captions

- Keep Don's early dialogue short and comic: the retirement-style critique of Dominion's look is a strong opener.
- Use Dominion's telepathic declarations as caption-like black speech balloons that intrude across panel borders.
- Use Don's trauma reflection as captions only on Page 6. Avoid expanding the backstory beyond what the log says.
- Keep the final Don line nearly verbatim, because it lands as both pride and unresolved worry about Dan.

## Visual Continuity Notes

- Don: green orc wizard, white beard, conical wizard hat, explosive and firelit palette, mischievous grin.
- Dan: icy blue simulacrum of Don, same facial structure, frost-covered duplicate, visually distinct from Don.
- Morana: pale/white-haired spellcaster, dark elegant clothing, celestial golden magic, skull imagery.
- Tobias: goblin or goblinoid gunslinger/cowboy, brown hat, red shirt, pointed ears, tusks, sharp grin.
- Riknar: scarred middle-aged human warrior, dark hair with gray, gray beard, fur-collared armor.
- Alistaire Zaprabbit: armored harengon mage, blue-green arcane glow, ornate collar and chest focus. The supplied portrait is Alistaire, not Aeristail.
- Dominion: black and red scaled armor, matching cape, black gauntlets and boots, pitch-black staff, flaming skull head.
- Weave Gate study: ornate arcane workspace with glowing liquids, crystal balls, animated pen, and blue/yellow Origin Gems.
- Battlefield: dense forest clearing, ivy-covered ancient stone pillars, green ritual flash, torn void.
- Antimagic fields: show as drained, crackling dead circles where effects desaturate or fragment.

## Key Continuity Constraints

- Dormo completes the ritual before the Defenders can stop it.
- Tobias kills Dormo early; the real threat is Dominion.
- Dan dispels Dominion's Foresight and Shield before Don's Sunburst.
- Dominion's Arcane Cataclysm creates antimagic fields that do not suppress Dominion's own aura.
- Don's major contribution is physical: he grapples Dominion and drags it out of antimagic.
- Morana counters Dominion's Power Word Kill.
- Alistaire's Meteor Swarm destroys Dominion.
- Dan's fate after antimagic suppression is unresolved in the excerpt.
