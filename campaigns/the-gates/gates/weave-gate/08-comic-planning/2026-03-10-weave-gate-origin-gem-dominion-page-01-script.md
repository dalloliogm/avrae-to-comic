# Weave Gate Origin Gem Dominion Comic Script: Page 1

## Page Goal

Open with enough setting context for a new reader to understand what "The Gates" are, then cut immediately into the climax. The reader should understand that Don is in impossible danger, magic has failed, and the solution is physical force rather than spellcasting. Do not explain the full mission yet; Page 2 handles the rewind.

## Layout

- Five panels.
- Panel 1 is a narrow establishing panel introducing The Gates.
- Panel 2 is a large horizontal cold-open splash.
- Panels 3 and 4 are two narrow close-ups beneath it.
- Panel 5 is a quiet hook caption panel.

## Panel 1

Narrow establishing panel. The Gates complex floats in a pocket dimension around an ancient mountain and cave. Several monumental magical Gates stand like arcane doors in the distance, each glowing with a different energy. They should feel old, defensive, and cosmic, not like ordinary city doors.

**Caption:** The Gates stand between the multiverse and everything trying to break through.

**Caption:** Today, Weave Gate nearly failed.

**Art Notes:** Keep this simple and iconic. The page only needs enough setting to orient the reader before the cold open.

**Prompt Note:** Rough comic establishing panel, ancient magical Gates in pocket dimension around mountain and cave, cosmic defensive portals, simple grayscale thumbnail with small magic accents.

## Panel 2

Half-page splash. Ancient forest ruins under storm-dark light. The ground is split by the boundary of an antimagic field: inside it, color is drained to gray and blue-white crackle; outside it, spell energy waits in vivid gold, blue, and violet.

Don Explodicus, old green orc wizard with white beard and conical hat, has no glow around him. His robes are torn and dusty. His Staff of Conflux lies several paces behind him on cracked stone. He has both hands locked into Dominion's black-and-red armor and is dragging the towering void entity across the boundary line by sheer force.

Dominion towers over him: black scaled armor, red cape, black staff raised, flaming skull head turned down in disbelief and rage. The skull-fire still burns inside the antimagic field, which should look wrong.

**Caption:** We built our defense on magic. Today, magic went silent.

**SFX:** KRRRKK

**Art Notes:** Don should not look triumphant yet. He should look strained, wounded, and stubborn. The panel must make the reader ask how he got here.

**Prompt Note:** Don Explodicus, green orc wizard with white beard and conical hat, dragging a towering black-and-red flaming-skull void tyrant out of a gray antimagic field in ancient ivy-covered ruins, staff abandoned behind him, cinematic fantasy comic splash panel, high action, dramatic lighting.

## Panel 3

Tight close-up on Dominion's flaming skull and upper armor. The skull turns sharply toward Don. The eye sockets are empty, but the expression should read as insulted disbelief. The black staff is visible at the edge of the panel, lifted to strike.

Dominion's telepathic speech balloon should be black with pale lettering, slightly breaking the panel border.

**Dominion:** Time to die, orc. Your magic is useless against me.

**Art Notes:** Let Dominion's speech feel invasive, like it is being heard inside the reader's head.

**Prompt Note:** Close-up of a flaming skull void lord in black and red armor, enraged and disbelieving, telepathic black speech balloon, fantasy comic art.

## Panel 4

Tight close-up on Don's hand gripping Dominion's armor. The hand is scarred and shaking. The armor plates bend slightly under the force. In the blurred background, Don's jaw is clenched hard enough to show tusks and teeth.

Use no spell glow. The absence of magic is the point.

No dialogue or caption in this panel.

**Art Notes:** This is the first hint that the grapple is emotionally loaded. Keep the burn-scar implication subtle; the fuller memory comes later on Page 7.

**Prompt Note:** Close-up of an old green orc wizard's scarred hand gripping black-and-red armor, no magic glow, strained fingers, cinematic comic detail.

## Panel 5

Small quiet panel. Nearly abstract: dead gray air inside the antimagic field, bright threads of the Weave fraying and stopping at the boundary. Don and Dominion are only silhouettes at the edge.

No dialogue, caption, or balloon in this panel.

**Art Notes:** This panel should slow the page down before the cut. Use two separate caption boxes so the second line lands as a hook into the rewind.

**Prompt Note:** Abstract fantasy comic panel showing bright magical threads stopping at the edge of a dead gray antimagic field, two silhouettes struggling at the boundary.

## Lettering Notes

- Dominion uses black speech balloons with pale lettering.
- Don has no dialogue on this page.
- Captions should be restrained and rectangular, preferably parchment or dull gold.
- The Page 1 title can be omitted; the cold open should carry the hook by itself.

## Continuity Checks

- Don's staff is on the ground before the grapple climax.
- Dominion is still dangerous and not yet defeated.
- The antimagic field suppresses the surrounding magic but does not suppress Dominion's own flames or aura.
- The page should not reveal Meteor Swarm, Morana's Counterspell, or the final outcome.
- The rewind begins on Page 2, not Page 1.
