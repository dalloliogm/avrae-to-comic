# Don Explodicus Comic Series Bible

Status: revised direction, 2026-06-05

Archived earlier direction: `don-explodicus-series-bible-first-pass-archive.md`

## Core Direction

This comic is the story of Don Explodicus, an orc wizard and cleric who enters The Gates carrying the weight of what the world thinks orcs are.

In public opinion, orcs are traditionally treated as evil creatures: violent, savage, allied with dark powers, and useful only as soldiers, raiders, or brute labor. Many people assume orcs have no meaningful culture of their own, only appetite, rage, and obedience to frightening gods. The same public that praises the Defenders for holding back the Evil Dimensions may also believe that orcs belong on the wrong side of that war.

Don is a direct contradiction of that prejudice. He is a scholar of arcane destruction, a worshipper of Gruumsh, a cleric, a wizard, and one of the most intelligent and wise Defenders ever to pass through the Gates by the end of his journey. He is not gentle or harmless. His magic burns, shatters, shields, commands, and resurrects momentum on the battlefield. But he is disciplined, perceptive, and purposeful. He represents an orc culture that outsiders have misunderstood, flattened, and feared.

When Don first arrives, the Gates do not receive him as a universally trusted hero. He arrives under a fragile political arrangement: a peace deal has been achieved with the Northern Orc tribes he belongs to, and he has been invited to represent his people in defense of the Gates. His presence is a test for everyone. Don must prove himself as a Defender. The Gates must prove whether their mission is truly larger than the prejudices of the world they protect.

## Adaptation Principle

The comic should be verbose by design. It should use captions, narration, and dialogue to explain the setting, the politics, Don's internal position, and what the Gates are. This is not a silent action montage. The story should feel like a fantasy comic with literary narration: clear, dramatic, and willing to tell the reader why the moment matters.

The missions remain the basis for action scenes, but the connective story is about identity, distrust, representation, and the burden of destructive power in service of defense.

## Don's Central Contradictions

- He is an orc in a world that expects orcs to be enemies.
- He worships Gruumsh, a god many outsiders consider proof of evil.
- He is a master of destruction who comes to protect reality from annihilation.
- He looks like the archetype people fear, but thinks and acts with exceptional intelligence, wisdom, and strategy.
- He represents a people treated as uncultured, while carrying religious, martial, scholarly, and diplomatic meaning at once.
- He is invited as part of a peace arrangement, but invitation is not the same as acceptance.

## Canon Anchors From Existing Files

- Don's first recorded mission is the Psychic Gate nymph battle, dated 2022-09-12 to 2022-09-19.
- In that first mission, Don introduces himself as an orc shaman/wizard of Gruumsh and Karaash, carrying a shield with Karaash's symbol and speaking in a rough common/orcish battle voice.
- Don has a deformed arm that cannot carry a weapon in the early logs, but the comic adaptation will not depict this because arm-side continuity is too fragile across generated panels.
- Don represents the Northern Orc tribes in the protection of the Gates, explicitly stated in the Whimsy Gate pumpkin mission.
- Don carries a painful childhood memory of visiting a human village for trick-or-treating, where humans thought his actual orc face was a frightening costume.
- Don's magic blends fire, thunder, abjuration, divine martial imagery, necromancy, support magic, and tactical encouragement.
- Don repeatedly empowers allies through Bless, Healing Word, Silvery Barbs, and Voice of Authority style openings.
- Don's signature recurring action is Fireball, confirmed 42 times in the indexed evidence.
- Dan Simulacrus is Don's icy simulacrum and should be visually distinct when he appears.

## New Adaptation Choices

These choices are now part of the intended comic direction unless contradicted by later source material.

- Don is invited to the Gates because of a peace deal with his Northern Orc tribes.
- The invitation is politically important, not merely personal recruitment.
- Many people at the Gates distrust him at first because he is an orc and a follower of Gruumsh.
- Don understands this distrust and chooses not to beg for acceptance. He intends to demonstrate value through service, intellect, and battlefield competence.
- Gruumsh is presented through Don's interpretation: a god of survival, endurance, war memory, and orc identity, not merely a villainous caricature.
- Don's arc is not "orc becomes civilized." His arc is "the world learns that its definition of civilization was incomplete."
- Don's destructive magic should be morally complex. The comic should never pretend explosions are harmless; instead, it should show how skill, restraint, and judgment determine whether destructive power is monstrous or protective.
- For visual continuity, always draw Don with two healthy arms and hands unless a later scene explicitly requires otherwise.

## Late-Story Frame: The Last Downtime

The following scene should be treated as a thematic compass for the entire series. It can appear as a prologue, epilogue, interlude, or issue-opening flash-forward.

### Scene Purpose

By the end of Don's journey, the old fear has not been erased, but it has been answered. A clerk who once saw a follower of Gruumsh as a threat to the shop now recognizes him as someone who has stood between the world and annihilation. The scarred holy symbol becomes visual evidence of years of service.

### Scene Beat

Two identical orc wizards appear inside Malkeen's Mercantile in matching flashes of arcane energy. The clerk groans in recognition: Don Explodicus and his simulacrum have returned.

One Don places a heavy iron amulet on the counter. Its Gruumsh symbol is scratched, dented, and covered in old repair marks from countless Gates. The other places down a large sack of gold.

Don asks for the usual simulacrum components. The clerk notices the holy symbol and admits that the first time he saw Don walk into the shop, he thought a follower of Gruumsh would blow up the entire shop.

Don concedes that many of his problems have ended with something exploding. The simulacrum adds: or on fire.

The clerk taps the scarred amulet and says that after all these Gates, Gruumsh followers can also be the ones standing between the world and annihilation.

Don answers that Gruumsh taught the orcs how to survive in a hostile world; people simply remember the wars more than why they were fought.

The clerk asks if a big mission is coming up. Both Dons are silent. Don answers: the last one.

### Visual Motifs From This Scene

- The scarred iron holy symbol.
- The shop clerk's changing expression from weary routine to respect.
- Don and Dan as mirror images: one fire-warm and living, one cold and artificial.
- The phrase "the last one" as an end-of-series shadow over the beginning.

## Opening Comic Structure

The first comic should begin before the first mission. It should not rush to the fight. It should establish the social and political stakes of Don's arrival, explain what the Gates are, and then move into the Psychic Gate mission.

### Page 1: The Road From The Orc Village

Purpose: introduce Don, his people, and the political burden of his journey.

Suggested layout: five to seven panels, with one large establishing panel.

#### Panel 1

Large establishing panel. Don travels alone or with a small escort along a mountain road or wild frontier path. Behind him, the Northern Orc village remains visible: smoke from hearths, hide and timber structures, watch posts, banners, elders and children watching him leave. The village should look like a real culture, not a barbarian camp: craft, family, ritual, tools, animals, and signs of memory.

Caption:  
Most people have never seen an orc village. They believe that orcs are savages, barbarians dedicated only to war, without history or culture.

#### Panel 2

Closer on Don's back as he walks away. His shield bears the sign of Karaash or Gruumsh. Spellbooks, scroll tubes, and component pouches mark him as a wizard. Both arms and hands should look healthy for visual continuity.

Caption:  
But what you know about Orcs, comes from the tales told by the victors.

#### Panel 3

Cut to a formal blessing. Don kneels low before an older orc elder or priest, almost prostrate, head bowed and hands placed respectfully before him. The elder stands over him in blessing while only a few villagers watch. No melodramatic farewell is needed; the moment should feel formal and restrained.

Caption:  
People remember the wars, more than why they were fought.

#### Panel 4

Don pauses and looks back at the village. His face is determined, intelligent, and guarded. He is not leaving as an exile; he is leaving as a representative.

Dialogue, elder or off-panel voice:  
"May the One-Eye be proud of you."

Don:  
"Then I will give him reason to be."

#### Panel 5

A symbolic panel: Don's iron holy symbol in his hand, scratched but not yet as scarred as it will become by the final downtime scene.

Caption:  
The Gates have invited an orc of Gruumsh to defend the world from evil.

#### Panel 6

Long shot of Don continuing toward the impossible entrance route to the Gates. The village is now small behind him.

Caption:  
The world will decide whether that is a contradiction, or a promise.

### Page 2: What The Gates Are

Purpose: explain the setting and why Don's invitation matters.

Suggested layout: six to eight panels, with heavy narration.

#### Panel 1

The Gates complex in the pocket dimension: mountain, marble-and-iron Main Gate, lake, forests, barracks, and administrative structures. It should feel old, enormous, and inhabited.

Caption:  
The Gates are not a castle, though armies live there. They are not a temple, though souls have been sacrificed to make them. They are not a nation, though kings send coin and soldiers beg for admission.

#### Panel 2

Historical image of Itrendor discovering the cave or escaping from the font of evil. This can be rendered like a painted historical inset.

Caption:  
Centuries ago, Itrendor found a wound in reality: a path to dimensions where evil gathered like pressure behind a dam.

#### Panel 3

Itrendor and Malkeen warning leaders who dismiss them. Strange creatures and dark incursions appear at the panel edges.

Caption:  
He warned the world. The world called him mad until the horrors began arriving.

#### Panel 4

The creation of the Gates: life magic, mountain and cave pulled into a pocket dimension, Itrendor's sacrifice.

Caption:  
Itrendor gave his life, soul, and power to create the Gates. They did not end evil. They held it back.

#### Panel 5

Defenders from many cultures pass through the Hall of Arrivals. Some glance suspiciously at Don as he arrives or is announced.

Caption:  
Since then, Defenders had come from kingdoms, temples, orders, ships, tribes, and ruins. The Gates accepted strength where they could find it.

#### Panel 6

A formal or semi-formal moment: a Gate official, recruiter, or administrator receives Don's invitation papers or peace-deal token. Don stands composed under scrutiny.

Caption:  
This time, strength comes under the terms of a fragile peace. The Northern Orc tribes send one of their own.

Dialogue, official or clerk:  
"Don Explodicus. Representative of the Northern tribes. Wizard. Cleric. Follower of Gruumsh."

Small whispered dialogue from an onlooker:  
"Gruumsh?"

#### Panel 7

Close on Don hearing the whisper but not reacting.

Caption:  
Invitation opens the door. It does not quiet the room.

Don, quietly:  
"I heard."

### Page 3: First Summons

Purpose: show Don preparing, meeting companions, and entering the first mission's battle.

Suggested layout: seven to nine panels, moving from quiet preparation to combat reveal.

#### Panel 1

Don in a sparse assigned room, barracks corner, or preparation alcove. He arranges his spellbook, holy symbol, shield, and component pouch. The mood is controlled, almost ritualistic.

Caption:  
He prepares as a scholar first.

#### Panel 2

Close on spell notation, divine markings, and destructive components. Fireball ingredients can appear visually, but this is before Fireball becomes his signature.

Caption:  
Arcane formulae. Sacred names. Wards. Fire. Thunder. Commands that turn fear into motion.

#### Panel 3

Don attaches or touches the shield symbol of Karaash/Gruumsh.

Caption:  
To outsiders, Gruumsh means conquest. To Don, Gruumsh also means survival in a world that has never been gentle to orcs.

#### Panel 4

The summons arrives as a mental message, echoing the Psychic Gate raw log. Don's eyes sharpen. The room falls away into the impression of a Sending-like call.

Caption:  
His first call does not come as a trumpet, a letter, or a hand on the shoulder. It arrives inside his skull.

#### Panel 5

Don reaches the small door near the courtyard and sees the overexcited Psychic Gatekeeper. Other first companions are present or arriving: Sigmund, Ufgar, Forge, and others from the log as appropriate.

Gatekeeper:  
"Hi! You're the new peeps, right? Oh, this will be so exciting, follow me!"

Caption:  
The first lesson of the Gates is that apocalypse can be administered by someone with very little sense of occasion.

#### Panel 6

Winding corridor. Companions react to Don's appearance, holy symbol, and confidence. Keep the distrust subtle: glances, silence, a hand tightening on a weapon, someone trying not to stare.

Don:  
"You may stare after we survive. Until then, eyes forward."

#### Panel 7

The Psychic Gate chamber opens: massive stone gate, portal, the Gatekeeper explaining nymphs and a ritual. Don listens rather than interrupting, showing intelligence beneath the theatrical surface.

Gatekeeper:  
"A few nymphs gathered near one of the gates. Ritual, chanting, dancing around. Best to stop that before it is already too late!"

#### Panel 8

Portal reveal into the clearing: fire nymph, water nymphs, shadow nymph, other gates, chains crumbling around the fifth gate.

Caption:  
The second lesson is simpler.

#### Panel 9

Don steps forward with shield raised. Red divine light begins to gather around him, foreshadowing Bless. Companions are in silhouette around him; enemies face them.

Caption:  
The Gates do not ask whether the world trusts him. They ask whether he will stand.

Don:  
"Hold the line. I will bring the fire."

## Dialogue And Caption Style

- Use captions generously to explain social context, lore, and emotional stakes.
- Don's early dialect can remain, but should be controlled and readable. It should communicate voice without making him seem stupid. His captions, internal logic, and tactical actions should make his intelligence obvious.
- Don can speak more formally in reflective or late-story scenes, especially by the time of the Malkeen's Mercantile flash-forward.
- Other characters should reveal prejudice through hesitation, assumptions, and nervous joking more often than explicit speeches.

## Visual Continuity Notes

- In the opening pages, Don should look like a young orc wizard at the beginning of his story, not the older white-bearded veteran from later portraits.
- Don's holy symbol should begin relatively intact and accumulate scratches, dents, repairs, and scorch marks across the series.
- His spellbook and component pouch should be visible from the beginning to mark him as an intellectual and arcane practitioner.
- Draw Don with two healthy arms and hands throughout the comic for visual continuity, despite the early-log deformity note.
- The orc village must show culture: tools, family, religion, craft, architecture, food, banners, elders, and children.
- The Gates complex must feel like a place that believes in its mission but is still staffed by fallible people with prejudices.

## Later Series Arc

The end-state of the series should make the Malkeen's Mercantile scene feel earned. The clerk's admission matters only if the reader has seen years of suspicion, survival, explosions, healing, mistakes, and loyalty.

Don does not become acceptable because he stops being orcish or stops worshipping Gruumsh. He becomes undeniable because he remains himself while repeatedly choosing to stand between reality and annihilation.
